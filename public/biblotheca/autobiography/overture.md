# Overture: a prologue to our life's work
In classical music, the Overture is the beginning piece of music. It's almost... story-like. Infact, most of our work doesn't make sense until we tell stories.

It's the triad: scientist, artist, and botanist.

- The scientist want's the analyze and understand every granular detail. The scientst, lives at the summit of **order and control.**
- The artist wants to explore freely, while... The artist, lives at the edge of **chaos**
- The botanist (gardener) simply provides an enviornment for greens to live and grow. The gardener, maintains **equillibrium** between extremes by tending.

**50% of our work is ordered theory, 50% of our work is  emotional, chaotic, messy.** 

This is the fundamental theory of chaos and order. 

I don't know where this belongs... but it's essentially an introduction to all of our work. 


- The paradox of the human experience is that in order to live fully, you need to understand
- But in order to understand intellectually, you have to live.

It's a paradox. You cannot have one without the other. It's a positive feedback.

I don't know how to articulate this... but I think it explains all of our life's work.



Emotion, chaotic relationships, is what gives life it's meaning. Structued theory, intellectual study is what gives life it's liveability. I think the answer between opposing forces, **is always equilibrium... balance.**
