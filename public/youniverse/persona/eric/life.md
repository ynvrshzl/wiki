# What saved Eric's life
_"I always hated people. I hated being thrown in this world. I never asked to be here... it's been nothing but pain... But then I found the Gang... they... they found something for me."_

Eric was about to end his life. He was days away from following the last plan he had: **burn himself alive.** In his eyes, what little remained of them, he had no reason to exist. His life made no difference. Nobody would miss him if he was gone. There was no joy in his life, only the dull, depression, that made his bed feel like a graveyard.

Eric was burned in an accident. He was left disfigured. People wouldn't get near him. Everyone was afraid of him. _"But I just want to be like you..."_

He saw the burn accident as a cruel joke. "If God exists, you  must really love seeing people suffer. Are we just playthings..." These nihilistic thoughts started infesting Eric's mind. Eventually, he became so isolated and weak... [??]

But a miracle happened, the prettiest girl, approached him one day during the school lunch. She asked him: _"Hey, why do you always eat alone?"_ Eric's heart was so broken he could no longer feel anything, but as soon as he heard her sweet, soft voice, his heart began galloping through his chest, a feeling he had long forgotten. Slowly, he looked to her, expecting her face to turn green, as everyone found him to be disgusting. She looked him deep into his eyes and smiled. He was shocked out of depression. Almost like a spark between them. Her eyes, shimmering with green, in the bright sunny day. 

For once in his life, Eric was terrifed of dying. No words could be muttered... he sat in silence, barely able to look into her eyes. He thinks to himself: *"She... is so... pretty... why... why is she talking to me... what does she want from me?"*

He had something to lose, someone precious. Now, his life had weight. He was worth something to someone.

This is where Eric's infesting thoughts are re-arranged into something more tragic, but meaningful. *"Life is full of pain. Life is messy. Accidents happen. If you have nothing to lose, you have nothing to live for."*
