# 1 
__"Life is fundamentally pain."__

The Ethos _(source)_ of Ethism is the belief that life is fundamentally pain. 

Pain is source data. Non-negotiable. It is as much of part of this life as the rest of the human spectrum. _[Editor: This probably sounds too menacing, perhaps we could shift the tone to simply observing pain, not justifying. A happy puppy doesn't need to justify happiness.]_

Imagine a wise grandmother taps your shoulder and wispers something in your ear, that you've quietly understood your entire life. "Life is pain." And then she leaves. Seriously granny?! You're just gonna drop a truth bomb like that and walk out? The belief that life is fundamentally pain, is an ancient human belief. But suffering, cruelty, injustice and evil, __are not necessary components of life.__
