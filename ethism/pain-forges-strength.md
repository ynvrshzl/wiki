# Pain forges strength
So perhaps the pain does shape us, the way molten lava shapes steel. But that does not imply  "life is suffering, therefore, we suffer to grow wiser or stronger." That's naive. A nihilistic perspective could simply argue "if life is so painful, why continue when we will all die anyway?".

Life is pain. Life _can_ be cruel, unjust and brutal. That does not define the whole of life. Life isn't cruel. Life is pain. People can be cruel. But people can also be self-sacrificial beyond understanding, for someone they love.

## Sharpening the steel of the ego
Let's think of the human Ego as less of a spiritual concept, and more of a physical model. If we percieved life absolutely, we would process information that would overload our senses. Computers are evidence of this. If we processed life entirely through our emotions and instincts, we would be... you guessed it... animals. But it appears that our human abilities, and whatever consciousness can be defined as... gives shape to this life.

Thus, if we have a model for how we experience life as human beings, we can now understand it as an engine, rather than "it's just life." No. No it's not "just life." There must be something I can do. [That should be the attitude of an Aethist or Ethist.](#) (Coined the term!)

If the ego is like the experience of life, messy, beautiful, meaningful, difficult... then what gives shape to the ego?

The ego. It is essentially a cliche at this point. That's unfortunate, becuase the human ego is less of a "prideful" component, and more of a engine of equilibrium for chaos and order.

The ego must be modelled. Through social upbrinding. Through morals. These are the constructs which sustain human development towards a positive vector. Without health, we are essentially agens of chaos and destruction. But it's not existential, as our life's work doesn't peer into the world outside, [we look at the youniverse within.](#) Study the self. Become better.
