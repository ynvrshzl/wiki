# Overcoming analysis paralysis

Whenever i'm overwhelmed, I treat everything like a jenga block tower, I can either carefully continue, or let it all fall apart & start from the very simplest of things, zero.

But that's what I do, what can *you* do to overcome analysis paralysis? Much like all of my work, I can't give specific advice becuase I don't know everything, but I can share the models that can adapt to your life.

Here, the model is the static-dynamic theory. Imagine a computer operating system.

The longer you stare at something, the more "mistakes" you see. Sometimes this is good for details that need to be correct, other times, more problems are created.

This is a powerful lens for ADHD/autism decision paralysis. If you separate what is non-negotiable static truth (scaffolding) from what is optional dynamic expression (visuals, customizations, workflows), then you can focus your energy only on what actually matters for the goal at hand.
