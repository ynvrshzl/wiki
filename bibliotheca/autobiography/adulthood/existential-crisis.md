# Existential crisis
> [!warning] WARNING:
> This is the scariest section in my life story, do not read this unless you are willing to expose yourself to dangerous, life-threatening ideas. Seriously, if you do not want to risk losing your sanity, I urge to not read this section. 

Now with that warning, if youre somehow *still* reading this, then I guess some part of you is curious to know what exactly I have discovered in my short but sweet 23 years of existence... I'll tell you that in a short moment, but first, how did we get here?

# 

I believe I will never be able to capture the totality of life, in the depth of both suffering & happiness. I think that's a good thing. I'm not trying to simulate life, I'm trying to heal by making beautiful works of art.

# Finding an answer
One answer which has saved my life is perhaps God allow suffering the same way an author would allow suffering in their story it's not that you enjoy seeing people in pain but rather it adds depth and contrast which somehow creates the experience of existence itself it is a push and pull a magnetic talk of war between light and dark. suffering and pain is as horrible as it needs to be because healing and the possibility of Hope are worth dying for.

# Cosmic analysis
"Nature is the teacher. The mother, the father, the sage, and the student. Observe from life, and you will learn the answers to all your questions." 

I finally had a name for this feeling — I wasn't afraid that we were living in an empty universe, I was afraid of my own death. What would it be like? When would it happen? What would happne to the dreams I had? Would my consciousness fade away? These questions kept me up at night, *literally.* I chose to isolate myself to study my inner world, and it was terrifying. I discovered things that I may never be able to put into words. But more importantly, my greatest fear wasn't death itself — it was having died with regrets, and never having experienced the dreams that kept me alive.

# What is true?
We can never know what is true for certain. Atleast, not in our tangled, human psychology. Perhaps, with machines, we could experience other senses, and even find cures to our conditions. It's a pipe dream, but it keeps my cogs going.

I've become apathetic, after getting my throat caressed by the sweet poison of cynicism. I just don't care anymore, but in a good way.

Something I learned today is if you spend too much time in one emotion, it is like only seeing either black, or white. We have to experience life from both sides, the bigger picture, in order for us to understand what is true.

Just like a true scientist would, you don't want biased information, you want the real thing. And the real thing can only be found outside of your own little world. You have to find the real thing out there. And that can be scary for some of us, and life-threatening for few of us.
