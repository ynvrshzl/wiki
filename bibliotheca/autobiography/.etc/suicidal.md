# "The tragedy is not death, the tragedy is the unlived life."

# Surviving suicide
I have attempted to end my life. I am alive. 

After the terrible things I've experienced, I am no longer afraid of death, nor ending my own life, the only thing in this world that terrifies me is an unlived life.

I'd say, death anxiety is a complete 180 from wanting to die, but it's a whole hell of a lot better, than rotting your bed.

And this is such a complex part of human nature, it cuts right to the bone of who and what we are. All it takes is one thought, one person, one song, anything, to keep you living. But it takes multiple thoughts, multiple persons, to make you want to tide. Light is stronger than the darkness, that's why all you need is one thiing.

It is not wishful thinking, it is real, it exists, but it only exists inside us.


# The story
I have felt like ending my life ever since, high school in 9th grade. I couldn't take the social anxiety, and being so confused in my body. I was tired of wearing the mask, and my ego was crumbling, when I realized people were better at wearing their masks than me. And so I said, okay, I can't cope with this anymore, I'm just done. **It's not giving up, it's letting go.**

There were many times in my life, and what seems like, almost everyday, I have a thought about ending my own life. The reason why I feel this way, is the most important, and is the first question you should ever ask someone who tells you they are suicidal. Ask them: "Why?" Becuase if you don't ask the person the right questions while they're still here, you're going to regret it for the rest of your life. So don't do that. Make sure to listen to the person, they are just like you, a human being, in this confusing existence, trying to find their way back to a life worth living.

Death, would not be like sleep, if I were to stop everything today. I have alot of things that I want to do, and see, and experience, and become. It's those things that are keeping me alive. 

For some reason, I seem to have multiple personalities living in my head. I used to believe that they were the cause of me 'selling my soul to satan.' One fabled night, my younger self decided it was a good idea to summon the entity in my room, and after alot of incantations, nothing seemed to happen. However, the coming years after I decided to do that, were plauged with mental illness. I repented & asked God for forgiveness, but the voices never went away, they only became louder. And so, I took charge, and decided to create something good out of this.

I imagine a gun, everytime there is an uncomfortable memory or imagination in my head.

Recently, I've started talking to myself in my own head. I imagine that I'm shouting into the Abyss, where all the negative imaginations come from, and I tell them: "You will not kill me! I will live a long, beautiful, healthy fulfulling life!" This is such a powerful exercise for me.

# The truth behind the veil
There seemed to be something missing in my life: emotion, meaning & feeling alive. I realized recently, that the only way I can appreciate actually being here, is by remembering there was a time I wanted to kill myself. Only in death, can I appreciate my life. It's a terrifying paradox... almost too dangerous, to be considering these thoughts, flooding me, but it's the only way I can grasp the fact that I'm alive & that's somehow beautiful. Wierdly chaotic.

# What saved my life
What saved my life from jumping infront of a train is, well, a mix of things. But primarily, it's having a reason to actually want to be here. For me, it's pretty simple: **be a beautiful girl, and share my gifts with people in need.**

I almost killed myself realizing that I am powerless in this world. I cannot change the wars crushing the children & their families. I cannot help the animals in the factories being slaughtered. I can't change human nature. But what I can change is the only thing in this world that I can change: my perspective. Perspective is powerful, but it's not perfect. It only works if you live in a priveledged space, where you can close your eyes & your ears, and silently ignore the pain of this world. And I said to myself: you don't have to carry the weight of this world. No single human being will ever solve the problems of this world, but we can put our energy to things that will make the most change in our short time here. So I realized, I don't have to think about these things I cannot control. Yes. I'm ignoring the terrible things that happen everyday. I can't control them. And If it kills me to keep thinking about them, then I don't even have to think about them.

# 
It seems like people on the outside can not understand what you're going thorugh, and yet, we need people more than ever, becuase when we stop functioning on ourselves, all it takes is another person to be a beacon of light. 

In your more hopeless of days, you get to see who truly matters to you. That is something no words can take away.

#

I am writing this on August 8, 2025. I am exhausted. I really just want to dissapear forever. I am tired of the problems that just keep getting worse. I hate this world and I hate pain. But I realize, even death wouldn't console me. I'm lucky. That I even have this choice, between living or dying. As soon as you put it into words, it loses it's essence, that's why I love to represent things in abstract ways, not the intense logic of human language.

Why keep living, when you're body has given up on you? Why keep fighting when you've got nothing left in you? A future worth living for? A past unsolved, that you could heal from? Why fix your life? Why avoid your death, if you will die anyway, and in this universe, where there seems to be emptiness, rather than a designer with answers. Why? I can't tell you why you should keep living, and sometimes — you can't save everyone. 

Bad things happen. We all die in the end. Suicide is not the tragedy, the tragedy is the unlived life.

Tragedy is a brutal theif that steals everything & leaves only scars behind. There is an evil so powerful in this world, 

But I asked myself, I'm lucky. Who's to say, all the people that wish they had my life, with my impossible problems & my unique biology. I've been blessed & cursed, and my perspective/attitude is the only thing that can save me from myself. Beyond that, I realize i'm lucky. I can't give up on myself, not yet. And if nature takes me before my time, atleast I died giving it all i've got, and to me, that satifies my soul, more than going to sleep earlier than I should.

So I accept, I accept the suffering. The cruel evil, the things I can't change, the things I hate. I accept that it's part of this life. But I only accept it, knowing that this life, contains mystery, beyond the walls of psychological torment. I live everyday now, to recover my health, and seek that horizon of euphoric healing. I want to transcend, and heal, and revivify the broken human within me.



# My meaning

I want to help people stop committing suicide. I can't solve the world's problems, and I can't even solve the problem of auto-death, but I can share my stories & be a beacon of hope, in a work that is becoming increasingly more isolating & hopeless.
