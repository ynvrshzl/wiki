## Solving the problems of existence
I believe that there will always exist problems, issues & glitches. That's okay. That shouldn't stop the entire system to continuing to exist & evolve, just like a human being. Computers have "fault tolerance," which is a concept that allows problems to exist, while maintaining the system alive. If machines didn't have fault tolerance, we wouldn't have technology, [which has improved the life of this planet, more than it has damaged it.](is-technology-worsening-or-bettering-life.md)

### Technology can cure suffering
One of the most challenging problems in software, productivity, or even living human life itself: **is getting lost in the sphagetti & forgetting why you started.** It's the classic zoom-in/zoom-out problem, but while you're in it, you can't remember, it's almost as if your world shrinks to the size of these problems, until all that exists are these problems. They begin to seem like the only real things. The answer must be to zoom-out. So you must find something worth zooming out for.

 conventional jobs easily avoid this becuase there is a rigid framework (bosses, clear objectives) but a self-employed polymath with tons of sprawling interests? that takes far more management & control to harness sporadic interest as the core strength and not the ultimate burden. this is infact why i'm happy i live in a world where i can create my own tools (that's why i am teaching myself programming) you can use the power of machines to extend your human will beyond what your capacity limits you. but this initself is another rabbit hole: what capacities are you going to make the most of? your original issue of managing your interests is one of the core reasons to have a personl system, but it often gets lost within the noise of: "new tools! boring theme, let's switch. new button!" those little things often make me forget why i even have a tool to begin withh. machines/tools are the interface between the things i can't do, and the things i want to do. and a good tool witll abstract away input so that you only do what's necessary and the tool does most of the heavy lifting. in programming, this is abstraction, which is essentially the purpose of programming -> to create programs & instructions for the machine -> which the machine itself is a tool of abstract layers that human beings can learn to use to solve the most important problems we face. that is the purpose of technology in my eyes. 

to solve the deepest problems, i believe technology + physical action + introspection is the answer to suffering, evil, crime, violence, disease, illness & all the terrible things that make life not worth living, technology is stepping stone, the missing piece is the human's will guiding all of that like the flow of water through gates of rivers.

# It's okay to be alone. It's okay to be alive.
I feel so guilty for being happy, knowing that I am not moving a finger to help the animals in the slaughter farms, or the children in war, the families dying, the injustice, and the cruelty of being one of us, a human.

I understand that, I do not belong in social groups. I feel drained: emotionally & physically. I end up spending hours thinking about things people told me, or things I could have said... if only I had been given more time to think. People seem to be great with words. They seem to know what to say & don't need time to think of a response. Analyzing our human nature made me realize: it's okay for me to be alone. Even if there are people like me out there, when I needed someone the most, all I had was myself, and that showed me I am not only enough on my own, but I could live an entire life without anyone.

# The place I always come back to
The place I always come back to, when living is just too fucking much for my brain, I remember the teachings of Dawn Zen. How everything in reality is like a magnet... it is not evil nor good, but rather positive and negative ends meeting each other.

Recently, I'm going about my day, doing my human being things, and then I'm suddently aware that i'm not in pain,I don't want to die, and my health is actually improving. Sometimes it's hard to feel anything but resentment for even being alive. When you're going through pain, it can feel like you're being punished twice: once for the pain itself, and twice for being forced to live to endure it. This is even worse with emotional pain, becuase sometimes, your body won't physically show how sick you really are on the inside.

I can still get resentful, and hate that I was brought to live... sometimes the good things in life aren't enough to make me want to live... and especially during those extreme painful moments, but that's my point... i'm grateful that life isn't those extreme moments... it's a messy plate of everything.
