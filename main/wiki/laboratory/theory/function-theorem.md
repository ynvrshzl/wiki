**Abstract:** we only understand what is important, when we first experienced what is not.

In a more tragic lens, we don't know how important, seemingly mundane things are (like socializing, being around other human beings, having a window in your room, drinking clean water) untill you've experienced, <u>for yourself</u> what it's like to not have those things.

I was realizing this as I opened the windows in my room. I've had this same room for over 15% of my life. I haven't payed attention to how important it is to actually have a window.

## The mission is not to save, but to send.
It's tragic... yes. To realize that someone has to go through suffering, in order to understand what it most important in human life. That's why I believe the purpose is not to extract knowledge, but rather, to be a broadcast signal. A radar. Not to save anyone. Just to give. To send signal. That someone was here. Someone walked the earth inside your mind. This terrain has already been mapped. You do not need to suffer. **This is probably the beginning of ethical knowledge.**
