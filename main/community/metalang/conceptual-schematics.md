## Case 0.1 - "at what point does programming become architectural?"
**Abstract:** Programming, fundamentally becomes architectural, once we are dealing with data flow. In essence, the architectural aspect of programming is our conceptual model of working with computer systems. A script is no different than a password authentication module, in that they function predictably (procedure). The architecture emerges when we have to humanly track data flow, which naturally emerges into structure, across a virtual system, with no visual guidance of where the data is flowing. The only adequate tooling we have as human beings, is our actual conceptual models and schematics of data flow. 

**Hypothesis:** and i think... systems require structure... structure requires systems... and they both require architecture... which is the integration of system and structure... organic and constructed. 

### Living things require systems 
**Practically applied:** In essence, this is important... becuase once you're building a system, if you approach it like "coding" or "programming scripts", the system becomes volatile and unmanageable. This most-likely explains why alot of projects get abandoned, even if they started with really a clear, good vision. The original spark of idae, could be considered momentum, in the physics of force. But momentum without sustain, becomes exhaustive energy, and that is exactly what happens with an idea that does not have systems or structure to keep it alive.
