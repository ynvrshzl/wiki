## Definition
Defining the problem. 

In the simplest of terms; we are balancing the weight between precision and expression. Between power and consumption. The engine of code is not the runtime; it is the human imagination.

- It is not precisely about mathematical correctness (i.e., programming is not trying to provide empirical proof about a system)
- Code is arguably designed for product first, inspection last. (we produce output before designing programs...)

## Tacit information as the driver of human-cognitive manageability

With tacit vs. explicity information; i hypothesize that we physically, cognitively cannot manage  details, rules and relationships of a system, without ^shifting the baseline of abstraction. This is illustrated in the classical example of Assembly vs Python;



- Assembly: high level surface area of information mass > little to no levels of information density

- Python: high levels of information density < small surface area of information 



Assembly has maximum control and precision, and Python has maximum expressiveness. This is understood mechanically; but phenomenologically, the story is far beyond language semantics; and how we human beings truly process ideas, systems, and product.
