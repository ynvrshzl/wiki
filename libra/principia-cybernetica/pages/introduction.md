# "Principia Cybernetica" Laws of Computation and Systems

## 1.0 - Abstract
What is computation?

Let's explore this research, especially with the phenomena of "artificial intelligence" recently...

...I propose the main concept of "domains of human-computer interface". Essentially, at the lowest level, it's not even about computers, or rules, or instructions; it's as low as lambda calculus; it's not even about numbers, or math or "what is computation?" it's closer to "what is truth?" eventually, this becomes the engineer's life task "how can we build a structure, that is reliable enough to handle increasing levels of sophistication and complexity, not as a result of engineering itself (becuase this creates a dangerous closed feedback loop) but as a result of meeting demands of the external world, and ultimately, solving problems?" That's the highest level of engineering; computers seem to be one interface of many engineering mediums.

### 1.1 - Between Hardware and Software
Within computers, we reach a level where the hardware becomes malleable... "soft" in other words "soft-ware" and the etymology of the word "ware" is integral to understanding not only what computers are ontologically; but how we can responsibly use them to solve the hardest problems in nature. I call this ["Cyberethos"](cyber-ethos.md) 

Back to the proposed concept of "domains" a key example of domains is web development. When I began my coding journey, I had no idea where to start. Research made things more overwhelming and messy; like trying to learn chemistry by mixing elements rather than reading books or studying real-world uses of chemistry.

In that case; I learned programming not by "coding", but by; reverse-engineering. I think that's single-handedly the most powerful approach for learning anything (my personal opinion)... becuase when I experienced what code was really useful for, all of a sudden; 
- The massive gap between my language abilities
- And the infinite world of code
- Became walkable; and tools like Obsidian, Blender and The browser, were the bridges between these distant worlds.

It may seem strange, but instead of "just coding", I ended up reading more about code. Instead of "watching 23 videos" I reverse-engineered my favorite code projects. Eventually, I built a vocabulary set that allowed me to "think" and "express" in code. That's when I realized; this is not just typing rules into a text file... code is the ability to encode systems represented as lexica... In essence, this section of Principia Cybernetica focused on the Phenomenology of learning (as applied to code, which is virtually infinite as an ability and medium, so understanding how we human beings properly learn, is pretty important);

- We benefit from reading books, rather than reading maps
- We explore nonfinite maps, through finite tunnels of focus
- We prefer to tour or walk things becuase of continuity, rather than explore fragments of ideas and features.

Those points are explored in a separate body of work (citations coming soon.)

Anyways, "Code as lexica"... is my current definition of source code... computation is beyond source text... computers and computation are proto-medium... they are conceptual... closer to plato's theory of forms and ontological devices, rather than strict systems... (though we should study that in a separate chapter, "Ontology of Computation.")

The strict systems are created by necessity; just as I did not have the capacity to learn code by being exposed to it's unfiltered endless state, the systems we interact with must also contain a necessary level of bias; that bias is the responsibility of the engineer to design properly.
