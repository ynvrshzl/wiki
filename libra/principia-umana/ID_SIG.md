> Core idea 
> 
> "it is precisely the most difficult days we've lived in our life, that define who we are."

we choose to respond on the days we don't want to get up, that precisely challenges our biology; what are you, human, when the body is not in your control? that leaves us with the only logical conclusion, this is exactly when we practice integrated agency; but when we are comfortable, not when we feel like it, but when we decide. autonomously. like pleasing heart fire shapes the steel of a tool, sometimes the most difficult days in human life, shape the autonomous human agency. 

The hardest part is understanding _"[The Continuity Principle"](../CONTINUITY.md)_ 


if identity is a core source of human  psyche orientation in the natural world, then it must be the difficult days that provides us with the only real source of friction, in order to define structure. to contrast what is comfortable. 

this does not change, how phenomenological painful parts of a human experience can be, and much of it is tacit; never spoken but understood.
