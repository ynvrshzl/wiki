# Why we schedule things
The human brain cannot compute "importance" in the moment; there is little to no distinction bettween potential and actuality; what is worth working on; what demands our finite resources.

## 0.0 - Classification

We cannot approach this issue from a purely mechanical lens; we are not machines. We require leisure and equillibrium across domains in order to properly function; and as a byproduct; productivity economics.

## 1.0 - Hypothesis 
The main hypothesis is this; we do not plan ahead (schedule) simply to allocate future resources, but to mechanically distinguish between what is important, what seems important, and what should not be allowed to ... our resources.
