## lived trials
for my own personal lift trials / experiments, I wake up and I'm already exhausted. the spark of motivation is that which ignites the engine, so to speak. but when motivation is not present, the human being has to start their own fire... and such a thing can only happen from reaction... the friction contact collapse of flint and stone, of rocks, spark...

... I honestly feel like punching a wall sometimes, when I'm very irritable and frustrated. however, can this pain be expressed differently? and what if this pain itself can shift forms of matter? what if instead of anger and frustration, it is a recalibration of the identity, of the psyche? 

I rehearse this when I am about to make a decision I would regret; "A pretty lady does not behave this way." this prompt behaves as a buffer, between the sensory state of irritation, and the eventual mechanical autonomous process, which would cause damage. so instead of the frustration becoming fuel for the movement of joints, frustration becomes a recalibration of the psyche. 

it is important to emphasize this is not a perfect solution, but I have never acted out to my frustration and anger, onto myself or to my environment, partly thanks to these recalibration programs. so they are significant, even if just at the locality of my own individual self. this could reveal an even more significant process for psychological rehabilitation and autotherapy.
