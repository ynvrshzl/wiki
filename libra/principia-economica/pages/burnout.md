## Classifying burnout 
burnout is an improper name for the phenomenological equivalent... it feels more like an engine running on oil rather than fuel... 

burnout is caused by many things, but burn out of overwhelm is a loss of orientation. the human body is finite and limited, but imagination is not. cognition outpaces autonomy.

well, from personal study, I have realized that burnout is one of the few cognitive states where both the brain and the body is affected; physical and cognitive exhaustion. that can be absolutely brutal for someone trying to focus, to be productive... and the major emphasis, perhaps the very primary emphasis of this discussion, is that I truly believe burnout is not entirely out of our control, it is not entirely physical, I believe burnout is a loss of orientation but not a loss of human agency; it is not clinically debilitating (although the phenomenology of burnout is similar to very real debilitating cognitive deficiencies and disorders, mostly due to the extreme brain fog and physical exhaustion. so this is the phenomenology of burnout.)

but I truly believe that burnout is not a loss of agency; we have the ability to change how we feel, even if we cannot change how we focus, or how awake we feel, or how healthy the body is. 

I know this because when I visually / auditorily switch my environment, either in the computer or in the physical room, my cognitive and autonomous abilities follow the environment...
... however I want to emphasize I am not to merely saying "if you go for a walk, you won't be burnt out." 
... and in fact this is one of the most difficult parts of burnout phenomenology; amnesia-like symptoms. when brain fog accumulates to debilitating levels, it can feel almost impossible to think clearly, to act, to orient, to make sense of the world around us. this obviously makes any kind of cognitive work impossible...
