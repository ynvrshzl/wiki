# "Schematica" a toolkit enviornment for software engineering

In architecture and engineering,  What are tools used to design and organize structures that are more complex than we can physically or cognitively manage? 

Not paradigms or concepts &mdash; **Tools that are physicall and cognitively \*required** to work in these complex fields.

Our capacity for planning and testing, working in symbolic space, and studying, exceeds our capacity for real-time working memory and capabilities. When viewed from this lens, tools can be deifined as;

> Mechanical processes, interfaces and extensions that guide our finite efforts to the most beneficial search-space.__



What's proposterous to me to me is that; we have software to design CPUs and Computers; the stuff that runs on programming languages! And yet; we do not software that helps us build software. As a result, we write code, and aslong as it "just executes" most of the time; nobody demands further inspection "hey, stop and look at the structures and systems emerging here, perhaps this makes sense today, but in weeks could you understand what you were doing?"



In this sense code is not merely "channeling my human imagination directly to lexical breadcrumbs" it is closer to the functional purpose of language itself; "snapshots of time and state, compressed into space using symbolic lexica."



What this means is;

- Just becuase code works today, and outputs correct machine behavior



I'm designing tools to close those gaps; between what we write and how we manage it. That is most likely why architecture relies on schematics and blueprints and calculus; not for extreme mathematical precision; but for structural-integrity when concept meets the pressures and states of the natural world. I see the software engineer, as a real engineer; and perhaps when we have the tools to visualize our code as architectural systems; we may finally understand the structural-integrity as a foundational principle, above rushed shipping & deployment of "code that just works" but leaves engineers "cognitively depleted, physically exhausted, emotionally burnt out, and physiologically drained." This is most likely a result of poor tooling; giving developers text editors when what they need is battle-tested industry grade tools, like in other fields.



It's time we make a change!
