Abstract, this explains why moving forward is more important than making the best decision. However, stepping away from non-linear systems, is the greatest act of problem solving. ["We must remember we are not machine, we have an organic autonomy, with a difference engine, but also a beating heart, and a hungry belly. The human being, is a creature of polar paradoxes that somehow, co-exist. It is our duty, to maintain the equillibrium."](human-nature.md)

Human beings cannot operate in both: non-linearity and unpredictability. 

This explains numerous chaos-driven stress and health problems, including [Burnout](mechanics%20of%20burnout.md) and [Depression](#) 

If you do not know **where** you are in a system, a system, usually the human cognitive system, collapses. We maintain position in all areas of our lives, even psychologically. Time, is the cursor of position. Everything revolves around time. 
- Cycles, are models of time. 
- Patterns are recordings in time.

These are fundamentally the substate components of life, and complex systems. We see this everywhere:
- In [computation](), a system is only useful when it has: transformation of logic, state, time. 

This means we can navigate non-linear systems (linearly), and we can navigate unpredictability (probability) but both are mutually incompatible, in human working memory capacity. This means human cognition works like a cursor in time: we only know what's preceeding, and proceeding. This explains why we actually require large goals to orient ourselves, otherwise, we cannot predict the proceeding position, which leads to [analysis-paralysis](analysis-paralysis.md)

This also explains why creating code is only possible, when we are moving forward, and backwards, in one position at a time. Hence, the "Graph depth 1" theory, explaining that we cannot operate in branches, at depth 2 and beyond. 

When we are creating code, we can only think about: what is infront of me, and what is behind me? That is the cursor in time, the cognitive lens. As soon as we reach to a graph depth beyond one _(e.g. "where is that third function that creates the fourth class, and why is it throwing an error?")_ we collapse cognitively.
