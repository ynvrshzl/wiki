i love the name of "Mnemosyne"... the greeks had incredible names in their mythology... and i often reach for "greek" or "latin"-esque names when authoring my work. there is something powerful and beautiful when we pay attention to the craft of our language itself... it's sort of a meta-lens... to be able to look at the micro parts of a system, and truly appreciate all the work a little cell does...

that's capturing the systems essence of the, system. and the paradox, something i'm learning, is that you don't "build" a system... it emerges organically, and it is "sculpted" so to speak... but beyond the theory... i really love how the name itself tells a story about the project. 

names are more than just titles for me, they compress so much into a tiny living space. i think if we steer less into the abstract infiniteness of combinatorial words, a nd more into established mythology... it really captures the human essence, in an otherwise, entirely logical body of work.

to also capture a sense of "duality" is really important for me, since the yin yang is basically the symbol of this project. studying the youniverse, not outside the human being; that breeds nihilism, cynicism, and insignificance (becuase it's the wrong scope.)

we don't need a "tele" scope... we need a "telos" scope. (okay that's pretty clever lol.)

- a "micro"scope to inspect the parts inside the human self (psyche, health, beliefs, etc.)
- a "telos"scope to inspect our integration beyond the self (meaning, purpose, symbiosis, etc.)
- a "macro"scope to inspect the parts that contribute to the human self (social, rythyms, events, etc.)

the double perspective of this project is captured by the famous analogy "do you see the glass half-empty, or half full?" that's not wishful thinking; it goes into the deepest depths of how we human beings operate. 

that's the core of our work. our ability to co-exist with chaos and order, and not become consumed by either-or... and our ability to change perspective... that's significant. 

a project who's name encapsulates the living, breathing nature of it's otherwise, static anatomy, is really important for me. i am not quite sure how to capture the "double-perspective" phenomenon that we're going for here, but honestly, learning more about the greek mythology, and other forms of mythology, is my favorite way to come up with names. it feels less like;

- "press button to generate epic sounding names, from epic" 
- and more... "inspiration from stories, told by ancient human beings, integrated and re-interpreted into a modern network of being." oh and don't forget "the name has to sound epic" lol!
