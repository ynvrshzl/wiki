one of the fundamental motivations , driving this project, is a deep frustration with the current established methods of authoring computer programs. after exploring and researching other fields, I realized something fundamentally critical, programming isn't just hard, for some of us, the process of writing code itself is the hardest part. I argue it is not exclusively a neurodivergent activity nor is this frustration, exclusively neurodivergent, I believe we are fundamentally approaching the field of computation incorrectly to how other complex Fields approach complex subjects. 

**we should not be thinking in the level of source code, we should be implementing at source, but working at our cognitive level. rather than trading programming language, as the language, we treat programming as the human-operable implementation source of the computer. using symbolic language designed for cognitive ergonomic manipulation and transformation of complex underlying implementations.**

realistically, how could one use a symbolic universal set for describing computational systems, and implementing them in the chaotic, often unpredictable environments of programming languages? 

that is one of the fundamental design constraints we are working with: [fundamental-constraints](fundamental-constraints.md)
