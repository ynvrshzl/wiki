"if you're tired, do it tired. nothing can change without motion."

exhaustion / sleep deprivation/chronic fatigue / MFS CE syndrome personified as a nerdy scientist girl. her nickname is "E" as in energy from the theory of relativity. 

she's chronically exhausted, caffeine is her second blood. I often feel a lot like her. I'm chronically fatigued and exhausted. I can't distinguish if I'm dissociated or sleep deprived, and over-the-counter advice doesn't seem to help. diet, sleep, exercise, these things aren't supposed to help, they are supposed to sustain something much more fundamental, which is your microcosmos. essentially, when someone is chronically fatigued, it's almost like a spiritual mental warfare battle, because the body simply does not respond the way the mind needs it to. 

and it's unfortunate because the mind and the body, are not essentially separate, but they are interconnected. when the body is exhausted, the mind has brain fog. when the body feels alive, so does the mind.

"E, how are you able would or do your science work, if you're so exhausted all the time?" she responds: "because I always stay in motion."

it's almost ironic, and one of her favorite catchphrases "we're in motion" might reveal the only real sustainable motive living, for someone with chronic fatigue. it's something I'm actively researching in my life, alongside e.
