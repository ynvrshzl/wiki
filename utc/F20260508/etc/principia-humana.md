and the strangest paradox, is that regardless of whether I was seeking meaning or simply surviving, or struggling to continue living, every single time I always became a fundamentally different creature. I learned and integrated parts of myself that I couldn't have otherwise, given any other circumstance. 

some things you can only discover on field, so to speak. and some sample data is extremely expensive, but even if it is never used, is not erased. it is integrated. it shapes the human to become a fundamentally different form. 

this is significant because it means, when we survive, there is often no other form of sustain, other than to simply biologically survive, what approximates hell. this isn't meaning, it's not nihilism, it's not philosophy. it's science. at our most primal, fundamental parts.

but there is also , some kind of depth that becomes integrated into the human, given they are willing to survive, for an extended period of time, without guarantee of positive outcome. when we don't know what is ahead of us, that is what terrifies the human being the most. we are creatures constantly predicting what may happen, preparing and planning, and moving towards something. if we do not move towards something, we become static...  isolated... perhaps even emotionless or lifeless.

moving towards a positive vector, is our inherent source of meaning as human beings. scientifically, when we establish a goal, and we actively progress towards it (movement forward) based on the current understanding of science, this is the most sustainable natural source of dopamine that we human beings have. in fact it may be the original purpose of the chemical substrate, and the original purpose in substrate form; to move somewhere positive.
