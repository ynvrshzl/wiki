# Apple Hypothesis
__We tend to completely ignore what is right infront of us.__ 

The image of the man with the apple. It's right in front of him, but the painting is somewhat capturing his ignorance. Is it his fault? Or is it that the apple has become so common, that he can no longer see it?

Beyond the philosphy, why does this happen, literally? 

Why does this happen? Why do we seem to ignore what we become used to seeing? Is it purely biological, survival instinct? Or can this explain a large part of the human condition; what we literally cannot see?


# Causes
This happens for this primary reason: we stop noticing what stops changing. We notice things that are dynamic. But, too much dynamic moving parts, and we get noise, which we also filter out. The result is things that continously evolve, but remain reliably steady.
