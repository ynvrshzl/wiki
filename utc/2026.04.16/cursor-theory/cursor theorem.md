# Cursor Theory 
this is a way of looking at subjects and fields, as models of source. essentially, it's the model view controller pattern, we increase understanding, at the expense of context. this is why the further you descend into mathematics or music, the less recognizable it becomes. so we must maintain a stable resolution at which fundamental human intellect is integrated with the world we live in. that's essentially the only ethical purpose of intelligence (and in the context of cursor theory, the only purpose of attention?)

Abstract hypothesis: we cannot understand or experience all of life at once, because everything, everywhere, all at once would be impossibly overwhelming for our senses. so our senses are the fundamental instruments through which we experience life. 

why does it matter to know that we can't understand everything or experience all of life at once? it explains fundamentally why they are so many branches of subjects. it explains why Everything feels so complicated, until we break it down into building blocks. that basically describes how we maintain agency amidst a hostile environment (volatility and chaos)

it also describes a consciousness as a cursor, or attention. a cursor is one object of agency / autonomy, but it can edit fundamentally any position on a two-dimensional document. the document itself is a representation of, three-dimensional ideas. and our ability to introspect is four dimensional. because we see cross-sections of things. 

in summary, this basically helps us understand why subjects and different fields exist. everything is highly specific, it's a perspective, a language, a way of cutting through ambiguity, and increasing our understanding of things, through our senses. music for example, removes spatial auditory ambiguity, and gives us story like emotional elements. that's deeply significant _(and a very complex part where this field branches off into other things)_

we have many subjects and styles, because they each help us clarify the source view of life, in a implemented interface. that's essentially the trade-off of a model, we always lose global structure, for local resolution. this is a massive problem because it means, the further we descend into complex fields, the less useful they become. paradoxically, the whole point of fields is to help us understand parts of life that are too ambiguous to study at whole! 

this is where the cursor comes in. it provides a model for exploration, like mycelium, but without extreme complexity. basically it allows us to explore things in our natural motion, which is; the exploration of multiple positions, one at a time.
