the human persona, is often portrayed as a theatrical mask, because the origins of the word, come from ancient Greece in which a persona was a theatrical mask, a personality for a story. however, the persona is not merely a mask, it is the executive personality which makes decisions on autonomy. 

whether or not every human being has multiple personalities, or multiple sides of their personality, is a question of semantics. the mechanics remain functionally similar; a persona takes the role of execution. 

this presents a fundamental theory, a persona is a role. arguably the most important role, autonomy. 


if any other personality takes the role of autonomy, it is almost always detrimental. growing up is not only necessary, becoming an adult is not only the occupation of a mere responsibility, but the responsibility of one's own autonomy. the fact that the human being can either cause harm or solve problems outside the self, supports the fundamental model; the persona is not only a theatrical mask, but a role, a map of which guides the psyche through the unknowns of life. a set of instructions, beliefs, and directions, in another wise non-linear, unguided, world full of chaos.
