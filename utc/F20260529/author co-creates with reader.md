one of the most fascinating aspects of literature, whether it is interpreted through a TV series, a video game, or a base book, is that, we are dealing with a very real design problem, while telling our stories; 

how do we tell a story, in a world that we do not fully understand? with characters who we do not fully define, with artifacts that are not mapped?

to my knowledge, this is why a wiki system is almost always the natural instinct; because when we deal with an information Gap problem, we want to understand as much of our world as possible before we tell stories in it. 

the problem is that, while this makes cents from a logical, structural perspective, from an ontological epistemological scale, of real human lived experience, we constantly deal with scenarios in which we must make decisions, without fully knowing variables, data, or context. Without having full knowledge.

this is fascinating, but what's even more fascinating is how we author is essentially co-create with the reader. We ourselves do not fully understand the world of our stories; and a paradox, is that understanding everything, would defeat the purpose of a story, or even of being. 

in fact this very idea is explored in literature, in the story of quote the library of Babel end quote. 

this paints an even more tragic idea about human nature, we do not operate functionally with excess of any kind, but we also cannot work with insufficiency. One of the most fascinating models for this area, comes from the human nervous system; a system which must deal with information and completeness, prediction, filtering, and decision-making. 

however one could argue that these are the balanced forces which make being possible. 

if we defined a story world, as being a video game map, with assets, objects, and structures, then the story would be walkable paths, told through the many perspectives available to the author. 

## Examples of author-reader co-creation 

arguably, the author is not the overseer of the world; the author is the engineer, oscillating between reader, and builder of worlds.
