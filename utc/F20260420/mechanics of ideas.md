mechanics of ideas
- when we get ideas, we channel random streams of voltage.
- however, when we try to realize these ideas, we realize they are far more complex, adjacent and parallel than we originally imagined them to be. this phenomenon resembles geometry, suggesting our ideas are structured as points, and work is geometric + structural+ multi-dimensional. why?
- at what point does an idea become a completed work? how do we human beings define completion? (hypothesis: we likely do not define completion as accuracy, rather, we define it as we do understanding itself; good enough to move forward. We define completion by systems outside the product; a movie is complete not when all scenes have been edited, but rather, when it is ready for people cinema.)

hypothetical models 
- imagination is the receiver of ideas. we do not "create" new things. we assemble and receive.
- imagination is the engine 

this possibly explains why trying to organize a wiki, or write stories cohesively, feels impossible; the system is non-linear.
