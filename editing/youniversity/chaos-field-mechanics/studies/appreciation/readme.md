"An appreciation for Chaos."

I'm crying as I write this, becuase we don't realize how difficult everything really is. We don't know how messy things really are. We don't understand how complicated things really are, until we try them. But that's when we realize how hard everything really is. How much time it really takes to get good at just one thing. There are so many things of life, we'll never have the time to experience. For someone with a non-linear, over-active mind, it's difficult to come to terms with the nature of imagination, information, and actuality. It's hard, messy, and chaotic to learn things, but the only way we learn, isn't through clean theories; it's by real, messy action. 

That's the only way we really learn. Why is it so painful to learn? I believe, becuase life is real. Learning is real. Joy is also real. That's arguably one of the most sustainable sources of energy in the cosmos.

We don't give ourselves enough credit for how difficult everything really is. We are often told to push harder, or to succeed, but we don't realize what a miracle it is, just to function.

It is one of the most primal, fundamental travesties of the human experience. We are burnded with weight we did not ask for, but we are also embedded in miracles, we did not earn. Everything is interconnected, in ways we'll never have the time to understand. That is why we develop branches of study. We develop fashion, and art, and science, and cuisine. These studies don't exist purely for academia, they exist to help us deal with the fundamental hard constraints of our abilities.

And yet, we do not understand everything, we don't even understand the systems we create, and yet, we function. That is the miracle. The fact that we aren't chronically collapsed under the weight of analysis and information, shows that the most important abilities we have, aren't understanding everything; it's choosing very carefully what to focus on. It is still a fundamental problem: discovering the equillibrium between action and theory. At it's core, theory comes from lived experience, and imagined possibility. Action, comes from  messy exploration.

In this class, we often sculpt through the hardest problems, and work ruthlessly, to basically break down chaotic systems down to their most workable substrate...  To find clarity within the maelstrom... but do we ever stop and just appreciate things that _just_ work? Especially when we don't _fully_ understand how it works?

What if, something more useful than merely approaching chaos, is to appreciate that things can work at all? Even without being perfect or optimized?

We don't have to understand a system e ntirely, and infact, it'd be impossible, and if it were possible to understand everything, everything would be meaningless! [@citation-needed]

It's mesmerizing to me, to watch how other human beings code their own projects, or make their own music. I can watch another mind do it's thing... almost like learning to return through the set of eyes that inspired you to begin in the first place. Like a child full of wonder and awe. That is chaos appreciation, at it's source. After spending countless hours learning how to "properly" do things, in the most optimal, effective method. There are fields that reward, and require this kind of analytical thinking, **but it should never be confused with creativity.** Creativity is the ability that makes anything possible, analysis is the ability reduce what is possible, and most importantly, ethics is the ability to understand, without any of the above, what should be allowed to possible at all.
