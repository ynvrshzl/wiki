_Abstraction, explored in the context of Chaos Agency_

Why is abstraction necessary for human beings to work with complex systems? 

Human working memory is incredibly finite. We can hold, an estimated 4 things at once.

1. Complete order

At the bottom-most layer, we have complete order. Predictability. Perfection. This is mathematic arithmetic. The color palette. 

However, as we work our way upwards, building complexity, we effectively increase the N (number) of possibilities. This is what we may call; Complexity, or in it's finest terms, Spaghetti Chaos. This is why we build layers, to hide what's underneath, and only see what we need at a time.

Effectively, this fucntions like an MRI machine, cross-sections of a 3-Dimensional thing, represented in 2D layer space.
