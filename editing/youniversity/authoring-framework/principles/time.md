Abstract: __A story only makes sense, when it has time, and when it is read together.__

Time is also a princple, not only for the model of the story itslef, but for the process of creating cohesive literature; it is inescapable. In order to progress anything, by definition, we have to spend time with it. The quality of our time is important, but we can't neglect the quantity; it's messy. It's annoying. **But if you love it, it's worth it.**

This immediately makes non-linear systems, impossible to understand, _e.g. wikis or world-building._ The paradox is that, the story does not live inside these systems, the story lives as a thread, when non-linear ideas come together.

__A story without time is a web of ideas.__

A story without time, is a loose interconnected web of ideas, resembling a wiki, unstructured thought. 

It is not inherently "too complicated", rather, it is our human thoughts being captured in raw, unprocessed form, without filtering or systems. This makes the story we want to author, __impossible to understand.__ Becuase [the purpose of a wiki system, is not to understand, rather, to archive a living system of knowledge.](wiki.md)

We use models to help us create linear cohesiveness, becuase so much of our stories are open-ended and loosely defined, _(despite the purpose of a wiki being to describe things in detail.)_

This possibly explains why stories, in their raw, fragmented form, feel impossible to work with. 

- [Time in non-linear systems](editing/chaos-autonomy/non-linear-systems/time.md)

For example even music is non-linear, until we introduce the axis of time; a piano is non-linear. Without time, you can get lost in-between the notes while learning a song.

This reveals an even more fundamental idea: coherence is only established at whole-scale. This means that a story, or a song, only makes sense when played as a whole. Chapters are tools for creation, not for creation. (?)
