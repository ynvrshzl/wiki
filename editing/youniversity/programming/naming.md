__Conclusion:__ Choose a naming scheme that you like the best, and stick to it!

When we decide on a name for something in our computer program

The implications of a naming scheme, teaches us something more important, outside the names themselves: but how we arrive at decisions and maintain consistency. It is important, to remember to choose one case to maintain consistency, which is more important than making the "perfect" decision. [^1]

Some cases are stylistic, while others are domain-locked. In the example of variables, a name cannot contain " " whitespaces or "-" hyphens. This is important, becuase names are single-handedly, the smallest atomic unit we interact with everyday in our code. It forms the basis of our language and the way we think about our computer programs.

| Case        | Function                        | Use cases            |
| ----------- | ------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| camelCase   | Gives emphasis to...            | Variables            |
| PascalCase  | Gives emphasis to...            | Variables, Classes   |
| snake\_case | Gives emphasis to **spaces**    | Filenames, Variables |
| kebab-case  | Gives emphasis to chained words | Filenames, URLs,     |


[^1]: "Consistency over urgency." is a core concept in [overcoming Analysis Paralysis](overcoming-analysis-paralysis.md)
