Granular-based learning hypothesis;
everything in music can be granularly decomposed into fundamental (^a) attributes of music. some (^a) in music are like...

musical base (^a):

- time: the actual movement of music. e.g., starting slow at first, and building speed as a side-effect, not a goal [footnote: 3]
- rythym: the position of notes in time

down to the actual process of playing;
- left hand + right hand: each hand has it's own range and personality in a piece.
- - taking regular breaks 
- proper use of the sustain pedal to avoid muddy notes

all the way down to the most granular details...
- hand tension, muscle anatomy knowledge, finger ligaments, etc.
- proper posture
- breathing during intense pieces
- chopin taught personality of each finger (dude, that's as granular as it gets lol)

and so i ask myself... who taught all of this? if i don't have a teacher, can i still learn to play the piano? yes! infact, all of this stuff is personal observation! and that's when i realized... wait... this must be how the meta-theorists (the people who make the theory) actually develop theory... they learn from first-hand experience... sometimes, painfully... "wait... the point of a teacher is so you don't have to rediscover it painfully on your own..." but "if you don't have a teacher, you can iindeed learn on your own.. and with access to the correct tools and knowledge... you can avoid pain."
