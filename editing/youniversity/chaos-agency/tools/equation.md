# Chaos Equation
Here is an arbitrary equation represeation of chaos and complexity:

$$
\sum (x = (t * p)^n)
$$

"Equation of general chaos in systems"

Chaos can be defined, in it's most irreducible, arbitrary form as;

- "x" (chaos, the unknown variable) 
- the total _"n"_ number of possibilities _"p"_ 
- within _"t"_ things of a system... _"E"_

The system is represented by the summation "E", becuase, the system itself, could be described as the __emergent compound whole__ of both, the chaos generated by possibility, and the system that keep it from consuming the whole. 

Through this lens, the system is a feedback loop; it protects the structure from collapsing by chaos, but it also allows chaos to exist
