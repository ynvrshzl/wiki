the only reason we use bash (or a low level scripting language) rather than source programming, is for much the same reason we have discussed in previous conversations; such as in SQL versus PHP/JS. bash may be limited and verbose, considering its capabilities are mostly system level access and scripting...

... however, bash, is much like SQL, in the sense that they are domain-specific grammars but precisely constrain the range of the technology, in such a way that it maximizes creativity, and reduces the unstable overhead of working with the full power of a source language. 

- Essentially SQL/bash is like working with Lego bricks; it is tangible and solid. 
- Whereas working with source, is like building the chemical compounds of Lego bricks; incredibly powerful, and any shape is virtually possible, but it's also unsustainable for actual building, because the cognitive bandwidth required to construct systems at the molecular level, exceeds our capabilities of building real things with it. 
that's why a compiler engineer focuses on a specific set of problems, a specific strata of the abstraction. and that's why a programmer designer focuses on the linguistics and features of their syntax, because that's the baseline of abstraction that makes programming languages functional. 

Baseline abstraction/complexity.
