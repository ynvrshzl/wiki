## (0)

"paradox of analysis"

why do we human beings classify things? (analytical lens theory)

hypothesis:

- we explain things in detail (analytical lens) to reduce ambiguity and noise.
- the more details we add, the less ambiguity of subject.
- paradoxically, at a certain threshold, we have so many details, the subject is lost in translation, and becomes ambiguous again.


  
This is what happens when we try to understand something in isolation, rather than synthesize information as insight, into a larger whole, into autonomy (action).
