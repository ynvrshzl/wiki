**"Context for this class."** 

In this study, we are using real-world examples, such as [music]() as a real-world basis of the [proposed general model of learning.](model.md)

[Music]() is a great example becuase it provides real-time feedback. We can observe how we learn, and also create, which is an even more complex ability.

In which we both use theory and exploration to continously improve our [autonomous abilities](autonomous-abilities.md) and our [sensory abilities](sensory-abilities.md) 

We learn through both **theory** and **experience.** What does this mean? The more music you **experience,** the more your own personal library expands. This means we have more materials for assembly, and the more we learn about how things assemble together __(theory)__ the more we can re-create, and study. For example, when listening to a beautiful symphony, we start to notice the layers, rather than the entire orchestra. This feeds back into our exploratory cycles. This is the primary feedback loop.
