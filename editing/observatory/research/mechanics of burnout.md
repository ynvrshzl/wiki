# Mechanics of burnout 
in the context of healing from trauma, or rebuilding your life, burnout is a shadow. we can only use so much with our human abilities, before we becoming exhausted. but paradoxically, if given a reason, we can endure, perhaps anything.[^1]

## 1.0 Burnout is not the enemy
so if burn out is not the enemy, What is the enemy? numbness. because when you don't feel anything, that's essentially...

if healing requires cycles and consistency, then burnout becomes the antithesis... however, treating burnout like an enemy, only makes things worse. in fact, it might be the biggest symptom of burnout. being afraid of falling behind or growing out of consistency, that fear means you care. that means you're engaged with something real, something real that you care about. 

if you were truly not healing, you wouldn't care. this is perhaps the first principle of healing from trauma, especially healing from trauma on your own without support. which is entirely possible, and it's my own lived experience, and it fuels most of our ethical work.

[^1]: the reason we used the term, perhaps, is because we still do not understand the limits of our human capacities especially when pushed beyond design. Victor Frankel for example...
