# Beliefs of Dawn Zen
Dawn Zen believed that there were several parts of the human. 

These beliefs eventually became his book, "teachings of zen." How to overcome pain for the modern human living in lost-angelis.

- Primal Self - The most primordial, animalstic self. The "ego". Not good or bad, simply, primitive. Must be tamed. Must be directly, like an animal.
- Primary Personification - Dawn believed there was a primary governing emotion/personifiecation, but this was not the true self. Rather, it was the duty to tame.
- Emotions and Chemicals as forces of nature. E.g. rage as electricity, love as fire.
- Actualized Self - The human is not the body, The energy does not die

These components were fundamental to understanding the self (which is intellect), Acting (which is autonomy, body, physical) and becoming (which is spiritual faith.)
