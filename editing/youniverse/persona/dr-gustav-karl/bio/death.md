The death of Dr. Gustav is tragic. It is a moral lesson on spending time, and learning to live with the imperfections of our human condition.

He understood everything there is to know about life, and in knowing infinite knowledge, he lost love for his grandchild, [Eva]() the person he fought so hard for, faded with time.

By the time he discovered a cure, his body had wasted away. What little time he had left with her, was gone. 

_"I regret nothing, for I have lost everything."_

He feels regret so deep, at a cosmic level.

His life story, isn't necessarily a lesson in "don't work hard." Rather, it is a lesson in extremity, obsession, and studying the limits of our human conditions. We can indeed, solve complex problems, but at what cost? **Is what we have today, enough as it is?**
