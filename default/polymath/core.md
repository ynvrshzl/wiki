# "The tension of the non-linear thinker"

**A case study, and research on the core tension and strength of the polymath/neurodivergent archetype human personality.**

"I want to do so much. I want to share so much. I can see and feel beyond my capacity to explain or even understand my own feelings. There is so much within me. Most of it will never be published. Not becuase it didn't survive the draft, but because it never made it to paper."

This is the core perspective of the author archetype, a sub-strata of the polymath archetype. [^1]

This is the tension of the polymath, one near and dear to my heart. We feel so much, and articulate so little.

There is so much I want to do, and such little time. Such low energy. So much effort. This isn't lethargic laziness; it's chronic overload, at the most biological extremes.

This may be our greatest strength. Becuase it forces us to work with the natural limits of our biology, and art of constraint. Constraint gives birth to shape, importance.
