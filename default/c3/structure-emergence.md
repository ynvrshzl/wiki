# Structure emerges from necessity

This study is designed to be practically applicable and informative, for those of us who struggle  with [Analysis Paralysis](), [Decision Fatigue]() and [General Overthinking](general-overthinking.md). These symtpoms appear to be phenomena from the idea of [Chaos and Order]()

The main idea is this; a canvas has infinite potential. We could have arranged a computer in any number of ways. But there are only a handful of useful arrangements. Which appears to reference the idea of [The Zone of Proximal Development.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zone_of_proximal_development)



structure emerges from necessity. think about it; a computer is assembled by structural parts, that were created to solve a problem, or to fill a necessity. From the outside, it looks like a single thing, a "computer." That abstraction is necessary in order for us to function with all of the chaotic information of everyday life. However, from a structural perspective, the abstract box of wires and impossible structures, is actually a sum of parts. The chaos from structure emerges when the sum of those parts, give possibility to things outisde itself...(i think?) 

If we conceptualize each part as as anatomy of a body, you begin to reverse-engineer the biggest parts to their smallest, most fundamental substrate. 

The idea here is that structure, atleast in computational models, emerges from necessity. a problem to solve. not usually an idea. ideas seem to be organic and chaotic by nature, whereas a necessity/problem, is structured.

So then, if structure emerges from necessity
