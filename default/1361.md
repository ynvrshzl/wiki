In continuation with our series of the positive applications of technology, we look at the very structure of the web itself: HTML.

As a base technology, HTML is ironically; state-of-the-art design, and almost entirely accidental.
- It is not a programming language, but contains interactive elements. 
- It is primarily document-graphical driven, but relies on browser enviornments for graphics.
- It can make easy-to-use graphics, but becomes unreadable for developers

It has arguably become the ancient backbone that powers the graphics of today's web, making a large portion technology used today. What is the significance of this technology?

Markdown is in essence, HTML but compressed and highly specialized for in-line content rendering.

If we examine the structure of HTML in general, the most basic element is the text itself. Naturally, human speech is organized by pause, emotion, body language, and other factors.

Visually, we encode these aspects of language, as symbolic represenations. E.g., the "?" symbol, translating to "Question?".

Beyond the symbolic analysis, HTML reveals a powerful cross-fade between: **natural human expression, information presentation, and display.**
