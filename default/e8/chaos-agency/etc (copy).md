## Mathematics and Chaos
hypothesis: the core of mathematics is quantity and change, represented as a symbolic language: numbers and operations. quantity (numbers) and descriptions of quantity change (arithmetic)

- numbers, which are actually symbols for real things (values, quantity, count.)

- for numbers, to be symbolically + realistically to be useful to the real-world counterpart of quantity, we have to represent how quantities change in real life.

- so we developed "basic operations" to change them. like how cells clone, divide, split, merge, etc.

- but before we discovered cellular life, we counted on our fingers. we counted rocks. we counted members of our tribe. keeping track of the quantity of things is what keeps chaos from consuming systems. it's built-into us. counting is one of our most primal abilities.

- addition, more, up, big, give the cell a friend

- subtraction, less, down, small, reduce cells

- this is base arithmetic... becuase before multiplication (e.g. cellular cloning) and division (e.g. cellular splitting)... those are modifiers of addition and subtraction

- after basic arithmetic, we establish a micro-language with 4 primities (add, sub, mul, div) and from this logic, we basically can describe more complex changes of quantity.

- (here's the practically exciting part!) mathematics + chaos agency hypothesis: no matter how complicated mathematics becomes, you can always reduce the concept back to these primitives, and even proto-primitive, the core engine of mathematics: a symbolic language, which helps us human beings describe quantity and change in real life.
