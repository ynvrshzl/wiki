> The code

Software Engineering is more similar to musical mastering, than to musical production.

This is contrary to the way we approach code today: as finished products

- Musical sound engineering focuses on stability of sound quality, emphasized frequency series, and 
- More specifically, sound engineering focuses on balancing sound reproduction, across a multitude of devices and equipment, often prioritizing quality over specific reproduction on  high-quality mixing enviornments.
- "A song must sound 'good' on both, a large stage, and a nintendo DS."
That kind of quality control enters engineering requirements, once we realize that equillibrium and quality, are key factors, not specific sound playback

# Implication
What does this mean? That sound engineering is similar to software engineering?

> Software arguably deals with a finished, compiled, output product. But quality of code, especially stress testing the system across a multitude of settings, is arguably the real quality of the human effort. 

> Quality is measured by: equillibrium across diverse, non-ideal enviornments.

> The code could be considered source, and the representation is a projection, one of many sound playback systems. 

> Over-specializing a sound system, or a single code representation, will become detrimental to the lifetime of a code system, especially when considering the amount of human cognitive resources required to build, release, and maintain these massive systems.
