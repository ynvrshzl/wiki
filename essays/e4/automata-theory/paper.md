One of the most important ideas, is that; things do change. The hardest part of mental illness, is not knowledge;
- "Things will change."
- "Your ancestors survived, you will too."
- "Your radiation levels are getting better."
- "There are signs of life."

The hardest part of mental illness, isn't "things change." It's believeing that your life *can* change.

Why is this so powerful and significant for the human being? To *believe* that things *can* change, not merely that they *do* change.

I believe, logically and mechanically, when something is reframed from;
- Action as effect, "This does change."
- To, Action as affect, "This could change."

Something imoprtant happens; we see the earliest signs of "Mechanical Faith." Faith, without vision.

The hardest part for mental illness:
- Anorexia
- Depression
- Dysmorphia

Is that they siphon the "Human Inspiritus", that which breathes life into things. Regardless of complex phenomena, all human beings, whom struggle with chronic issues, eventually converge into
- Futility
- Hopelesness
