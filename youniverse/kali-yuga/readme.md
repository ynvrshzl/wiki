# "Kali Yuga"
Is is a world of darkness, where the monks must forge the respite of life by creating fire, light, energy. Through dance, chants, martial arts...


## Story

The world of the monks, there is a giant gong, it is said that it takes the force of a thousand monks.

the idea is inspired by several sources, such as ancient Chinese war tradition and history, as well as Alan watts, we used to give lectures, and used the sound of a gong as a placeholder "the purpose is... (gong)".

this world is meant to represent the force of human life. the force of life itself. which is one of the most important principles of the yagi tribe. 

this world definitely represents the force of life, as during a scene when Joey Doom is suffering tremendously, there is a monk singing ancient Tibetan throat music. there is another Monk that is yelling maniacally as the gong slams harder and harder. this is meant to represent torment, tragedy, insanity, it's one of the many worlds we use to symbolize horrific aspects of the human experience, and The duality of the force of life, that in spite of such suffering, life will persist and survive, even against all odds. this is represented by the maniacal monk who is yelling, As the World turns into a red storm of blood. the sky's turn dark red, the crimson fog clouds the eyes. and even despite such darkness and pain, the force of Life persists, as a strike of lightning throughout the storm. there is also the concept of the eye of the storm which is peace amidstuffering. this is a deep principle from Buddhism and Hinduism traditions.
