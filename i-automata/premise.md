there is a common understanding in neuroscience that, neurotransmitters, chemical bonds, little molecular forms of life, create what we call our experience. emotions, memories, abilities, trauma, comedy, all of that lives somewhere within these mechanical systems of life. but that is a very reductionistic lens, and it often leads to nihilism, which is actually the opposite effect we should conjure, so reductionism is ineffective here. 

when looking at stars outside of our planet, it can seem like an empty careless void. nobody knows if the universe is uncaring, or if there really is a higher order of logic and power, far beyond our understanding, and I believe with this project, this video game of cells, we are not asking the question, "is there a God?"  we are asking "given how big the universe is, both outside self, and within the self, what can we do to reduce suffering, and better life, ethically?"

that is essentially the main ethics of my work. and in this project, i, we are exploring a philosophical story;

what if cellular automata, joined neuroscience, neurotransmitters, molecular life, universe, microcosm, philosophy, together in one shared sandbox space? 

a cellular game, inspired by games such as doodle god, and the universe cards and everything, essentially treating aspects of human life, like emotion, ability, stories, as events, cellular life. where neuroscience understands emotions as chemicals, the game understands it as cells of little worlds.
