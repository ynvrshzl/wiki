What i'm even more fascinated by, is how we human beings produce "forward thinking" 



I think our abiliy to predict in the future, is much less about;



"Where is this trajectory going to lead if I calculate my odds"



And more embodied;



"If I throw this object, given my past history of muscle memory, I can 'project' a path of aim."



This is foundational becuase it implies;

- Goals, tasks and directions are synonymous with a fundamental object; the target. An eventual destination of effort.

- It is even more important to realize; goals are only as meaningful as they are related to a context. 

- When goals become I/O feedback of a larger goal. 



Now this is very abstract; so I will provide a real-world example where this is really difficult to understand otherwise.



Music. When we create music, we are working with local areas of ideas. Perhaps a melody, or a 4-bar sequence of patterns. From that local area, we begin sampling outwards. Not necessarily in a perfect continous timeline, Rather; we begin generating *enough* material, such that we will be able to sequence and assemble loose parts, in a timeline.



Now, i observed this not o nly applies to the creative processes; but it also applies to incomplete models of information; becuase we we think about it, the musician does not "create" the song from beginning to end, the way an architect would present a schematic as a "completed system." We apply forward thinking; predictive processing (among other complex abilities.)
