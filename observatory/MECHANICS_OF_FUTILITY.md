the problem with logical pure truth, is that; it confines search space into small boundaries where truth is local and observable. 

what this means phenomenologically, is that, conditions like depression, anxiety, and specially non mental conditions like futility, and hopelessness, are states of human cognition, in which we experience extreme compression of our lens of focus, usually onto a fixed point, a finite local space. at this scale, negativity is the default source of truth, because...

I experienced this today, I woke up early, I thought I did everything right, but it felt like I was female sisyphus, pushing a boulder uphill, only for it to roll all the way back down, and I have to start all over again, climb the mountain. with far less energy and respite. and yet some things somewhere inside me continues wanting to push that Boulder uphill, knowing it's unfair to fight an uphill battle, being a finite creature. and yet, when my focus expands, I realize that although the default condition is negative, today is sample data, in the grander scheme of my life. I realized that waking up early, cutting my sleep, and forcing myself to be productive, simply does not work. we are not machines. but our anatomy is very machine-like. 

however, we are not machines. we cannot simply be programmed to follow perfect order. we cannot be optimized. sometimes bad days just happened. 

but for someone like myself, we noticed the fleeting moments of life, and the finite nature of all things, which makes everything incredibly more significant, but it also makes wasting, feel catastrophic, because those are experiences and moments, time that you will never get back. and instead of revisiting a meaningful memory, the self becomes tormented by what could have been. this is one of the most profound form of grief that I know of, perhaps it's the most ancient human grief; a grief for what could have been. 

for people like myself who often get lost in systems, we know what it's like to discover a perfect optimal path for a problem, so when that is not our reality every single day, it feels like we are doing something functionally wrong. we must remember we are not just systems; the strongest systems are the most adaptive.

not knowing what to do, yet being able to perfectly analyze in retrospect. that's one of the most brutal functions of human cognition, our ability to see clearly only in reverse, but being forced to live in forwards, without the ability to see the path ahead. and sometimes history does not simply repeat itself statistically, we can't merely predict the future by looking at the past, sometimes learning happens in the moment. perhaps it's exactly in the most troubling, confusing Days of our human lives, that rather than discovering new meaning, we simply remember the quiet hum which keeps us alive. the engine that drives our seeking of truth.

## Fragility,  the twin of futility 
if utility could be personified as a person, I believe fragility would be a mirror of futility. 

we often forget just how distant we are from absolute collapse. this is not existential horror, or cynical apathy, I believe understanding the fragility of life, maybe the only antidote we really have to futility. and I believe utility arises from the natural side effect of living. if one feels futile it is a sign they are alive and they are trying. it is also a warning sign that's something in the living system is not functioning properly. 

how many times throughout our life do we ever stop and pay attention to how fragile everything really is? and how paradoxically, most things are not collapsing... even though everything is incredibly complicated...
