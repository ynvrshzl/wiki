I have a hypothesis for how we human beings truly think. perhaps this is more phenomenological then a new hypothesis, but here is the reason why we don't really solve life's problems methodically. take for example this statement; "set your goals, plan your day, focus." those are abstract properties, describing object, those are objective elements. but we human beings experience life phenomenologically, subjectively, through subject. e. g., "my goal is to become a singer, I will schedule every afternoon to practice, that's when I can focus at my best." notice how the abstract elements become objects of subjects, rather than subject in and of themselves. 

this exposes how we think as well, we often classify using familiar properties and attributes. for example, we may remember we lost a password, while not remembering the exact string of information. we may eventually remember structures of the password, such as password length, relation to real life, etc. even if we cannot access specific information, we can access the structure describing information. mathematics exposes this idea, especially algebra, because while we do not fundamentally understand everything, we can model expressions using variables. this provides two things, the possibility of model expression for unknown information, providing structure. as well as, reusability of model and pattern.
