# "Systems Coherence and Process Interference in Human Creative Systems."
This is a quick hypothesis about creativity design, and I think it has more to do with creative architecture...

## Areas
Specifically, i'm emphasizing two areas for the focus of this study;

- Stories
- Coding

I also want to introduce "Music" as counterbalance field, of which i'm experienced in, so the hypothesis makes itself somewhat transparent. More on that later.

## Hypothesis
So the hypothesis is something I call "System Coherence and Process Interference in Human Creative Systems." The problem is "process interference" and the subject is "system coherence and continuity." In essence...

- Creative productivity-driven systems, wether creating stories or coding, exhibit a specific pattern of human creativity; something like an "Evolutionary, seasoned, prototoyped, modelled, staged processes of systems."
- This is contrary to what we may conceptualize as "architecture is the optimal way to build things." However, In all of these fields, we almost never begin with precise architecture; we begin with proto-mediums. Ideas. Problems to solve. Eventually, these assemble into instructions on how to build the blueprint itself. The blueprint then becomes the system we base the product by; wether code or stories
- This is fascinating becuase it implies there is a boundary between architecture in systems, and creation of systems. But both must work symbiotically in order to produce a coherent system. 

## "Coherence and Continuity"
What defines a coherent system? And why does it matter?

"Coherence and Continuitiy" are properties of a system, that measures both;
- The manageability of development of systems *(i.e., the author of a story)*
- Systems human beings can understand *(i.e., a reader of a story)*

I believe, these are key principles in creative, productive systems, regardless of field semantics. I also believe it is important to understand processes, especially for complex systems becuase; understanding the process (order of things) may be the difference between an abandoned system, and a completed work.

## Significance
What does this mean? What is the significance of human creativity & productivity as a non-architectural model? 

I believe in both examples we see a phenomenon; **some ideas, of which are introduced in later stages of a system, become significantly influential, that the entire system changes as a result.** Example 
- Introducing a new pattern in code
- Redefining a character's motives in a story

This phenomenon is closer to organic growth, exhibited in natural ecosystems, than to industrial architecture

- It is at this stage that we often "re-write" or "restart" a chapter, a module, and more often than not; we often restart entire systems.
- This study is focused on the hypothesis; "is this a natural part of building complex creative architectures? or is there a proper model for separating these processes so interference is minimal?"

## Symptoms
The phemonenological analysis is; do we begin by crafting, or by architecting? Both processes are required for complex creative systems. However, according to my observed hypothesis of human creativity; we don't start a system; the system emerges and we co-create with it. This means a system is not entirely explicit information; it is part tacit (implicit), and part precision (explicit).

In the real world this looks like;
- Drafting processes in writing
- Versions in code

## Purpose of discussion
The phenomenology of creating complex systems, is often not discussed in productivity or industrial settings; yet these are the enviornments where human creativity and productivity is most required.
