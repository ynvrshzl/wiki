# "Implicit Systems & Frameworks theory"
I am fascinated by this phenomenon; and it seems that it is not explained industrially becusae it is largely a phenomenological process; it happens as an implicit process, not explicitly.

But I believe this single-handedly makes the difference between;
- Abandoned systems; An incomprehensibly complex system, that is inevitably abandoned
- Living systems; A sophisticated complex system, that is extensible.

## Main hypothesis
The question is "How are frameworks built? Are they (a) discovered, (b) architectured or (c) co-authored?"

The implications of this hypothesis are deeply systematic;
- Coding
- Stories
- Music

## Significance
This is imoprtant becuase it is not merely about design choices today; these choices become structural foundations for systems we build in the future. And that means some systems are foundational; not continously evolving. 

This is why standardization is a major aspect of systems & frameworks; they provide pattern and stability, and those are the foundations of strong structures that stand the battles of time.

## Adaptive systems theory
This implies an implicit observation; the systems we end up using are almost always not the most perfect; but the most adaptive.
