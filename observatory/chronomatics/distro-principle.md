# Distri principle
this idea suggests that time efficiency + intuition must be considered in parallel. 

often, what is intuitive is not most efficient. what is efficient is often not most intuitive. 

For example when we work, the most effective method would be to simply follow a perfect absolute sequence of steps. but how is the sequence of steps created in the first place? that must come from an external source, that external source is human intuition. we begin with human intuition, sampling, exploring, testing, learning. just how we learn to walk, not by observing, but by practicing. we may stumble and fall, and that is part of the calibration process. once we learn to walk, it becomes embedded. 

This same principle of learning, applies to time management.

we have to understand that distractions may be part of the natural process. we cannot be 100% perfectly efficient and optimal with our time. it's simply not realistic and not physically possible. because even the development of a plan, requires us to deal with the unknown. and that will always require sampling, testing, trying, externalizing, reflecting. these are all processes that are not immediately effective or noticeable, but over time they compound and as a result, ability becomes embedded, integrated. that's the productivity we usually see in superficial 

In an ideal World we could schedule 1 hour for highly specialized, compressed focused. however, distraction may be a part of productivity. the word productivity itself is is a combination; 

product + activity. 

this suggests productivity is not merely about producing an output, but the process itself; the activity.
