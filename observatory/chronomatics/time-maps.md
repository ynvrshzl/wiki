we have a genuine, non-trivial problem; how does a human being deal with the increasing demands, pressure, and complexity of the modern world wild balancing individual elements? 

one cannot simply live for utility or product. Aristotle explored this [citation needed](). however one can also not live entirely for interests or goals. we have to be fundamentally integrated into a cohesive larger whole, because that establishes a baseline for optimal human function. 

we could consider time to be bounded reality, snapshots of the self. what this means is, time establishes what is real for us right now. we cannot think about 50 different parts of our lives, if the current moment in time requires a specific task. for example, if one is exercising, it is arguably physically impossible to work on anything else, even if it is possible to think of separate elements in one's life.

so if what we think, is dependent on condition and prompt, and what we are able to physically act, is dependent on time context and ability, then we need a instrument, a tool which allows us to both prompt, and provide context for the current moment. this is why the schedule, is one of the few Tools in this area that generally helps us human beings translate imagination to reality. 

once we see  time management as the translation between headspace to physical space, start to realize the importance of productivity, as an integrated element of human life. it is measurable, it is not forcible. productivity itself is not a force, it is an effect. we usually think of productivity as a product itself, but the name productivity is actually a combination of product plus activity. productivity itself is a process, and we usually only measure progress.

the general model of time management in human life is simple in essence; what we schedule, becomes our temporal reality, relatively speaking. however this is too much of a ideal bottle because we may schedule certain things, and the biology will simply refuse to perform. this is really a question of understanding one's own energy levels, circadian type, and creating a map based on energy, rather than time or responsibility in isolation. it must be mapped according to our energy cycles and levels throughout the day. this is how our ancestors used to work. in the industrial age we have forgotten that we are not machines. we cannot force ourselves to produce, that is simply a recipe for burnout. exhaustion. 

this model proposes an equilibrium between responsibility, adaptability, and individuality. 

- responsibility can be defined as the external relationships 
- adaptability can be defined as the internal ability to change according to the necessities of the external world 
- individuality/locality can be described as the natural state of the self without external pressure. 

when we balance these three variables, we start to see that the schedule is not merely a timeline, it is a map. a map of cycles.

logically it makes sense to simply schedule what one wants to achieve and work on, however, awareness is half the battle. showing up and putting in the work, sounds extremely superficial, because it is missing crucial elements that are required for human action. we don't merely "lock in and focus" we are not automata, we depend on biological systems, just as much as we depend on synthetic systems. 

the synthetic systems discussed here are one third of the equation. biological systems influence most of our ability. but our psychology drives the entire system. it's important to understand what is important. it is important to balance between utility and individuality. everyone has their own custom build. just like in an RPG. here we simply study and present different abilities, items, potions, statistics, etc. it is up to the player to define their build.
