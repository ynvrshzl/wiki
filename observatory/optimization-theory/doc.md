# "Theory of optimization"

I have a theory, as to why perfection is not achievable for living systems, and especially not for us human beings. 

I want to illustrate what perfection looks like, classically speaking in today's economic driven fast-paced life. perfection may look like always being on time. always having energy. being as healthy as humanly possible. always being productive. never wasting time. never losing money. never making the wrong decisions. 

many of those perfect ideals, have patterns; they are either decisions or external forces we cannot control. perhaps it is our ability to make decisions, and live with forces we cannot control, that truly defines what it means to be human. we are essentially navigating an endless ocean without a map pre-installed. we sort of forge the map from what is given to us, and what we choose to make of it... 

I want to clarify that's a hypothesis, and one cannot simply reduce the conditions of every single human being's life, as a result of the choices they make. sometimes, we can make the best decisions, and still have negative outcomes. and sometimes we can make poor decisions, and have very lucky outcomes. this is essentially a contributing principle, that makes life incredibly difficult to reduce or understand. perhaps we're science reaches its terminal velocity, philosophy, mysticism, spirituality, explain the interconnected integrated parts of life. 

I also want to clarify that, according to current understanding of thermodynamics and physics, a system can never be 100% resource efficient. so it is literally physically impossible to be perfect. 

## Emphasis: "The model of Optimization"
he emphasis of this theory, is optimization. since we can never achieve perfect state, we developed the ability to optimize. 

however, optimizing also has its drawbacks, and the major emphasis of this study, is the model of optimization. essentially when we optimize, we are reducing search space, surface area, which makes optimization possible at all. optimizing is a post process of exploration. it cannot happen during sampling phases. because of the very definition of optimization is to reduce the gap between resources consumed, and resources ineffectively utilized, which we may label today as "reducing time wasted" or "reducing income loss."

the philosophy of perfection, is also a narrative driven by illusion; perfection seems like the answer to all of life's problems, but upon closer inspection, when we interrogate our ideals of perfection, we realize that it is not our ability to make perfect decisions, or control external forces, that would solve life's problems on tragedies... rather, the greatest force of optimization that we have ever seen in living systems, is the ability to adapt. to change. to evolve. 

if the only constant in the universe is change (c) then an ability which actively mirrors it, is required by living systems. adaptation is the mechanical, autonomous ability to respond to environmental, internal pressures of change. the immune system protects living systems, buy crafting biological super weapons, capable of defending the body from any virus that will ever exist. there is perhaps no greater force of adaptation, in the Kingdom of anatomy, then that of the engine that is the immune system. but in the universe, perhaps there is no force greater than that of human adaptation.
