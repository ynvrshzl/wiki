# The Author's  Dillemma
I have noticiced in my own local practice, there is a phenomenon: The "completeness" of a world. This explains why we exhibit "perfectionism" when underneath-the-mask, it is closer to "a general sense of missing material." The story world could always be more complete. There could always be more realism added to a world, or more material developed.

The problem with realism is the scales of resolution vs. abstraction time. Eventually, authoring becomes a task of labor, and we miss the point of the story: it's not the forest, it's the trees.
