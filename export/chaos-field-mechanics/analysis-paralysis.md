Analysis Paralysis (AP) is a truly problematic condition. 

Something chronically under-explored, it reveals one of the deepest parts of our human cognition: when we become paralyzed by analysis, the mind is desperately trying to reconstruct order, from within chaos.


So how does our biology solve this? By choosing the imperfect chaos anyway. We see this everywhere.



We are incredibly good at selecting what to ignore. That's essentially what focus is, it's not about subject; it's choosing what to ignore.

AP is terrifying becuase is masquerades itself as progress, and genuine creativity. The problem with unbounded analysis is, it behaves like creativity, which is defined as unbounded by nature. But even creativity is motivated by a domain goal, a reason to be creative. Otherwise, we run into abstract possibilities, at which we can no longer make sense of. This is the origin of [Chaos](#)

Still, i genuienly ask myself, "how is it possible that we aren't completely paralyzed by all of the opportunities and possibilies we have?"

This is a geniune question, and not a single answer. It's open-ended in nature, and our biology, is one of the many non-linear solutions to this non-linear problem.

So how do we avoid information overload, and focus on real goals?
