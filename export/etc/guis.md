here's a fascinating series of Technology research almost specifically human computer interaction, and how graphical user interfaces either increase or economics or become detrimental to navigation. 

the study begins with a hypothesis "is the effort of graphical computer systems, justified by its complexity, as compared to its ergonomics?" 

one area where this becomes fascinating, is a conversation we had a few months ago. basically, I was studying the difference between source code, and graphical software (which is an abstraction of source code) 

from that study, we concluded that for some ergonomic activities, such as sculpting or 3D modeling, specialized software, actually creates somewhat of a cognitive environment, the way a forge would contain ergonomic tools and architecture designed for producing specific work.  

I think that's key: while software is indefinitely powerful, it is most useful when it can be used to produce work that is not source itself (this is why software like blender may be justified in its system complexity, because it is code designing software, software as an interface to design virtual work that would otherwise be impossible if represented as substrate source (this was the conclusion I proposed in the original discussion...  it's about ergonomic interfacing!))

Source code on the other hand, is the driver of the software; it is something like the proto-smith; a generalized assembly of tools without specific ontological arrangement... of which the baseline is construction of more capable tools, to eventually construct specialized tools... it's like "The Ontological series of tools and instruments."

UNIX/GNU (somewhat) implement this philosophy in its design: tools like "cat" and "id" serve tiny specialized purposes (somewhat generalized...) but when composed together, they produce more sophisticated specializations... especially when scripting.

We see this phenomenon in GUIs vs. CLIs too...
and outside of the virtual world, I believe this appears in architecture. the analogy I presented over the forge vs. tools is helpful to explain what I'm observing and why it matters to understand! (because each technology, tool, instrument and environment, serves a specific function of human extension. I believe understanding the use of each tool, is critical when sophisticated problems arise; or understanding of tools and problems must also be sophisticated!)
- Software is the environment, like a blacksmith.
- Source is the base function of construction, like the tools that construct a blacksmith 
- Interfaces are specialized layers...

in the virtual space, a CLI is incredibly fast, both from me human user perspective, and the engineering process. however, once we add graphical interfaces, the complexity of the system increases drastically, to produce the exact same function as source... this explosion and complexity must be justified by the ergonomics added by the interface...
