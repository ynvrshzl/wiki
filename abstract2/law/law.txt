someone says, I did not ask to be here. 

law responds: "neither did the victims, neither did the crimes, neither did the problems, but who is going to solve them?"

the person said: "it would have been better if there were no problem at all..."

law becomes frustrated and angry, because such a statement is irrefutable, and a non falsifiable, masquerading itself as a parent truth... and so instead of acting irrationally, law takes a deep breath and looks towards within, to find an answer...

"when we left for ourselves, life is empty and meaningless. when we live for others, to serve, to protect, life's cruelty is justified in the pursuit of Justice... the crime already exists, the problem already exists, it is up to us to respond."

the person is silent, I reflecting on laws words of wisdom...

"maybe it gives you reason, but it makes me miserable. why do people do so terrific things to each other? how come some people suffer so much more than others? how come nobody does anything about it? why does nothing ever change?"

law nearly breaks down because those words carry humanity suffering between the spaces...

"crime solving gives my life reason, because when criminals are brought to justice, the world is a better place. perhaps we will never solve all of life's problems, but perhaps that is why we are  stronger than isolated, than the sum of our parts. perhaps it is unity that gives meaning to the suffering..."

"have you ever seen a person rescued? do you know what I see when I look into the eyes of a person? I see gratitude. perhaps that shock of life is truly the answer... they are not miserable they suffered, they are grateful they survived."
