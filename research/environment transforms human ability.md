the medium could be considered the environment. medium absolutely transforms capability on expression and even shaping the way we approach creating things. so you could consider mediums like different languages. they all Express basically the same thing which is life, but they do so with almost completely different methods. however each language follows rules and syntax and these are essentially the complex abstract models that define a language. 

and so we were thinking about this, practically speaking, imagine a musician learning to play the piano. we have two options here, we can learn with a visual piano roll or we can learn to read sheet music. and learning to read sheet music, is arguably more complicated and time-consuming but it's a language and opens the door to new expressive potential. almost like quite literally expanding your lens of capability. 

and so if you learn to read sheet music instead of learning from a piano roll, this fundamentally changes how you think about music and how you express music and how you learn music 

and so we were observing this in our own life in fact, and realizing that writing in GitHub versus obsidian versus vs code, those are all fundamentally the same language, which is markdown. but the expressive potential is absolutely different and it feels completely different to work in those environments even though they are essentially doing the same thing which is giving you tools to edit markdown files. they do so and it drastically different ways. and instead of dissecting methods, we want to focus more on the conceptual model, the idea that medium transforms capability. and so what does this mean? perhaps it's good to experiment with different mediums and art forms and to try things from different domains, *with different languages and environments...*
