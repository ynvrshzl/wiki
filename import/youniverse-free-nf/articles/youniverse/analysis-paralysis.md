# Analysis paralysis
Let's consider analysis-paralysis (AP) to be like a train at a freight stop. There are many tracks you could switch to, but you dont know where to go. You can't explore all of them, and sometimes, you can't change your mind once you've picked a direction.

A common issue in struggling with AP is hoarding. It is a psychological symptom of something deeper. AP is not your fault. AP is not a problem in your brain. It is infact a sign you are more careful than the average person, that is something to revel in.

# Writing as a tool
Writing is powerful. It is the written form of human language. Our language, is perhaps, the most important tool we have. Language is the bridge between our abstract human experience & the tangle.

**Writing vs Action:** how much should you write to yourself, and how much should you share with others? What is worth writing about, and what should be just acted on?

Naturally, if you dedicate most of your time & energy into something meaningful to you, all of these questions will answer themselves. You'll know exactly how much you need to keep to yourself, and how much to share.

### Writing vs Action
What's the purpose of writing? To express your thoughts on paper? To preserve your expression, memories, thoughts? To encode meaning? To compress? To bridge the abstract human experience, into a tangible form?

For whatever reason, I enjoy writing becuase I get to see visible progress, and feel it at the same time. It's both short-term, and long-term. But too much wriitng, leads to clutter, so what's the magic balance?

**I have a simple rule of thumb:** If I write something to myself, It will be to help me act in my life. If I write something that will help someone else, it will be to share & preserve things.
