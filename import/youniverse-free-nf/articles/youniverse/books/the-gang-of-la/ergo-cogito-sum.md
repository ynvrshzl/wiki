I remember when we would steal food from the town court... was it wrong to take away from others — **hell no.** I remember we would dress up in masks & pretend we were the superheroes of the city, we would pretend to have magical elements and fight the evil doers. We were going to build a world without evil.

Until I met face-to-face with "the real world."

Mary looks at the child, his arms as thin as the twigs on the forest floor. She looks at me emotionlessly: "I told you this world is broken." I didn't want to believe it at first... I didn't want to accept that life was suffering... but here I was, standing infront of someone who never asked to be here... come to think of it... it would have been better to have never been born at all.

But I can't... just stay that. Becuase that would be neglecting all the beautiful things I have felt.

She wispers to me: *"You're too much of a poet Eric, wake up. This world is broken."*
