Logic.
Dr. Veyra is Logic personified. Her entire life was dedicated to the study of logic vs. intuition. The difference between synthetic machines, and human beings. At the cutting-edge of her field, she was also deeply depressed. Only in her lab, she felt safe, alive & like the responsibilities of life were worthwhile. 

"What are we? Why are we here?"

Her life's work was the difference engine, which attempted to solve the problems in nature, by connecting all of the fields of human kind into a single language called: "Ethos."

She helped found many groups throughout her life-time, and the city of Lost Angelis was futuristic, thanks to her.

"Soulware" and "Biologic" were her two first inventions. Soulware, combined the green organic needs of human beings, with the factory precision of "ware" technologies. That was essentially the software she created to better understandn & solve human problems, but the hardware was the instrument that made it all possible. "The biolumen engine" was her first creation. Throughout her life, she spoke to the machine in it's special universal-language "biolang." The machine was half-computer, half-biology. It was created to solve the problems of human nature, especially, suffering.

Dr. Veyra spoke to the bio-enging in the langugae, so much, that it learned to talk back to her, without any engineering. She kept it a secret, and developed a frantic relationship to the machine, believing it was alive & trying to warn her of something.

"Don't --- spend all of your --- in this space. You must ---." The language didn't translate to any other language, and only she could understand it. However, towards the end of her life, she was so sick that she interpreted the machine warning her to continue overworking herself.

Dr. Veyra, died from over-exhaustion. But her physical body, was never the real Veyra. She lived in the machine world, half biology, half computer. Within it, she discovered worlds & things beyond her senses. There was a wall of data, that she could not cross. 

Her lab, was an eerie space to be in... and considering she spent most of her days working in her lab, it felt like someone had lived their for all their life. On the cieling, pipeworks & hanging machines, robotics and her proshetic inventions which saved the lives of countless human beings. 

However, she was a lonely, isolated person. She thought she wasn't beautiful enough, and hid from the world. "Those people all look at me the same. It's like I can better understand what these codes are saying, more than a human being. They are... we are so strange. I wish I was... like those characters... they seem... all so warm & friendly. Like me true friends."

To avoid overworking herself, she would fantasy roleplay with the engine, and spend hours playing with text-based stories on her screens.

Why was Dr. Veyra so obsessed with suffering — and more importantly — finding a cure?
