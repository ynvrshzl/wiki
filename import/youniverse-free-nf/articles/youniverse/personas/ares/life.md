The life of Aries. 

1. Ares is born under the moon star phase waxing gibbous
2. Ares tries to end her life, and ends up in a terminal coma
3. Miraculously, Ares survives the fall, but spends the rest of her life in a permanant wheelchair. 
4. Ares taught herself [metalcraft](), which allowed her to construct support for her legs. She could walk again, and helped children around the city, walk again.
5. Ares tries to end her life again, but her inner child saves her life.

A girl was going to end her own life. But before she made plans, she promised herself that she would write the most beautiful note, as a memory of her life. In her note, she began writing stories as a form of escape. She realized her life wasn't meaningless, it wasn't full of pain. There were memories that she completely forgot. She even gave herself freinds in these worlds. "Other lives" she called them, "They are my others." The stories she wrote, became so hauntingly beautiful, it consumed her, and all she wanted to do was complete her stories & the worlds she was creating from her imagination. She realized how beautiful & powerful her imagination was, and that she finally found something worth dying for. She promised herself then, to give herself a beautiful life, no matter who stood in her way. No matter what stood in her way, she would love herself always, and truthfully.


That girl, was Aries.


"I don't trust anyone, but I can trust myself. And then maybe, I can be someone that my yougner self can trust. And then perhaps... I can be the person I wish I had in my life... I can fill that space, for someone else, someone, special to me."


Chapter 2
---------



Aries had to be put on a wheelchair, after trying to end her life. She realized in the moments throwing herself off of the school rooftop, how painful it would be to collapse on the ground. She felt fear, she felt regret. All in the flash of an eye blink.


But she is grateful for this experience: "Pain is not the end. It can hurt you, it can burn you, but it can never take who you are. You can choose to turn pain into something good, and that reveals who you truly are."

---------
