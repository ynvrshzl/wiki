A man approaches Yami: "We're living in the end times. Don't worry, I know how to survive." That man, was Dawn Zen. A beacon of hope for the youth.

Dawn spoke to Yami, as they watched the orange skies fade to blue: "Some are terrified to lose their lives, and others throw their lives away. Do you ever wonder why that is?" Yami wispered, grievngly: "I wish I knew why. It... it will be a tragedy when you're no longer here." Dawn looks at Yami: "We will meet each other again. Everything will come back to time. That is the order of the stars."

Dawn cried with Yami. "Even my heart of iron weeps for the lost youth. You do not understand what I feel... my body feels the weight of the earth... and yet... I came from the dirt below... and the skies above. We all did. All of us are connected... we are all equal... and yet... even my eyes of an old soul can see clearly... none of us are equal." **Yami quietly says:** "I just wish... I had been born in a world without so much... so much pain... and people who... have walked on me my entire life... I... So many children without food... so many... unfair, cruel things I've seen... I... I just want to do something good in this world... something... that made my life worth having lived."

"There is always a push and a pull you always let go of something in order to receive another. that is the law of equal exchange, but my friend, even in the universe, laws will be broken."

"I've seen leeches, worms, maggots and parasites eating things from the inside out... but nothing in this world is more disgusting, than human greed."

Yami asked: "Dawn, why do they hate you?" Dawn replies: "This is why." He submerged himself in a water tank & showed to Yami how even though his body was begging for air, he could keep his mind calm & govern his body. He left the tank barely a live, his eyes grey, his skin pale. "We are more than these bodies my friends, we are the eternal consciousness born from the stars... and someday, we will return to their ashes."
