When tools become exhausting:
If tools become exhausting to maintain rather than helping us save time & energy, then you have to take a step away from the tool & ask yourself core querstions like a detective:

1. Is this tool helping me stay on path with my life meaning?
2. Is this helping me reach my long-term life goals?
3. Am I actually using the things I'm creating?
4. How much time does this save me?

### Simplicity -> emerges complexity -> complexity allows us to solve more real-world problems

The design of obsidian, markdown, html or whatever source-code tool is simple:

If used properly, basic **file, folders, links & queries** can be assembled like **logic gates** to solve real, complex problems in your life. That's the tricky part, becuase there are **so many ways you can arrange the parts.** Only a few are useful, most, are just noise. It's like if you took a bunch of logic gates & stuck them together, you'd probably end up with broken, random circuits most of the time. Or like smashing a piano, hoping for a beautiful melody. It seems that for every craft to be harmonious, there has to be a season of very slow, patient progress, where you learn to play with small pieces. Then slowly, work your way up to bigger parts. The more complexity you can handle, the more oppurtunities you have. Think of beautiful melodies, new genres waiting to be created, unique ways to use colors. This is where your lens of life expands, happiness, joy, meaning, fulfillment **and that's what makes life worth living.**

it's also a paradox: you're designing the tool you're also testing. so it's like you switch between writer-mode and reader-mode. it's very tricky to not get stuck in writer mode, becuase the tool is so tempting to just build things with.
