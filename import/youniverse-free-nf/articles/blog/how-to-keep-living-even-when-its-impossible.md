# How do you keep living, when you're drowning?

**This is why happiness seems fake, futile, and pointless.**

WHenever i'm deeply in a negative psychological cycle, it feels like it lasts eons. Perhaps, somewhere in my mind, time perception is so slow, becuase of how little I experience in my day-to-day life. It's a negative cycle fo ra reason: whenever I actually put in the effort to better my life, or do neglected things, my body screams with hesitation & denial. Almost like Gojo Satorou's special ability, where, the closer you get to him, the further you are from touching him. It feels that way alot for me, whenver I've spent some time viewing at all of the negative aspects of being a human being. 

I tend to easily forget the beautiful, euphoric, often, heavenly things I've experienced. Why? It isn't that those things aren't important to me, but in the dark, you can't see things without light. Those things aren't the light of my life, so what is? Perhaps it is my own will itself. Every single human being has a will of their own.


What does a human being do after surviving something horrible? Is there a reason to keep existing, even after so much pain? What does it mean to be a human being? 


For the longest time, these questions have been shadowing my every breath. I can't seem to enjoy the things I used to do, I don't see the world the same way, and I know I never will. But like Eren Yaeger, we keep moving forward!

Comedy, games, arts, helps me to stay alive during the most emotionally painful moments of my life.


Learning to detect bullshit
---------------------------


There are kinds of pain that is impossible to describe or imagine, this is where I stop writing, becuase it can cause harm to anyone reading this. Irresponsible human beings have influenced my thoughts & beliefs about life. It is absolutely crucial to be careful with the knowledge you share with others.


I've learned that... you can't solve problems in your life using your head. From what I've learned, I have to learn to work in unison, both my body & my mind.



There is pain so deep, it reaches to the bottom of my sea. And yet, that's the only place where you shine at your brightest.


I ask myself, when I reach that freedom. Lifestyle, financial freedom, healthy diet, a loving partner, a cozy
spacious city skyscraper apartment, finding a therapist that actually cares, maybe even children someday, I ask
myself, what will that feel like? Will I have been so broken by these years that I could never experience true
happiness, joy, or even feel human ever again?


I've heard music that is so beautiful, i've had beautiful dreams & visions here inside my room, and so I tell
myself: My beautiful, it is normal to be afraid, but don't let it lie to you. You have to go out there and show
it that it was wrong. 


I have decided to stop living my, sheltered life and explore what is outside these walls that have been
created for me.Abigails Window
