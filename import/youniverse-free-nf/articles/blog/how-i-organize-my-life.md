### Tools are not the stone
I'm grateful for my tools — **but they aren't my master.** I am the sculptor, they are the wedge. My goal is refining the stone, not my tools. 

If i woke up tomorrow with a hard-drive error & lost all of my files, I wouldn't end my life, because my meaning exists in simply being myself, I contain all of these things,they are part of me, but not my whole. "Losing" the externalized versions of my internal worlds is the same as a building collapsing, it may feel permanant, but remember, **what matters most is that nobody died, only the structure did.** 

We can recover things, [even impossibly complex things](), but a human life, you can never bring back once they are gone.

I'm working on externalizing what's in my head, so I can see the important parts & try to make sense of this, so that I can move forward in my life & heal & accomplish what is keeping me alive. let me guide you through my guiding obsidian vault (i'm meta guiding you!)

Concept:
I essentially treat my obsidian vault as an operating system organize for my life. My permanent storage. I think of my daily waking mind like RAM, good for in-the-moment access, but lose ideas if not loaded into a source of persistent storage. (like a computer)

Structure:
My obsidian vault has an intuitive folder structure that I've developed after years of stress, frustration & love & passion! It's a pretty simple but scalable system, since I can easily input/output files & find things, which is the purpose of a vault system, to work, not be pretty. If it's pretty, that's a bonus. I mean, just take a look at my obsidian theme, i'm happy with it, and I made it feel like my own personal browser for my thoughts! (which is in essence what obsidian is)

Navigation:
Obsidian opens by default with my file "Home.md" which is a grid of "Headspaces." I used to just show all top-level links (therapy, recycle bin, inbox, etc. this clutters contexts & essentially, is the same thing as the file-tree. It looks aesthetic but non-functional for separating contexts in large systems like mine.) The home page is like my own personal explorer, it's like an interface for my obsidian vault, so I never get lost in the sidebars with too many contexts, I have images, titles & descriptions for each area and aesthetically arranged as a grid card. Very practical & scalable, it turns my system of files into a living breathing masonry, with eco-systems of links, folders, files & queries. It's taken alot of years for me to get to this point where my tools are helping me, rather than wasting my time tinkering. The way I got to this point was by stepping back from the tool & asking myself the big-picture orienting questions: "What am I struggling with? Can I write it down?"

 So for headspaces rather than tinkering & adding things that seem useful (believe me, i've learned my lesson!) I ask myself outside of the computer: "What am i struggling with in my life? Can I write about it? can it fit into a logically larger area?" Essentially this the thought processs behind everything I write. I ask myself: "If future me doesn't benefit from this, it's a ephemeral/temporal note, and I will discard it once I'm done using it for externalizing. like scratch paper.)
