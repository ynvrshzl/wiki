# How I survived depression

One of the biggest things that helped me recover my health was novelty. Having new experiences seriously escalated my recovery.

Everyday is a battle in my head. Souldiers die on both sides of the battlefield. Sometimes, the my side wins, other times the depression immobilizes my souldiers. But everyday we wake up, there is a new chance for perception, knowledge, wisdom, etc.

# Novelty

The biggest thing that saved my life was change. The David Bowie song? Changes? Yeah. That song still slaps. I'm discovering aswell, not everything in life has to have a grand significance... Life is complicated enough. Our bodies try to simplify things as much as possible... why get in the way?

For some of us, Depression is a once-in-a-lifetime experiene. For some of us, it's a cycle, coming & going, sometimes, coming back like an enraged maelstrom robbing you of your emotions, and other times, it's gentler. There is only one way that I have survived depression: by using weapons & tools, as if my life were a video game. **Let me share with you what saved my life.**

Rules I live by: Never compare your pain to others. Would you compare someone's happiness to your own? No. That doesn't make any sense! We are all unique biological ecosystems, each of us processes the world differently. That means that we each experience pain & happiness, differently from everyone else.

There is no fixed answer, but there is a process. Let's explore my process together:

It's the most cruel thing in this world to monetize the suffering of others. This is why I believe therapy is such a sad part of recovery. It's like people tell you what's wrong with you, but not how to improve your life. Remember, the most important part of healing from depression is actualy wanting to heal in the first place.


For me, what saved my life was the key in depression: Do I actually believe life is worth living? To me, that was the most imprtant question, and it's so important infact, that it's the question I return to everytime I feel hopeless or lost in my life. It's like home. Do I believe life is worth living? Yes. From there, I asked myself alot of questions. The same way that a therapist would interrogate a person. Can you see a life after depression? Can you see a life outside the walls? A life where you are in control? A life that is worth living? Someone worth dying for? A cause worth giving your life to? Okay genius, but what does that actually mean? When I'm suffering in deep pain & the abyss inside me is swallowing me up, and nobody seems to get it, what the hell do I actually do?! Emotional detachment is also a poweful weapon against depression.


The shortest answer is: [I realized how precious life is.](#being-grateful-to-be-alive)


When you have a reason to suffer, suddenly you stop becoming a punching bag, and you realize you will fight for what it is you love, **even if it kills you.** If you have something to die for, you have something to live for. There are a million reasons to end your life, but all you need is one reason to stay alive.


A very important shift in my perspective was looking at my life like I was the first-person player of my video game. The author to my own story. 


When I changed the way I look at depression, mental illness, and pain in life, I began to realize I have much more control than I thought. While I am isolated in my life, watching youtube videos, [Ted X talks](#my-favorite-ted-x-talks) and therapists, equipped me with the technical knowledge, and most importantly: that I was not the only human being to experience life this way.


# Macro micro theory
**This sounds overly technical (which i find that absolutely cool) but at a basic level, this is all about how big you're thinking.** If you zoom out to the grand scale — universe, entropy, death, suffering — then yes, existence can seem like a net-negative, especially through a pessimistic or anti-natalist lens. On that scale, individual joys may feel meaningless.

But scope changes perspective:

Zoom in: a hug, a piece of music, a sunrise, a laugh with someone you love — these moments matter immensely, even if they don’t “add up” to a cosmic victory.

Zoom out: everything fades. Including suffering. Including joy.

So you have to choose your zoom level wisely — not ignore the larger picture, but not get swallowed by it either.
