Essentially, there exists a kind of pain that no philosophy, or belief, or habit can cure. 
A pain that makes you beg for death. And if you've ever experienced severe trauma, you understand that there is literally nothing you can do to change the past. Yet it still haunts me today. The only answer I've found to not only reaching these levels of suffering, but also surviving them, is by making myself forget it ever happened.

That feels cruel. Becuase it's almost like letting the trauma get away with what it robbed me of, but I see it as my only thread that I can hang onto, if I really love something about this life.

Asfar as I've learned, love is the only reason to continue existing. Without it, everything else is just coping from pain. Human life, itself, can be considered suffering. We are the only creatures burdened with the knowledge of our own mortality, and our consciousness.

Thats' why I believe it is love itself, the only thing can keep a human being sane. Even during the most painful, torturous moments in our existence.

I used to think that this was just me having wishful thinking, but then I saw real signs of life, that I found something fundamentally true about us human beings.

A soldier will sacrifice their own body to save someone else's life. A nurse will deprive themselves of sleep, to care for others. A firefighter will rush into flames, to save a child. These are the people that keep our world from collapsing under the immense suffering & pain that decays everything, just beneath the surface of our daily lives, superficial conversations.

Yes, pain gives a source of meaning to life, but what's the point of meaning itself? That's when you ask the final, atomic question, and I had to learn that if I kept questioning further, I would go completely insane by disintegrating my psyche.

There is a spectrum of pain. On one end, there is pure joy, ecstasy, happiness, euphoria. It's like that stupid saying goes: "We're high on life man!" However, on the other end, there exists pain so terrible that you beg for it to stop, and immediately, death seems like the only way it'll stop. An escape. But is it really an escape? Does the pain really stop at death? We have no idea what happens after death, we only believe that we've been told by the natural sciences & what our eyes tell us.
