# Gratitude, and the invisible miracles in everyday life.

Here are some of the thoughts that keep me up at night. It's like a voice in my head, perhaps, the real, subconscious me.

_"People... most people are so ungrateful. It hurts me so much. Do most people have any Idea how lucky they are? WHy is it that we waste the gifts we've been given? THings that our ancestors could not even dream of. We take for granted so many liberties sacrificed for. We have no idea how deep or long the lineage of human suffering spans, just to be able to make our own choices. I often complain about money, society, and the flaws of the modern world, but when I study history & remember how bad things really were, I am shocked into immediate gratitude, and honestly, it's painful & unbearable at times, to even begin to comprehend how lucky I am, and how many tiems I've just taken my life for granted. To me, I believe we have so much to be unbearably grateful for... it is one of the most horrific tragedies of life to take it for granted, while someone wishes they could have had your life."_

Being grateful for the tiny, invisible miracles in my life, like just being able to breathe, or not have chronic unbearable pain... I take that for granted. THe problem with my thoughts above isn't that they are wrong... they just assume that "people" are the problem, when in reality, it isn't "people" but our perspectives that govern our perception of existence.
