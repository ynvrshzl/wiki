# Is life worth improving?
If you study history, even for a couple of hundred years, you can see the destructive nature of human beings on the planet...  *(Insert statistic here.)* But what seems more ominous is the apparent curse of futility: whenever we advanace as a civilization, we do more harm to the enviornment.

So the question is posed: is it even worth living your life, to improve the world? Regardless of my beliefs on this subject, I realize we are operating on the wrong scope: [this is the wrong question, and wrong questions can be fatal.](asking-the-wrong-questions.md)

# Signs & evidence 
In [software](software-engineering.md), and even nature itself, there is an incredible ocncept that proves to me, that no effort is futile, in changing a system as big as the world we all share. Fault tolerance describes how much and what kind of errors, damage, pain and irregularities a system can take, before it is destroyed. In [software](software-engineering.md), this is why engineers prepare for erorrs by using [error checking]()
