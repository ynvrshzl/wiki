title: My arguments against solipsism
---

Solipsism is the belief that nothing in reality can be proven to be absolutely true, except yourself. And even then, you don't know if you really are "real." This may sound, even, comical to someone who has never considered this... but if we sit down for a moment, you begin to realize the terrifying possibility that all of this, could have been a simple dream. My argument against Solipsism, and any other non-falsifiable theory, is this, when we tend to ruminate on the negative aspects of life, there ceases to be a reason to continue existing. Therefore, if there is any reason to be found in existence, it must be found outside of the negative aspects of existence. When we immediately jump to negative possibilities: it fools us to believe these are the only real possibilities. We forget that just as much as Solipsism or Nihilism could be true, we could just as easily switch it around and look at the positive aspect of believing these things. What if, life isn't meant to be understood? Or ponder to the stars about meaning? What if you have already been given so many wonderful gifts? What if we are experiencing [spiritual amnesia?](#spiritual-amnesia)

To me, Solipsism is a perfect example of asking the wrong questions in life.
