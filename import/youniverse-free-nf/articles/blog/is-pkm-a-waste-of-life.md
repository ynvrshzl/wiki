title: Is PKM (Personal knowledge management) a waste of time?

**SUMMARY:** if I wouldn't share something with others, it's not worth keeping.
 



 I've realized over the course of my teenage years and early adulthood, **there is no formula for anything
 in
 life.** Life is
 so impossibly complex, that our bodies have been forced to adapt to life. Within us we have systems that
 give us
 everything we need to simply just exist.
 



 Survival is not only for the stereotypical predator in the wild, but just think about all of the harmful
 microbes &
 bacteria that exist on the outside world. Our bodies have amazing little workers that defend our insides, to
 make
 sure the outside won't harm our sensitive internal workings.
 



 We also have system that can adapt: they actively change and adapt to meet the requirements of our lives.
 This
 is
 why I believe now in
 doing. That intuition will always surpass intelligence. I believe now that you should trust your own body to
 take
 care
 of what is most important.
 



 I get it — brains were not designed to retain vast amounts of information sometimes ideas get lost
 sometimes
 thoughts get
 discarded sometimes memories get replaced. Personally I'm okay with this because it shows that there's a
 deeper
 fundamental issue in your life if you seem to be forgetting a lot of important things you could have a
 health
 issue
 or
 it could be your lifestyle or you could be putting too much expectations on yourself. For whatever reason
 your
 brain
 is
 not able to retain the things that you are consciously assigning high value to. I get it. Following a PKM
 productivity
 system seems like the right decision at first.
 



 But it's just a Band-Aid over a wound that you won't be able to ignore
 forever. That's something I would tell my past self. Where are all of those hours going to? All of that time
 setting
 up
 your system personalizing your space I get it you wanted to feel personal you wanted to feel personalized to
 you
 so
 it's
 not a proprietary box but something that is limitless and fits to your needs. I get it. This is what made
 PKM so
 appealing to me. I remember the original reason I got into obsidian was because I was searching for a note
 taking
 tool
 that could help me organize and formats the mass amounts of notes that I was taking. I tried so many options
 from
 the
 early days of zotto notebooks to random note taking applications, Google docs OneNote I was searching for a
 flashy
 solution essentially.
 



 I ended up forgetting that What mattered most to me was recording my thoughts my ideas my beliefs
 my daily reviews etc the most important elements of my life things that I certainly did not want to forget.
 So I
 understand in this case that recording parts of your life in today's busy technological world, is
 inevitable. It
 seems
 like things are getting worse, with the rise of AI and further technologization of our human species. But it
 also
 makes
 me realize this is all still human. Human beings created all of this.
 If you're a person that's not concerned about making the most of your limited time and energy as a human and
 you
 want to
 actively put in extra work to manage a system of arbitrary information, then remember it is your life you
 choose
 how
 you
 spend it. But after all the pain I've been through I realize they're only very few things in life that
 really
 deserve
 your time and energy. We live in a time where there are so many diverse sources of distraction, that's
 trying to
 solve a
 technologically, could only further add to the stress and frustration. This is why I've learned to detach
 from
 the
 technological world and come back into the physical world. I've noticed the most amount of improvement in my
 life
 learning how to balance technology with my intuitive human physical needs.
 I can remember my earliest memories with obsidian. I was so excited by the possibilities of note-taking. I
 got
 introduced to the world of PKM early on. I saw how psychology students neuroscientists authors creatives
 CEOs
 and
 generally hyper- productive people, we're using applications like obsidian and notion organized their busy
 lives.
 This
 seemed like the missing piece in my life. I was finally going to fix my life and stop wasting my youth.
 Little
 did I
 know these tools were the ultimates procrastination station, and a massive source of emptiness unfulfillment
 and
 overwhelm in my life.
 Obsidian introduced me to web development. I had no idea that you could write JavaScript inside your notes
 and
 it
 would
 actually do stuff. This idea has been around for quite some time with Jupiter notebooks, but this was so
 much
 more
 approachable.
 The reason I'm writing about this is because I want to share this publicly on my website.
 Essentially I am moving my privates obsidian personal knowledge Management nonsense, and making it public
 for
 people
 to
 actually use. This way it's useful. However going back and reading almost 20,000 notes is not going to be an
 easy
 task.
 Especially since I create a lot of content everyday. I can't imagine the amount of hours I've spent at
 obsidian
 trying
 to make myself feel like I was making progress in my life. Being productive and taking hundreds of notes
 everyday.
 It
 actually hurts. But I'm happy to move forward in my life. **If the pain is part of showing me that, then
 it's
 all
 worth
 it.**
 Obviously, this depends on how you define wasted time. Objectively, if a system requires more work to
 maintain,
 that
 means it's adding more work to your life. Living with chronic burnout, frustration, emptiness could simply
 becaused
 by
 the little actions we take every day. My personal experience with PKM systems is heavily biased with my
 extreme
 decline
 in mental and physical health. This is my personal experience in my conclusion and I hope this helps someone
 who's
 going
 through the same thing I was. Trying to manage overwhelm in your life information overload, projects, ideas.
 If
 I
 don't
 have a PKM system how am I supposed to manage all these things in my life? For me personally I realized if I
 wouldn't
 share something publicly then it's not worth spending my time and energy on. This is because I realized the
 precious
 nature of my life, it is short and I have to spend it on things that matter most to me. I can't afford to
 let my
 days
 pass with little meaningless activities that all regret in the end of my life if I keep living this path. So
 because
 it's not too late, I'm changing my trajectory now and **I'm stopping this obsession with personal management
 once
 and
 for all!**
 This also depends on your brain type. If you find yourself to have a perfectionistic obsessive qualities to
 your
 personality, it's probably best to avoid any notes taken. The problem that I face was formatting my notes
 linking my
 notes and the General Lee obsessive writing
 Has a conclusion, I will be deleting all of my past notes and posting stuff on my website so that I only
 work on
 things
 that are meaningful and impactful to others and I don't digitally hoard.
 If you're worried about losing your knowledge or information remember, you're going to lose knowledge that
 you
 don't
 use. Even if you haven't written down my perfectly set up if you never use the knowledge you won't remember
 it.
 I'm noticing that in the PKM community there seems to be this obsession with productivity and efficiency and
 I
 see
 people coming up with crazy systems and designs and it's so unfortunate because from the big picture from my
 perspective
 I can see that they're just complicating something so simple which their brain already naturally does. All
 of
 our
 brains
 naturally do this regardless of your neurodivergence or uniqueness. All of our brains are designed to learn
 and
 retain
 knowledge that we actively use in our life. This means that the brain is already the most effective storage
 source
 because it only retains what is important. Ask compared to a digital storage source which you can dump on
 infinite
 amount of knowledge in and end up creating more work in your life to manage a system that you won't even
 use.
 I ask myself this question now: *Should I get rid of notes entirely?* Very rarely in my life do
 I
 ever
 completely ban something. But I have a rule: **if it is negatively impacting my life, I cut it off immediately. I've heard time & time again,
 especially
 from
 elder folks, life is too short and regrets hurt more than death.**
