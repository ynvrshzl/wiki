# Historical versions of this website

The Youniverse website has gone through many versions:

### 2025-03-01
This website started as a public wiki. A recreation of Obsidian (Personal knowledge management program) and made the most important pages (Blog, Youniversity, Youniverse)

### 2025-06-06
This version had an emphasis on images. It resembled a social media app: (Inspired by Instagram, Sora, ChatGPT). It looked great on both computers & phones. It felt professional with a very complex settings system, that would let you save & load settings for the site. I used this to make preferences, fonts, and even a theme system. But things became out of hand, when I lost sight of the vision: I wanted you to read content, not waste time on another brain-rot platform. This website was just a way of me sharing my writings publically, just incase I die or something (lol).

### 2025-06-17
After this version was archived, I began development on a cleaner version of the website. This time, I kept the emphasis on images to capture your attention, but then the readable content, contained the meaning & purpose of my work. This website was the most complex, with an even more minimal & clean UI. It had a custom cursor, and even sounds (which is a rare thing in websites.) This website became so complex, that maintaining the codebase became impossible, as I struggled to understand the code I was creating. Especially going back & forth, trying to find the "best" look. I decided that I was exhausted of making things look & feel good, I wanted something that completely pushed me out of my comfort zone.


### 2025-06-20
The website became a more experimental playground. I used a video-game aesthetic (Inspired by dark souls, path of exile & diablo 3) to give the website a game-like feel. It had a black background & glowing RPG text. It even had a custom cursor that would react to specific elements. Eventually, I had to scrap this design, becusae I wanted something that was timeless & could stretch between different stories & themes.

### 2025-08-11
This time, I was going through extreme depression, and my physical health was deteriorating. But I had just one more idea to try out, before I completely gave up on this website. 

This is where the website became at it's most complex — a virtual desktop. Inspired by MacOS & Linux, it was a completely interactive desktop, with custom little apps, folders & files. Each button had a purpose, and would allow me to explore the website's many files, in a desktop aesthetic. But things quickly became static, after realizing mobile users cannot access this site, and to me, the vision was always to make the reading content front-most. 


### 2025-08-12
This is where the website is at today
