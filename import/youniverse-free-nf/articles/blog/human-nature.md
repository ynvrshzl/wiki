# Human nature

### "When I feel happy, it feels pointless... I've seen so much suffering, happiness feels pointless to me. Especially knowing it won't last, but the pain, feels like it never ends."

Do you struggle with this too? These are some of the thoughts I get, especially when recovering from depression.

I want to break these thoughts down, not so that I can interrogate or challenge them, but so I can see how I really feel on the inside. That's somethign that could help you too, whoever may read this in the future.

The first part, "happiness feels pointless." I ask myself, why? How come when I'm in a good, joyful mood, and it's dream-like to just enjoy being alive, there's no pain in my body or brain, so why am I reminded of the suffering i've endured? Perhaps it's part of me making sure I never forget the impossible things I've survived, at times where I couldn't see any way out. Escape seemed impossible, from depression that kept cycling, to my failed attempts at bettering my life. 

Lack of emotion (anhedonia) made this even worse, becusae you're desperate to feel when you've been numb on the inside for so long. It really does feel like you're dead inside, [like Eric always says.]()

I cringe being happy. especially seeing others happy. It's a trigger for all the horrible things I know. I could try remembering when this happens: what's my edge of chaos? What saved my life? What do I enjoy? Those things keep me alive, no matter how cringe they are, they are important to my cosmic existence. The silly stupid cringe things keep me sane so I can share my meaning with you.

Any time I am happy my brain reminds me how pointless this feeling is & how cruel life is to innocent children in the east. 

My response is: yes, happiness is like a leaf in a maelstrom when you see a child suffering or something awful. But the thing is, you're comparing two extremes:  the joy of your priveledged life and the horrific parts of someone else's existence.

Does it mean that you should ignore their pain? If I can't help someone, I am only making myself suffer. I can't save those children, and I can't end my life just becuase there is so much evil in this world that I will never even begin to wash away, even if I dedicated every breath of my life to helping people, evil will always exist.

So that logically means, none of your efforts will result in any meaningful change in life, so you should logically just die to avoid suffering further. That is the logical answer to so much complex computation suffering. But we aren't meant to live in the long scope of realiity: we are the only creatures burdened with a logical, machine-like brain *(intelligence)*, but the illogical, feeling-oriented body *(intuition)*

In short, my answer to the logical problem of excess suffering, is the illogical feeling of love.

Love yourself, love something in this life worth dying for. And if you can find any meaning whatsoever in helping others, doing what you enjoy, then you've found exactly where you belong, and dedicate your life like it's a movie. You know when you're off path, if you feel it. That's your body. Your compass. The logical mind helps you process, but it is the body that lets you experience & live.

Everything that I see, is a duality. I love the symbol of [yin and yang]().  The human experience is a duality itself, and consciousness is a double-edged blade, especially those of us that are burdened with overly-active imaginations, vivid inner-worlds, overwhelming ideas & thoughts. I believe that human beings are the only creatures that can & will solve the problems plaguing nature. I don't believe we'll ever reach a point where all evil & suffering is extinguished, becuase there will always be that one fool who's short-circuited & actually wants pain. Those are the people I wish never existed, but I don't want to place my anger in the existence of someone, becuase then I become resentful & my beautiful inner worlds rot away. So instead of hoping for all pain everywhere to end, I focus on what can I do about my pain?

Wabi-sabi is a japanese concept that also saved my life. It's about turning broken things into whole beautiful works of art, the cracks filled with gold. This almost seems like a fairy-tale when you examine the deepest suffering a human being can withstand. Like a parent losing their child, or a couple in love, and losing a partner.  And suffering also extends to all creatures, not just us human beings. So if you look at us through the lens of logic, we are causing terrible suffering. If a computer without the illogical human emotion, would examine us at a high-level, it would see us as a parasite. But if a machine with a human heart could see each individual, especially the people who give their lives to save others, then we human beings are no longer terrible forces of nature, but infact, expressions of the exitstence itself. 


### A moral responsibility to apply yourelf 
Don't let the soft wispers of nihilism fool you, while it feels futile to keep going, keep going becuase you're not aiming for the highest thing, that's not what I was designed for. 

A single human life can influence so much, in ways we cannot see. Most of our influence comes from simply being alive, and being yourself. That goes for the good & bad influence we carry through our lives.

### Microscope analogy: if you feel insignificant.
If you ever think your life is insignificant, remember that a grain of sand is nothing compared to the ocean itself, but to an ant, the grain of sand is a boulder like sisyphus. It really is about perception & perspective, switching between the differnet zoom layers of life. Never too zoomed out, or things feel insignificant, but never zoomed so far in, that we can only see the problems. 

When I focus what little energy this body can give me, on things that are actionable & meaningful, I start to feel alive & that's the change I really seeked. It wasn't saving this world from children that suffer, it was solving pain in my own existence so that I could have the space to start solving the problems outside of me.

I believe this is where I havea a moral responsibility, not only to myself, but to life itself, thtat I can't waste my consciousness or let these nihilistic, futility thoughts destroy my being, I must apply mysefl to the things I am capable of, in the hopes I can open up a pocket in the future, for bigger change to happen
