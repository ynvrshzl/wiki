# Things Minecraft taught me
These are things I/'ve learned from playing Minecraft. A seemignly, child-like sandbox,  could hold such depth & significance.

## Minecraft taught me to to live. 
Without a reason to play, the game is just a boring screen of meaningless pixels. But when you have a reason to play, suddenly, the world is shifted upsider down, and everything carries significance. It's like the quote: ["Either nothing matters, or everything matters."](matters.md)


## Attitude, perspective & perception are your superpowers. 

Switching the way you mentally see things. i'm currently enjoying role-playing a medievial playthrough. [TES IV: Oblivion](tes-4-oblivion) & [TES V: Skyrim](tes-5-skyrim) music in the background, medieval, fantasy buildings & shops. i turned a seemingly boring game, with nothing to do, into something that i already enjoy: medieval roleplay! roleplaying is the imagination of a child, so if you do it, you are controlling & immersing yourself in your own experience. if you apply this to your outside life, and treat your life like a video game, suddenly perspectives shift & everything has a reason.

Tou are the controller of your own experience. you give your self a reason to play & not quit.


## Combatting Burnout
When you are burned out, it's becuase you've done too much of the same thing without variety. the answer is to work in small steps & always keep yourself busy with passionate projeqts


## Time makes things significant
You have to put in some effort for your world to be worth coming back to. memories are built from experiences and this is one of the only games where you are in control of it.


## The reason
the hardest part is answering why? the reason you do things comes from why? what's the fucking point? if you enjoy it, and it gives you something to spend your time on, that to me is a good enough purpose to keep doing something.
