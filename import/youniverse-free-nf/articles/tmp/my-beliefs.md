I'm grateful to even be alive. To be relatively healthy physically, to be able to walk outside. To live in a 1st-world country. I can't unsee the trauma in my eyes of the things I've seen in this world, I can never forget what I've seen, and that's okay, I accept that. I've gone through so much pain, that I reached the other side of life. I reached a side of life that is so fragile & beautiful that it's honestly more painful that it is pleasurable, and again, I'm okay with this. Life is more than just experiences to me, life is everything happening right now, whatever you make of your life, is up to you. Like a blank canvas, we are all designers, but only few of us can paint the canvas with our own colors.


I believe that regardless of religion, we are all souls and we all come here to experience a specific life, like a story. We choose every relationship, situation, decision & event that will occur in our lives, but we choose to live without this knowledge, becuase that would ruin the experience of figuring it all out as you go. I don't know why I believe this so strongly, but it's the only thing that makes sense to me in this world.


I believe few human beings can change the world for good. My eyes have opened realizing that human beings create impossibly complex structures, digitally & physically. Quantum mechanics, gravity, string theory, chaos theory... and yet I don't understand how to kiss someone, *lol.*


I've survived depression. I've seen the cruelty of human beings & how horrible we can be to each other, animals,
and ourselves. While I can't be blissfully ignorant as I used to be, I realize that we can transform this, use
this darkness to make the light worth something. It's during the darkest times of my life that I need meaning
the most to give me a reason to keep living.


I believe in the power of the human being. The mind, the body, the greatest distance in the universe is the
distance between my head & my heart.


This world makes no sense to me. People make no sense to me. But I've learned by being a hermit, that the wisest lessons about what truly matters most in life, comes when you block the world around you, and listen to your body.
