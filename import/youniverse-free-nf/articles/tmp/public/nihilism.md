title: Nihilism & the answer to suffering
---


Is life ultimately meaningless? If it is... how come my brain always jumps to that being a bad thing? Think about it... if I see a beautiful girl or a lovely sunset, I don't have to carve at my insides, desperate for "meaning" — the question doesn't even exist when I'm occupied by beautiful things! Of course, this becomes complex, do material things make life beautiful? Are experiences the answer to suffering? One thing I've learned recently is there is no direct way of dealing with the horrifying nature of pain. The body will experience it 100%. There seems to be no way to detach from the pain. I've seen images of Bhuddist monks being burned alive, while they are meditating and I ask myself: How is that even possible? 



 Gratitude. Mindfulness. Awareness. Respect. These are the natural side effects of living a fulfilling human
 existence.
 The pursuit of our personal "feeling good" can only lead to misery. Inside our bodies, there are impossibly
 complex systems that require us to be useful, and working. It's almost as if we were designed to be
 designers. To create. To sustain. Not to indulge or consume.
 It's a truly empty existence when you only feel good. Eventually, you'll have to face the nature of death.
 That someday, you won't exist. That's what shook me to my core, and I stopped living my hedonistic,
 pleasure-driven lifestyle. I used to not care about death. *"Death? Ha. Dude... Who. Cares. Not me. I'm
 gonna be happy & enjoy my life as much as I've got time."* But I didn't realize
 



 Is there any hope left in today's world? If you look around you can see that civlization has continued to
 push in a direction of hedonism. We have mis-used our resources, technology & human capacities, to create a
 fantasy world of pleasure, hidden away from the scars of our human neighboors in 3rd world countries, and
 the animal life we are neglecting for profit.
 If we do not use our capacitiy for good, how will we prepare when mother Earth erupt volcanoes, or even
 asteroids?
 All of this is extremely existential. I wish somtimes, I would bury my head in the sand and just breathe a
 different life. That is why I escape into my stories, when things become too painful to bear on my
 conscious.
 The sheer weight of the suffering of everything we human beings have caused, sends me down a hopeless
 spirtal.
 Nature must have worked endlessly to have given us the ability of self-awareness. SOme of us, like myself,
 spend unhealthy amounts of time living inside their head, reather than experiencing life. That's something
 that I'm learning more & more, in my early adult years.
 But then, I realize, Hedonism itself isn't evil. Happiness, distractions, Joy, Pleasure, Birthday parties,
 Friendship, Love, Occupations. We don't have to try & save the world. Not even the greatest figures in
 history could do it. I know this makes me feel hopeless, and that's the beginning of true hope. You cannot
 hope for things that are non-sustainable. You have to realize you are human, and you need to be in the
 correct ecosystem in order to function healthily. Neglecting your health, ignoring your body, valuing work
 over your sanity, will only leave your reality in misery, becuase you will realize. I must start with
 myself. If enough people like me, focus on being the best versions of themselves, then we have a fucking
 chance at fixing this mess. Salvaging this hauntingly beautiful planet.
 It all fits into a balanced ecosystem. You must find that balance within yourself. A balance of pleasure,
 and discpline. A balance and external
 **Ultimately, I see life as Yin-yang, duality, balance.**
