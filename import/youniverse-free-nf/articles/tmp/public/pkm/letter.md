
"Don't fear losing or foegetting things, fear the unlived life."

The best way i've discovered of tangily (actually) doing this, is by compressing. The best way to compress meaning for me, is by being. Becoming the person that radiates that meaning. By reflecting in the things I actually do, not just write down.

So here's a design problem, what's the point of writing things?

If I would have shown these notes to my past self, it would have saved her so much stress, frustration & ultimately her time & energy. To spend it on the things that really matter to her. 

But that's the thing, there was no clear direction. No map or actionable step to take. It was worlds apart between who she wanted to be, and where she was. She wanted to do so many things, but her body was just barely trying to survive, in a world that made no sense.

And so, order came. If the world we live in, made it so difficult to find a reason to exist, then she would make it her mission, to help others find their reason to exist & better life.

What she needed was her own personal balance of chaos & order. A system that wasn't so time-consuming, but also provided clear daily direction on how to best spend her time.

And so, with a model in mind, came the testing phase. She began building little systems, some inspired by others, and a few, completely from her own imagination. What helped the most, was asking: "What do I actually need in my life?" **Never looking at the design, but rather, the source.**
