# The glass walls effect

This feeling is like a mixture of amnesia & regret, triggered by a deeply powerful sad nostalgia.

Have you ever had that shocking feeling, that you've been spending the last 6 months on something completely pointless? The worst part is, you're only aware of this when something reminds you. Sometimes I see something, smell a perfume, listen to a forgotten song, remember a lost memory. These little external things remind me of a space that has become foreign to me. A space I once spent so much of my life dreaming of, now forgotten, buried underneath the ashes of my failed projects & hollow relationships.

I start with this: I am grateful that this feeling is so painful. When you come back home, it feels like every part of your insides are torn apart, but it has to be that way: your insides are re-assembling themselves. 

What matters most in life, and you realize that you've been giving your everything to chasing this thing, that's one of the most defeating feelings I have ever experienced. It feels worse than dying, becuase you're still alive to experience it. IT feels like your dreams, your future self, family, love, life, is all slipping away from your fingers like sand, and you desperately try to catch this emotion before you forget about it again & repeat these soul draining cycles. I have been here many times. Infact, the only reason I'm writing this note is becuase I have finally been able to put into words what I have been unawaringly experiencing for the past 8 years of my life.


Imagine if you were to spend an entire lifetime on things that are utterly insignificant to you. There could be possibly, no pain more soul-crushing than the weight of regret. But this kind of regret, is the deepest: the regret of things that could have been.

 I've experienced this emotion time & time again, in my quest for meaning. Along the way, I've made bad choices. I've spent time on people, projects, things & thoughts, that really don't matter to me. I always find my way back home where I belong: **writing stories.**

How can we avoid locking ourselves in our own mind? Espceically when we are trapped in a circumstance that feels like it will destroy us. How can we avoid staying inside, and remember the freedom outside outside these walls. How can we remember that even in the darkest, most hopeless of moments, we have life, the collosal impossibility that is life?
