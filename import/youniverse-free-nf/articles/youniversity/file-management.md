# File management

Welcome to the class of file management, with your host, professor Hazl! In this class we'll be exploring the mystical world of digital files, and learning to apply these management principles to your life. Learn them once, they stay with you forever.

# Organizing vs. Access
When organizing our files, we need to remember they will be accessed. This is where the structure of our system, emerges.

# Type vs. Topic
We have 2 fundamental choices for how we structure our organizing & accessing: by category or by topic. For example, we could structure our files by images, videos, photos. This makes it simple to organize, but harder to access information. If we have topic-oriented needs, we can organize the images, videos & photos based on the subject, which is always harder to organize, but easier to access.

The tricky part is knowing when to create branches for categories or topics, inside of a bigger branch. This is entirely dependent on your needs. **The needs of your system, is what shapes it's structure.**
