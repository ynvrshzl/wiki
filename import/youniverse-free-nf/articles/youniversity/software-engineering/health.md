# Avoiding burnout, stress, overwhelm & mental health issues
Software engineering is an incredibly technical task, that's why it takes a specific kind of human being to be interested in the world of machines, and even moreso, to want to learn the language to speak with them.

What kind of person is software the right choice? If you're naturally curious about computers. Are you a detail-oriented person? Do you enjoy the feeling of coding itself? The tactile feel on your fingertips of the keyboard, as you watch stuff happen onscreen.

Rather than a specific guide, here is a framework I use to guard myself against the negative side-effects of software engineering.

1. Remember everything is a building block. If you treat a single block of code, a line, or even a character, 
2. [The goal is to learn, not to finish](learning-vs-finishing.md)
3. Don't stare at something for too long. Try to avoid spending too much time in one place.
4. Concept to method. The bridge between humans & machines is code. But what is the brdige between our natural language, and machine language? It is having a concept, an idea, or something that drives you to do something. Code is practically infinite and it is impossible to understand everything. That's why we have to purposefully limit ourselves to an idea, or a problem to solve. From the idea, you learn the many different ways to make it real.

#### Curiosity over frustration
