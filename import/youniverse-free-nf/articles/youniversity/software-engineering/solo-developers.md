I've learned to love the complexity because it's part of engineering. the more complexity and engineer can handle the more useful their projects will be. and how do engineers manage complexity? by using the right techniques and the right tools! just like a real life architecture. this is why I compare software engineering with architecture, because you are designing the internal structure of a building but also the exterior aesthetics that breathe life to a city.


I think at a certain point, no matter how well you structure your code — one person can't do everything. 

Now I understand why applications like obsidian and vscode require entire teams to maintain. I mean, it's just a window with some fancy tools inside, how hard could that be to maintain? Behind the panels & buttons, are thousands of invisible threads, that were carefully tested & weaved by many people. One person can't handle an entire structure, not becuase of it's complexity, but becuase we human beings are limited by time.

These programs give the illusion that they are simple and manageable in the front end, but that's only because there was a team of people behind it that spent hours and hours fine-tuning the layers of the program so that the front end interface layer could be as accessible as possible so that the user can interact with the most powerful parts of the program underneath.
