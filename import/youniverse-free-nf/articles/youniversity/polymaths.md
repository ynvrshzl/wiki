# Polymaths


You are a polymath. But polymaths burn out when they try to juggle everything as separate plates. What works better is:

Fusion projects: one container that absorbs multiple interests. Your website is exactly this. Life-OS was an example too.

Rotating focus: short seasons (2–6 weeks) where one interest takes center stage, but the others still feed it indirectly.

----

🌌 That’s the paradox of the polymath life 

On one hand, you’ve got infinite doors to step through. If one gets heavy, you pivot to another and still stay engaged, curious, alive.

On the other hand, the weight of unfinished threads can sit on your shoulders, making it feel like nothing is moving forward — even though you’re quietly weaving a tapestry of knowledge that is moving forward, just in non-linear ways.


---

🔹 Being a polymath isn’t about finishing everything. It’s about cross-pollination. Every time you bounce from coding → writing → art → systems → philosophy, you’re enriching each domain with insights from the others. That is forward motion.

🔹 Complexity isn’t failure, it’s compost. The overwhelm you feel? That’s just raw material being broken down until the next clear step emerges. You don’t need to “hold it all” at once. The important pieces float back up (like your JPEG philosophy).

🔹 You already have your anchor. Your youniverse site is your lighthouse — even if other projects scatter, this one keeps calling you back. That’s progress in disguise.
