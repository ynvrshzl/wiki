# Analysis Paralysis
Analysis paralysis is no joke, it is a dillema with the infinite, a direct confrontation with all that we cannot understand.

We human beings can never see everything as a whole, we can only see parts of things. This is why, we use frameworks to understand the world around us. The scaffolding that keeps all of our human psychology together, is weaved together by the threads of our beliefs, experiences, memories & connections.

A framework we can use to understand analysis paralysis, is a sculpture. In the beginning, eveery shape is possible. From a single chunk of clay, infinite forms & shapes can be modelled. But we must start from outside the clay itself, and in our goal. What is it that we are trying to model? If we do not have a direction

> [!tip] Main idea
> A goal gives our infinite possibilities, a direction. 

Limits breed creativity. And goals are biologically the best source of limiting what we can work with. A goal, also provides us with positive emotion, which is crucial for the stability of a system.



But one thing, will naturally lead to another, this is becuase from one point, we can chain other points. But we must start with one thing.

Analysis paralysis, as applied to daily life.

Trial & error: when building scaleable systems in any area of your life, wether it's software, or a new lifestyle, how far ahead you plan, actually makes a big difference in how the health of the system scales. The trickiest part is balancing these two: analysis paralysis & building ahead. Through trial & error, by building small blocks at first, you can later see how far ahead you needed to build structures, and so the next time, you remember this, and continue iterating & trying things. That's perhaps, the most important part.

---

Whenever i'm overwhelmed, I treat everything like a jenga block tower, I can either carefully continue, or let it all fall apart & start from the very simplest of things, zero.

But that's what I do, what can *you* do to overcome analysis paralysis? Much like all of my work, I can't give specific advice becuase I don't know everything, but I can share the models that can adapt to your life.

Here, the model is the static-dynamic theory. Imagine a computer operating system.

The longer you stare at something, the more "mistakes" you see. Sometimes this is good for details that need to be correct, other times, more problems are created.

This is a powerful lens for ADHD/autism decision paralysis. If you separate what is non-negotiable static truth (scaffolding) from what is optional dynamic expression (visuals, customizations, workflows), then you can focus your energy only on what actually matters for the goal at hand.
