# Zooming in/out: Getting lost in little everyday problems

If you dive too deep, you lose sight of the problem you wanted to solve; if you stay too shallow, you risk reinventing the wheel.

> [!tip] Main idea
> Do you often find yourself forgetting why you started? That initial feeling seems to get drowned in the noise of little things. I hear people say: _"It's about the little things."_ Yeah buddy, try saying that when you have hundreds of _"little things"_ wasting your time. Lol. Negative thinking is the enemy of growth, but you don't want to take this life so seriously that you are [paralyzed with meaning.](paralyzed-with-meaning.md)


Getting stuck in the details can make me abandon projects or even ideas before I even attempted trying them. 

It's like zooming too much in a map, and seeing pixles rather than the big-picture. This is not only a metaphor, but it literally happens! In digital art, it's common to get stuck zooming in on an illustartion & spending hours on a little spot, only to realize it looks completely incorrect when flipped around or zoomed out. This happens becuase we need to "re-calibrate." The human brain get's numb & things begint o feel like noise. We focus on the nose, rather than the face. 

How do I avoid getting lost in the problems, the small details? So I can remember the high-level picture.

Use symbolic representations taht remind you of the original purpose/big-picture. For me that's music & anime scenes.
