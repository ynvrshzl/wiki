**Gravity star theory**
In all creative fields, there is a reference. Without source material, it is like an unstable particle, pulled in all directions, without a gravity source to anchor it.

This little idea came to me through the wonderful, sometimes, cursed gift of **observation.** 

I was thinking about storytelling and the nature of using references in order to study and learn. I believe all art forms are connected in some fundamental sense for example before a storyteller can create their own vivid scenes they first must learn how to retell moments in real life. for example I'm realizing it has been extremely difficult, nearly impossible to create my own vivid scenes just for my imagination this is because again there's the nature of too much possibility it's almost like an unstable particle that has no gravity source you're pulled in all different directions. the one thing that helps the most give direction is something that already exists, an external source like a reference. I don't know what other tools are used by creatives in order to create a gravity source in their work. by using real life as a reference it's almost as if you can recreate a video you've watched or a movie like seeing in your life. I believe this is how authors either intuitively or explicitly learn how to tell stories, especially the vivid kind. it's a lot like how an artist will use references of anatomy before they are able to draw the human body at all angles. they develop what's known as the visual library almost like on AI chatbot learning an algorithm the more you do it the more you learn. however this is where the hard part comes in because the quality of your study material determines the quality of your visual library and therefore the quality of your work. hopefully I'm not analyzing this too much but intuitively, if you have good study material you'll be prepared for when the time comes that you cannot use the study material. kind of like a student and a test! tell me what you think GPT this is a theory? has this been observed by others? I like to call this my little gravity star theory

I had no idea that human language could be used so creatively. 


it really is more than just vocabulary, but the alchemy of using these words in more and more creative ways to paint images in the human imagination. writing for me is incredibly difficult without an external source of reference. if I have an image or a sound, it's almost like my writing has a gravity, and all of my words start to orbit that source of inspiration. 


### The edge of chaos
I even have a name for this concept that has helped me to avoid analysis paralysis, it's called the gravity star theory. having no reference or inspiration is like having pure chaos, like a song that follows no structure and it's a clashing series of sounds. but having too much order is like a programmed drum beat. 

It is repetitive and it becomes mind and numbingly boring to the point where you stop listening to drum sounds and everything just sounds like noise. so there's this edge of chaos or proximal development that I've heard of that exists somewhere between these extreme polarities. I think having a reference helps me fine tune that edge. 

I really have no idea where to take my universe stories forward I know I have a lot of little ideas for each of the personas life stories and I even have some scene ideas, but even if I could separate each idea by its type, I think it would be double the work if I did that and then I started writing. I think I can write as I go and organize these things in the stories themselves. 

and with external sources of inspiration like imagery, favorite stories, anime, movies, music and soundscapes, I think I can weave together these stories. that's the hardest part telling a story as if it's meant to be read from page one to page n.

but most importantly the biggest gravity star in my universe is human emotion. I think that should guide everything. and leave the logical structuring as a lesser priority.
