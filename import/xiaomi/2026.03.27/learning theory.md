# Learning
The mechanics and purchases of learning, a lifetime ability, built into us. 

Let's explore this fascinating observation.

So, recently I've been noticing that it appears that in every single creative field, the artist or musician or even the baker, they all require references. And it could be considered inspiration sources, but regardless of semantics, material which is used as guidance, I think, could be mythologically described as working with the chaos by referencing a body of work that has already contained chaos. So what I mean by this is that, if you think about it, here are two points. Human abilities are limited to our physical locomotive range, right? So that's why we build tools. But the thing about tools is that you can essentially create infinite potential. So like, a canvas is essentially infinite potential. And so, it looks blank, and that's really because literally any illustration could exist on that canvas. And so that's pure chaos. That's essentially the analysis paralysis equivalent for the artist. And I think this applies to all fields. But the second point is that human beings develop fields and professions because someone at some point in time decided to sit down and contact the chaos, so to speak, until this is essentially how we got musical notation and color theory. It's really because human beings sat down without a established standard formal approach, and they just asked questions and did the normal human thing. Before there was approach and technique and the correct way to spend your time, there was endless tinkering with the void, so to speak. And so eventually, when you reference a piece of work, You're essentially looking at a contained form of chaos that another human being has shaped. And that actually has a historical lineage, and so this is why art compresses time and space, and I think it also explains why music can be nostalgic, and carefully crafted aromas can be incredibly powerful. Like, it's more than just Darwinian biological evolution. These are lifetimes of so many people we will never meet compressed into a medium, into a language that we can learn to speak because there are techniques and ideas and the chaos has been formed and sculpted. And so when you play music, you're not just making sound, but you're actually looking at a language that human beings have developed as a form of expression. And so I guess... ummm.... I guess at a meta-level, all of these mediums are like languages comprised of sub-languages; forms of expression with techniques & fields established over centuries of human experience.

i think this is an incredibly under-explored part of our human nature... almost like a ghost of archaeology!

okay so back to the main idea:  reference materials. you could in theory, rediscover the techniques and reverse-engineer your way to creating something akin to the reference... wether a pastry or a song... but this is where our earlier idea comes in: if the empty canvas is like the human mind... infinite potential, no form, no path, no structure... then the formal techniques and the structure of the field itself, is the structure which guides the human being through that infinite chaos... essentially, proper study helps you avoid wasting your time lol.

so what do you do if you don't have access to these resources? what if they aren't shaped to how to actually learn things?

that's a question I'm exploring everyday. infact, its the reason I created my own personal university... with lore... "youniversity." come on. that's so "universe" on brand lol!

but wait; aren't we just reverse engineering the craft? isn't that "wasting time?" yes! and no! "wasting" time assumes we are spending time like currency... if you enjoy the process of learning itself (my youniversity helps me tremendously with this)... then what other purpose does time like money serve? money is meant to be spent, not hoarded. just like knowledge. use it, or lose it

this is a fascinating part of our human nature... seriously... it has so many implications outside of "art is just a hobby"... no... i know firsthand... art is the most human thing we have. stories are art. pottery is art. even neural networks are art. so I think... it's important that we understand the abstract (pun intended lol) structure of creative Fields for human beings... and possibly... this is how we human beings learn everything and anything at all... though that's a big leap TBH!

let me explain my hypothesis; if art is guided by reference materials, which help the human work within chaos... then perhaps we learn by observation and recreation... rehearsal... play... that's how we learn as baby humans to speak... i think lol.

anyways! this is a fascinating field because the art of learning itself is probably one of the most important abilities we have as human beings. learning the structure and mechanics of learning itself... (that's very meta lol)... helps us the work within the chaos and make great things!!!

why does this matter? For someone like me that wants to learn several domains, but I don't really have access to formal branches of study, I am learning my own personal style of learning, which resembles a lot like a mycelium network exploring various paths until we find a stable path towards the source. And so, now that I'm learning my own personal learning style, everything has changed, because learning itself is the fundamental ability, and it is proto-language. Like I stated earlier in my hypotheses, I think we human beings learn through observation and recreation. And I also think we learn through forms of play and rehearsal and role play. It's such a fascinating area of human nature, because this comes back to living with the overwhelming noisy mind that wants to understand many domains and express many domains. And so, this has helped me to balance the overwhelming information. in all of those domains. Because now that we understand the fundamental ability, now I realize that we're focusing on the right things, and it's almost like a mycelium network finding the stable, strongest path towards the source. Now, it doesn't mean that we've discovered a golden theory of learning, and we know how to learn everything, and I can learn a language overnight, LOL. But it does mean that learning has become a joy, and when there is more joy, there is less stress. And when there is less stress, there is less burnout and less frustration and less abandonment. And I feel like that speaks for itself, because when inspiration dies, it's almost like the fuel source of an engine, and the fuel of the engine that is creativity is inspiration, and inspiration is only really found by engaging with life and engaging with the chaos and engaging with reference!

so honestly this is getting pretty complicated to explain but it matters both out of personal level and also for other people especially my neurodivergent folks who struggle with managing multiple interests and learning. I really do think that learning is the fundamental ability. well, one of our fundamental abilities!
