what if telling stories... was paradoxically linear and nonlinear? what if there are modals of an author who tells stories? 

for example, there is the structured approach. where you create maps and get back stories to characters and try to construct a wiki to make sense of their relationships. but the more structure you try to impose on stories the more you realize, the very nature of stories is a unfolding path. you explore a single narrative, but they are an infinite amount of potential narratives inside a story. almost like little Mandel brought branches. and from what I've learned in mathematics, analysis, has no zoom limit. you can always zoom in and you'll never get to the very end. and so this is important for two reasons, one, you cannot fundamentally structure a story while you are still writing it, and two, this chaos is actually the fuel of stories. so if structured storytelling is our main modal, what if we just sat down and embraced the chaos for a little bit? I've never genuinely done this. it almost seems too obvious, your problem is that your stories are too complicated, and you don't really have any ideas, just sit down and write!

that's actually very similar to abstract painting. so now we have two modes of operation. we have structured, and we have abstract.
