The civilization that preserved memories, they were able to construct a way to share memories with each other.

In full detail, all sensory details as pure as when they happened.

They combined biotechonologies of images, video, with the 5 human senses.

It was no longer science, it was spiritual ascension beyond the human form. 

But they realized a tragic truth; to be able to save what another human being saw through their eyes, is not only overwhelming, but never the same as the source memory.

They realized then; "It is not the memories that make life significant, but rather, the fact that they fade."

So death, then, it seemed was a necessary and natural process.

So naturally, the civilzation began falling apart > one to continue preserving the miracle of life, and others completely  hopeless.

This question single-handedly shaped both communities;
"What's the point of life if we all die in the end?"
