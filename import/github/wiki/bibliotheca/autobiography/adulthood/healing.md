# Healing
I was afraid to feel again. I just wanted to lay in bed & heal that way. But the more time I spent in bed, the more dead I felt. Healing. That's not found in bed-rotting. Or doom-scrolling. Healing is a complicated thing, and sometimes, it doesn't look like anything. You could be working on something that makes you really happy, or you could just be in a hot shower, with your eyes closed. Feeling the warm air, and your lungs relish.

# How do you know you're healing?
Healing is painful, sometimes. But healing can also be beautiful.


# Healing hurts
Healing is painful, proportionate to the depth of your scars & the weight of your trauma. But it's worth it beyond measureable description or what words could describe. And the euphoria, the beauty of healing, is in the contrast of the pain survived. Like a warrior, treading through the final stages of a collosal jungle temple, uncovering it's most deepest secrets, and finding the way back home. The only way the warrior can escape, is the way they came in — through the entrace, through the beginning of all things.

It hurt. It hurt me so deeply. People that I thought loved me... people that told me my whole life they were "good." My parents. My family. I learned through this time, that letting go, was the answer. If I was going to heal & get out of here, I had to accept a few things. I had to accept who they truly were. I had to accept who I truly am. Finally, I had to let go. Let go of who I thought they were. Let go of who I thought I was. Who I thought I had to be.
