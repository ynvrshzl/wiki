# Healing
This section is dedicated to my healing journey. I can only describe it as a rollercoaster, and honestly, that makes it all worth it, even the bad things. After all — what's a rollercoaster without ups & downs?

When I began to awaken, to the incredible fragility of life, I realized just how fragile these bodies we're living in are. Beneath the para-social & larger-than-life persona's that we mask ourselves with, we are all the same inside. We all need food to survive, and we all need shelter to feel safe. We all need a reason to wake up everyday, and we all need a reason to live. But I realized, few people are in-tune to themselves... few people know themselves. This, as far as I can tell, is becuase in order to know yourself, you have to spend some time alone, completely away from others. You begin to ask yourself: "What is it that I truly want? What do I truly love? What makes me happy? Why should I stay alive in a world so cruel?"

Denial is like a safe bubble, but once that bubble is popped, it’s nearly impossible to **"unknow"** what you now see — the impermanence, the cruelty, the randomness, the fragility of life. You become like a person who took off the VR headset, and everyone else is still pretending the simulation is fine.

Some people call that depression. Others call it awakening. Often… it's both.

# Healing without a therapist
If I could have afforded a therapist in those moments, God knows how much pain I wouldn't have had to survive on my own, but I didn't have that. Some of us don't have that, and that's okay. I realized I am enough.
