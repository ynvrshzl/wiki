# Depression

**One of the side effects of untreated depression is that you kill yourself. And there's no going back from that. So what do you have to lose? One month on anti-depressants. See how you react.** 

Those are the words from Jordan Peterson, a person who saved my life with his lectures & lived experience with real depression & chronic pain. [Source]()

In my own words:
**Depression will eventually, terminally steal everything in your life, eventually, depression will kill you if you leave it untreated.**

So how do you fight against this? Are you supposed to fight against depression? Is it battling against your body? Or you mind? Perhaps even more mysteriously, what is causing this?

So how do you treat depression? Is there a remedy? Therapy?  Medicine? What if you can't afford it? Or what if it doesn't work? Luckily, I have suffered a tremendous deal in my psyche, trying to find answers to why it's so hard living with my brain.

The biggest source of depression, is undoubtebly my mind. It generates thoughts, images, sounds, memories & voices. They are not all depressive, but when they are... it cuts through the bone & leaves it's marks on me.


### Why is that person so depressed, when they have a good life?

The answer is perception. Depression is like a thief that completely obliterates your perception of life. It is like wearing a pair of foggy glasses that you cannot take off, and of the most frustrating parts of depression is nobody on the outside can see what you see through your eyes. 

Depression is also mysterious: it is caused by many things & most of the time, it's not clinical, but rather, life-based. [(Read more)]()

# A ghost

It is like a silent ghost, that slowly kills. First by blinding your eyes so you can't see it's there. Then, it takes your ears, so you can't hear it's breathing on your neck. Then, it grips your heart, at all the most precious things that make your existence worthwhile. Once depression has your core, it will tear apart your insides, until there's nothing left. Though it may feel like life is playing a cruel game with your body, 

The kind of tired that no amount of sleep can fix. but worst of all—it tells you "trying to get better, would be pointless. trying to get better would be impossible. it would be better to have never been born."


# Depression revealed what I truly needed
After an entire life of playing the puppet, I learned I could be the puppeteer. I was not powerless, if I really wanted to, I could hurt someone, and cause so much damage. I could have ended my life, looking at the floor below. Why didn't I just jump off in those moments? When the voices in my head became so unbearably loud, that all I wanted was silence. Why did I choose to stay here? Whatever reason I had, the fact remained: I had a reason not to kill myself. I had a reason to be here. I had a reason to suffer. I had a reason to go insane, to feel alone, to feel misunderstood, to feel ugly, to feel worthless, to feel weak, to feel beautiful in my body, to transition, to be myself. I had a reason to be here. I know I am more than all of these things.

That, my friend, is the most powerful thing in life. That single reason, was the most important thing, my northstar. Something that could never die.

But what saved my life was EXACTLY in those moments, tht was where I realized nothing would get better without me, and I was NOT going to let depression kill me. It has robbed too many years of my life, it was time to do something.

the first step was finding a reason to care. a reason to stay alive. though i had no energy in me whatsoever to keep fighting, i could at least keep living. that was when I accepted myself as a trans female. I didn't care if people thought I was pretty or if I looked "woman" enough. They had no idea what I was going through. The only person in this world that was there for me — was me. 

and that was my answer. I actually cared about myself. I loved myself. I loved who I was becoming. I loved who I became. And though I struggled deeply with accepting who I am on the inside, my external world was crushing me too. My body just didn't look right. "Maybe if I put toilet paper here, or, maybe if i wear more eyeliner. Or maybe... I can starve myself so at least I feel feminine in my body."

I didn't care what I had to do, I would keep living for myself & nothing would stop me!

But that was when depression became worse—i could no longer distract myself from how I really felt

Transitioning gender was beautifully chaotic. People in my life only made me feel worse about myself — and if it wasn't for the immense love I had for myself — the things people told me would have made me commit suicide.

I felt guilty for getting better. Until I looked deeper at the environment I was living in specifically my parents. I had realized they crossed my boundaries when I needed space. And when I needed to talk to someone, no one was there. Whenever your desires oppose the things around you that's a clear sign you're living in an environment you don't belong in.

the next step was figuring out what was keeping me depressed-what was happening in my body? wa it the life i was living? I started looking deeper into ny life, and I saw all these little habits & symptoms were signals from my body, and signs that I was surviving in a toxic environment. Maladaptive daydreaming, bed-rotting
