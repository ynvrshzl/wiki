# Would it have been better to never have been born?

i too wish i had never been born, and though i've wanted to end my life, i'm still here... sometimes I don't even know why... but maybe we would rather suffer & gain something in the end, than not exist at all. maybe we were created on some higher level, in the image of God... to experience the glory of rebirth after death... 

and like a rapper once said, you can die many times while you're alive.

We are all, perhaps, kaleidoscopes of a fractal reality, in which we can't comprehend the outside world, but we can imagine it. Like a flower unfolding onto itself, regardless of how glorious our knowledge may be, we are still human beings, and we can forget. This is, according to what I've experienced, the purpose of our existence. to ascend but also, be human. like Jesus did. *(but honestly i don't know anything lol.)*

Or a new world? Is it better to not bring children into this world?


First of all, let me say that I'm grateful that we as human beings have the ability to process reality through our cognition. That means we have options to escape pain, or atleast, numb it. And these options do not include ending your life or anyone else's life. Sometimes, when the suffering of life has weighed you down for so long, the logical outcome is to have never been born. Unfortunately, ending your life, or someone else's life, is not the same as never have been born — we each have a responsibility to utilize our cognition, to the best of our abilities. That is the sole purpose of the human immune system, to survive, withstand, and regenerate as much as possible.

I tend to ruminate on the darkness of existence, and how powerless I feel, especially seeing how irresponsible human beings are with the power they've been given. Look at modern life. Clean, drinking water. Hospitals. Schools. Yes, society is disgustingly narsissistic, but when you're hopelessly depressed, sometimes, the fairy dust of this world, is enough to stop you from ending it yourself.
