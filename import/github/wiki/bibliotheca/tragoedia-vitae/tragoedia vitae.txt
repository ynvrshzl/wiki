essentially, this is a body of work that is a resolution to our intense desire to help reduce suffering for human beings and animals and all of life on this planet. but we realize fundamentally we can't solve every problem and we can't save everyone and our message and our stories can't be completed in one day and they won't reach everyone and fundamentally all of this is just a tragedy. like there's nothing positive I can say about it other than maybe at least we are expressing ourselves rather than feeling hopeless. that's actually a natural reaction to this realization that you can try to do good, but you will never truly know if you truly helped me someone's life better. and so the only real answer I see moving forward is to just accept this and to just have faith. it really is just a chance for faith and to believe in something even if we don't understand it. 

I think to just believe, that's actually necessary... and just like love it's enough.

but the biggest problem with this work is clarifying that we are not accepting the tragedy of life and we are not accepting hopelessness, but rather we're trying to make the most of our limited time on this planet and really understanding our biology and how the human being actually works and what we need in order to not only function but also create and evolved and more importantly why any of this matters at all because there will be times where we are pushed to our human limit and we ask that dreaded question, what's the fucking point? 

I feel like most of our ethical work is literally to help you find the answer to that question because in those moments you don't have time to dig through philosophy or religion, that answer has to exist inside you and it has to be real and you have to actually believe in it. at least that's fundamentally our answer to the tragedy of life, it's not essentially to accept it, but rather to understand that it exists as an unfortunate side effect of the beautiful parts of life.
