## Pg. 01
Inspired by Newton's Work. Like a survival-guide for polymaths/neurodivergent minds.

This is like my personal efforts of trying to share everything I've learned on how we have learned to navigate this confusing life, as a polymathic.

one of the most fundamental ideas is that __in nature nothing is wasted.__ and for a polymath, we are more connected to our natural biology, then we give ourselves credit for. especially in today's modern world with technology, it often feels like we are being consumed by networks and technologies and distractions, but nothing is wasted. I've seen this happen in my own life. we have no idea how events will connect in the future or how our past...[???]

there's also another fundamental idea that we're learning recently, the theory of possibility and practicality. essentially when we are experimenting freely in playground mode, that's actually where the polymath belongs in. it's like a natural flow for us, and if you know anything about work, we can't force flow. you can't force productivity, it is a natural byproduct of the ecosystem we live in. the word product, and productivity, are actually the same idea, 
they are results, effects of something. and that's something is the environment. 

that brings us to the other fundamental idea which is not limited to polymaths, but it's incredibly influential in everything we do, the environment itself influences the human being more than almost anything else. and the environment can be split into many layers, the physical, the emotional, the sensory, psychological, virtual. that's an underrated idea, the fact that you can create a virtual emotional inner environment even if the outside external world is chaotic and fundamentally misaligned with your inner world. that brings us to another idea or more specifically our experience with living in an environment that was noisy and painful. ultimately this is the deepest philosophy I've ever discovered in my life, and it's the fact that without love, there is literally no point in anything. 

the only reason I even care about this is because I love, and I love so deeply that it hurts, and that's actually okay because that's a sign that I love so deeply. that's a sign that it's real. if it hurts it's real.
