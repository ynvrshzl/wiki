## Pain

> "For the human being, pain without reason, is pointless. Pain with sufficient reason, becomes meaningful. Infact, one could argue, it is the fundamental source of meaning in human life."
>
> _Excerpt from [Pain theory](#)_

In the context of healing, pain is actually necessary to understand what makes life meaningful to us human beings. It's almost as if this knowledge is the most dangerous double-edged blade. You either learn to wield pain, or it makes you suffer. I hate admitting this, becuase it almost sounds like the universe is governed by a tyrant... but I don't think that view is even scientifically, or spiritual coherent.

Pain, from a biological view, is the only constant that produces change in systems. Without pain, there is no movement. Desire, is actually a form of pain [^3][3], and desire, is __the__ fundamental driver of life.

My first reaction to this discovery was "why isn't this taught to us as children?!" I think it's becuase, it sits in the "compost" side of life. Meaning, the eastern mystical healing, has been describing pain as a necessary part of life, for centuries. It is simply not accessible information, to a young person today, who is either bombarded by technology, or busy with school work.

This is what makes pain-metabolizing mediums, like Anime, Music and Movies, so crucial to our times. They actively metabolize this knowledge, into accessible forms, for the young human being.

## Vacuum

Healing cannot exist in a vaccum. Therefore, healing of any system in life, especially that of a human being, requires some kind of **sustainable** contact with life.

## Force
Healing cannot be forced. Like a chinese finger trap, the more you struggle, the stronger the trap becomes, the more stuck you feel. Paradoxically, loose, gentle, strategic, sustainable movement, let's the finger slip out of the trap. The human being requires the same function.
