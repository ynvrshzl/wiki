## Structure 
Autobiographical, Conversational, Mythical, Badass

Fundamentally, our first talk will be structured as an [Autobiography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autobiography). This provides adequate structure to an otherwise completely chaotic network of interconnected memories, knowledge, wisdom, and more. Space without time is chaos.

So structure is deeply important to our talk, it is by definition; foundational. Because, the [ethos](#ethos) of this talk, is less the pain I've experienced as a human being, and much more about what has actually helped me, and most of all, what saved my life from committing self-destruction, and what continues to save my life everyday.

This infact, is the most important part of our ethical work; the study of what matters most in this short human life [(Ethism).]()

But if our talk only contains ethics, it'll be extremely boring and paradoxically; defeats the purpose of this talk. So we imagine balancing the heavy topics, with mythos, specifically our [youniverse]() work like our anime, the personas that we created during this depressing time, maybe music, visuals, and how exactly we metabolized pain into art, and what that practically means for the human being who is suffering.

It's essentially our life story, tethered by time as history — the only way I know give order to chaos! But also conversational, not with the audience or even the listener, but with my past self. Why were you struggling so much? What saved your life? How is that practically transferrable to other human lives?

# Theme
Jordan Peterson's talk surely saved my life. But, it was definitely difficult to understand and practically apply individually. I think What mattered more during his speech, was his enthusiasm about extremely existential and Abstract concept of human nature, life, and especially meaning.

I think that's what matters is most than anything he said. as well as showcasing the yin yang symbol, introducing us to the concept of chaos and order, and being deeply engaged, authentically honest about what he was sharing. those are the things that made his talk so important and a life-saving to me. 

However, Jordan focused primarily on politics, whereas we focus on the individual, and then their relationship to the world. Regardless, I would love for him to see our tedx talk, and just to shake his hand and tell him thank you.
