# Analysis of Jack Mooder - Why should human beings never define justice?

Context: I wrote this in real life, while going on some afternoon groceries. 

We were walking at publix just now for some afternoon groceries, and we just saw two kids but she told on the ground and front and I really wanted to step in and it just felt like a moral violation on a crime that they were hurting each other, but I just don't understand the complexity of the situation right? And this isn't me just intellectualizing my way out of a problem because I think the character of Jack is essentially an exploration of what happens to a human being when they witness so much cruelty and prime without justice, and he tried to become justice in human form by judging people for what they did wrong. Sort of like kira from death note. The problem with a human being judging other human beings is that your essentially trying to be a god-like figure because morality and ethics are inherently non-human there's something outside of human... like we don't create laws as so to speak, we discover them. nobody created the laws of physics We just discovered them and some change all the time...

there are definitely laws that are constants and they don't change. 

In essence, Jack Mooder is the exploration of why a human being should not try to become a God-like figure. We cannot define justice in our own lens, because we are inherently flawed and biased and we judge each other based on our own interpretation of good and evil but only God has a pure sense, because again it's inherently non-human.
