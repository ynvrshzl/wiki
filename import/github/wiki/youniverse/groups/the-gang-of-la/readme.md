
# The "Robin Hoods"
Kobe is part of the most aggressive "Robin hoods" of the gang of la. they essentially will do whatever it takes to create a better world, even if it means creating their own laws, or stopping criminals themselves. 

it's an incredibly dangerous political group in the city of "Lost Angelis" a world that already is founded in tragedy, the word lost, is literally embedded in the name of the city. 

the gang of la, join forces with the Lost girls, and together they form the robin hoods. a group, a subset within the gang of la.

most people believe the gang to be criminals and outlaws, but in reality, they justify animalistic violence, as righteousness. 

"Cleaning up the streets, because the Royal party of the city are ignoring the people. they'd rather build towers of gold and silver, so they don't have to hear the cries of the people below. we've got to knock it all down. and he's criminals? the real outlaws roaming the streets? it's going to take a dark night, a robin hood, to stop this madness."

Kobe helps steal from the ridge to feed the poor. the attitude of the gang of la, is much the same as the folklore tale of Robin hood. except they practice at a social, world scale. filled with broken, hopeless youth, the gang gave them a reason to fight in a world that doesn't seem to care whether you live or not.

but the gang of La is also an incredibly dangerous sensitive part of human nature, which is evil justified as righteousness. there is a reason why the law system has to be a collective monument and an unchanging oath. when a single human being tries to define what good and evil is, they do so through their own bias. this is extremely dangerous because it essentially gives way to pathological belief systems and that gives way to war and human tragedy. as studied in real human history. 

Their story is an incredibly sensitive gray area in human nature, and it is a moral responsibility, to share their story as a warning, rather than glorification.

most of the members eventually realize that, we were all fighting for the same thing, but we believed completely differently. that is the core moral message of their story. what you believe in defines your action, and your action shapes the world around you. if you believe in the wrong things, you could literally make things worse. this is something that we personally struggled with, during our [existential descent.]()
