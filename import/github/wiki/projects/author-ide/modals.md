# Modals in Authoring
This concept describes how authors experience the actual writing process.

Think of this psychologically speaking; when we are authoring stories, we have two primary modals. **Fluid and Step.**  Like in mathematical functions, interpolation & step functions. One is smooth gradients that flow, and the other is highly specific.

- **Fluid -** is the modal of the **reader's** experience. The story is experienced as flowing, fluent passages, flowing forth.
- **Step -** is the **author's** primary modal. It's all about attributes, details, scenes, events, and fragments of a story.

However, when the author enters a "flow" with the writing, they actually simply let the words flow through them. Somatically speaking, it feels alot like reading while writing. Like mapping a terrain, and using the map at the same time. In software, this is known as ["Bootstrapping"]() which is "The usage of a smaller tool, to construct other bigger tools. Which then help construct even bigger tools."

# Modals in Author IDE (IDE-A)
In IDE_A, the t wo primary modals are switcheable. Think of it as the ability to "Compile" a list of ideas, into a flowing passage. This is an incredibly useful tool to have, becuase it allows us to experience one model of source, without fundamentally changing our approach to authoring.
