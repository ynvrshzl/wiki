
# Abstract: What if computer-generated enviornments, could have a positive, therapeutic, healing effect on human beings?

From our personal, lived experience _(Autobiography, Chapter 7: "Healing Arc")_ I created a virtual target store. It was a place that already had positive feelings associated within the spatial memory, but actually getting to build it, brick by brick, inside of blender, gave me a reason to wake up everyday. I had suffered from agoraphobia (fear of going outside), panic attacks, deep anxiety, and I thought i'd never go outside again. But seeing how beautiful this enviornment truly was, with the sunlight, and the glassy windows, and the coffee shop, and the everyday, mundane life... it inspired me to live again.

This then is evidence, that in some human beings, enviornment is actually therapeutic.


The main purpose of the game is really like an environment, spatial, trauma healing research laboratory. 

It's like both a virtual-game-lore laboratory, and part of our real-world research on healing trauma. basically, we are creating enviornments from places that heal us sensory like > aventura mall, blender, target, and even my dream home + dream room. there are few things as healing and immediate, as being able to walk in a space you built completely on your own... every block, every choice... was yours... that's... like walking through the halls of memories in your mind... 

like if sims 4 + minecraft had a therapeutic, realistic child. lol.
