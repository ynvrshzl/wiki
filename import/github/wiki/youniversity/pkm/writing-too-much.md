# Is private writing pointless?

Well, we need to define the purpose of writing to begin with.

Here is my idea: What if private writing is completely pointless, if you don't share it with others, or if you don't use it yourself? If you don't even remember the notes you take, or if it doesn't positively change your life, isn't it just clutter?

Writing is externalizing, it shows you're doing the right thing. You're processing what's in your head, in the outside world. Trying to find a thing that actually bridges the outside & the inside, enough for you to be able to walk on that bridge, that is the question. There are countless ways to do this.

Instead of writing so much, i learned to compress things into other forms, music coutains my  memories & emotions (that's why it saved my life) stories contain long-term stoarge & memory, and meaning. instead of writing specific things, learn to use writing to guide checktopints, rather than confine you into a box.

This is the same line of thinking as "Min-maxing" which is all about maximizing productivity & effectiveness, while forgetting that we are human beings and we are organic creatures, not turing machines. If you enjoy the feeling of writing, and watching your notes & systems grow with you, why is that not enough?

This kind of thought is a symptom. In what other areas of your life does this show up? "If you don't do only useful things, then you're wasting your time." That almost entirely captures this feeling.
