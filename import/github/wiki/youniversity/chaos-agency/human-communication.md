# Human Communication and Chaos Agency

**Abstract:** Human communication is a direct observable enviornment for chaos agency. The way we communicate with each other, reveals _2 major concepts about complex systems and chaos;_

1. [Sustainable systems reflect their inner structures.](#10-systems-reflect-structures)
2. [Complex, Sustainable systems are fundamentally fault tolerant](#20-fault-tolerance)


## 1.0 Systems reflect structures
if you think about how chaotic human language is, it's kind of like a reflection of our own inner human cognitive world. we often mythologize this in our stories. but it's not just a mythos, it's physically visible. our complex chaotic human language somehow works, it's somehow is enough to map the chaotic inner world. that might be where human language emerged from. and so the chaotic outside tool (language), reflects the chaotic inner world (human)

## 2.0 Fault tolerance 

conceptually speaking, fault tolerance is what gives a complex system, sustainability against the entropy of chaos. for example, in human communication, we don't have to describe exactly what we mean, so long as we point in the right direction, it's enough to convey meaning. this explains why things like show and tell, body language, and visual arts, are so powerful. because they seem to compress vast amounts of information, while avoiding ambiguity. 

fault tolerance is also what gives a system, sustainability in general. for example, you don't have to feed the human body the exact amount of nutrition every single day, levels can balance out, systems can borrow energy from other systems. 

## 3.0 How is this practically applicable? 
when we understand that human communication reveals that there is no perfect expression medium, and we also have built-in fault tolerance, as well as anti-fragility systems that benefit from stress... I think at the very least, this reduces the overwhelm of perfection. 

there's this idea that if you reduce something fundamentally to its substrate constituents, that you'll arrive at a perfect answer, but actually all you're doing is rewinding history and tracing evolution of change. a complex system actually benefits from the reflection of its structure, not reduction (naive simplicity). 

## 4.0 Scalability of systems
when a system scales to the point where probability of possibilities becomes incalculable, it becomes alive. (mythologically speaking, it's almost like it breaks the sound barrier, or it escapes the speed of light.)

Perfection is no longer attainable. Learning to navigate within chaos, becomes the only sustainable method. 
In our previous example, we described human interaction as an example. Language and interaction are very chaotic and nuanced. There are so many words to describe the same thing, and beyond language, most of language is nonverbal. and there are components and layers and levels to human interaction and language. empathy is one of them. you have to take into consideration how the other human will feel. this is the point where data analysis becomes almost unusable as a practical tool. because you can't analyze how another human being will process your expression, this is the point where you have to intuitively feel, empathically.

that might actually be the only sustainable conclusion, of this entire analysis on human communication and how we navigate chaos. more importantly, how we can learn to use these principles and apply them to our daily life.
