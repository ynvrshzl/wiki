especially for neurodivergent / autistic / ADHDers. 

but this is incredibly important to understand, learning anything in life will always fundamentally feel like learning to walk again. It's because whether we are learning something psychological like mathematics, or a sport, we always carry this somatic orientation. And that's essentially what we're doing when we learn a new ability or a language, we're learning to walk again and learning to balance. That's what makes walking so difficult, it's a controlled fall. That's essentially what it feels like to write a bike, you have to confidently be moving otherwise your center of balance.

there's also this desperation to express, even when you don't really know the language. is like wanting to run without even knowing how to walk. or playing your favorite piano piece, before learning the basics. 

in theory it should set a goal. something long-term to strive for as motivation. but it often ends up feeling more like desperation. I mismatch in fluency and expression. like speaking in a language you don't understand. you know *what* you want to say but not *how* to say it.
