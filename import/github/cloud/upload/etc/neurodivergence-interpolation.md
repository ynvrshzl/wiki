# Interpolation as a sustainable mechanic for Neurodivergent persons

Interpolation (gradient-transition) is the neurodivergent-friendly mechanism of change.

we absolutely cannot handle things like... sudden explosive sounds, immediate change, abrupt brakes or breaks.

Actually this is very neurotypical, regardless of the neurodivergent theory... like nothing sustainable in life can operate on pure explosive impact, like a clap for example.

We, as neurodivergent human beings, __can indeed tolerate difficult enviornments,__ but i think only if this transformation is gradual. like it's the mechanic of sustainable neurodivergent living...

Maybe most people can tolerate a fireworks-based life, but i'm definitely more of a "watch the sun and moon paint the sky slowly", type of gal :)

i think this is also deeply important for a neurodivergent person, not just myself.

like it needs to be publically accessible information!! i think when we metabolize this into a practical form, it'll be really helpful to know that "hey, you aren't broken. you just need slower, smoother gradients, where most people do fine with 16 palettes."

so practically we're studying this irl:
- slowly fading out music instead of abrupt pause/play (huge positive effects on sensory experience)
- slowly transitioning from work to play to rest, rather than "IN 5M GET UP AND GET READY." (also, hugelly positive effects already, even after a few weeks of observed change! usually that's a great sign in science.)
- even purely somatic things... learning to breathe slower. especially when anxeity shwallows the breath.

neuro-gradient theory? idk, something that captures the somatic importance of this mechanism!!!
