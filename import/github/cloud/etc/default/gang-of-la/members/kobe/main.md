There is a scene in Kobe's life where he is so desensitized by the trauma he's endured, and knowing his little sister needs him, he just tries to make peace with this life. 

He's got his legs suspended on a rail, way up high a collosal city tower. He slowly closes his eyes, and grabs the rails with his hands. Kobe wispers to himself in imagination, as his eyes rest: _"I won't fall... but some part of me... some part of me knows we must go on... this misery continues"_ He openes his eyes slowly, watching the city below, from a tower so high, even he feels nauseous vertigo looking below. 

(Intense) A burst of anxiety rushes through him. Kobe slips and nearly falls... his legs remain locked on the rails... if it weren't for that, he would have slipped and fell.

(Anxiety) His heart is thumping. "Why am I afraid to die? What do I have to lose?" Suddenly he realizes _"My sister... I can't leave her alone... if I were to leave her in this world... No... I... could never leave her... but why? Why were we made for so much pain?"_

When Kobe meets Joey Doom, he almost sees himself in part of him. His blonde side, looks so much like Kobe. He fundamentally changed his perspective on suffering and human life. "It is then... we must suffer to protect what is precious."

"I am a machine... a machine made for war... to feel... to endure... to strengthen."
