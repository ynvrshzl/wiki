# What is Literature?

There are two modals of literature: author and reader. The reader experiences literature as spoken language, flowing forth. But the author experiences the construction process as laying brick by brick. Like the difference between walking through a minecraft world, and actually building it by hand.

**Radical:** Literature, itself, in experienced form, is **an interpolation function.** Weaving points of ideas, in-between passages. Like a mathematical function would smoothen the distance between two points, as smooth bezier curves, rather than stepped points.

Practically speaking, as authors, we naturally think in terms of points points that are important to us _(climax, memorable scene, epic fight, character struggle, etc.)_ But the completed, experienced form, is actually a thread in-between points. This must be the art of narrative... being able to weave beautiful fabric without losing the original model.

Meaning, not every word has to be obsessively packed with meaning; you can, and absolutely should, for the sake of your sanity, enjoy your writing process. Express ridiculous scenes, ideas, and completely off-topic conversations. It's the [minutiae](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/minutiae) in stories, that make it feel real. (Unless you're writing stories that shouldn't feel real lol.)

# The paradox of literature

This is a fundamental paradox, that we're observing whilst constructing our stories.

## 0.0 - Source is Imagination

Begin at the source of stories: human imagination. If the human imagination is as boundless as the universe, then it explains why it can feel so empty. You could imagine basically anything, even things not physically possible. Like a flying, farting pink wombat (lmao).

## 1.0 - The author's role is to guide imagination
If so much possibility exists (practically infinity) within both the cosmic universe, and the inner human youniverse; written literature must understand this is the fundamental model. **Guiding the infinity canvas that is the human imagination.**

## 3.0 - Paradox

The paradox arises when the author actually wants to guide the reader... the mind goes blank. It's like, wait what happened to all those vivid sensations? Those scenes? A bit of a mind-fuck paradox (lol). When the author is working from the perspective of the reader, it would be akin to trying to map a terrain, whilst navigating it, using the map you're navigating... while navigating. It's a recursive problem, it feeds into itself, and perpetual motion is not a sustainable source in the universe. Equal exchange (symbiosis) is the only sustainable relationship in nature. [^3](#footnotes)

## 4.0 - Show vs Tell
Over explaining things, actually makes them more complicated. There is an equillibrium, a fine balance, between ambiguity (poorly described) and verbosity (overcomplicating simple things).

# (E) Expressiveness
If we game-ified the author experience, expressiveness would be a core attribute. 

For example, compare these two tiny passages. _(a) "Rain fell in the forest."_ Versus _(b) "The forest drank the rain—cool needles of water tapping leaves, wet soil breathing that deep earthy smell after a storm."_

The second one isn’t longer by much. But suddenly the brain is doing work: sound, smell, temperature, texture. The reader’s nervous system is participating.
