# Abstract 
recently we've experienced exhaustion starvation and biological and physical suffering. but it felt a lot like it was spiritual, almost structural. I think this world represents what it feels like to have an inner universe, and the entirety of your inner universe feels the weight of a rotting world. almost like a rainforest that is giving its last breath. 

this is not the world of death, probably it is the world of spiritual death and rebirth. 

# Core

we imagine almost like the world taking a deep inhale, almost like a human being taking a deep inhale, and then everything exhaling, almost like steam being released from a pressure valve, a giant clockwork titan.

perhaps this world is about the seasons of a human being, cycles, trials. in nature, even life itself is not just a linear thing. like we human beings think of ourselves as just one point, we live, and one point, we die. and everything in between is you know whatever you've been given that you call life. but this is simply not true from what we've observed. like we have no idea how time itself works. it's not linear the way we think of it. it's cyclical. just look at a clock. there is a reason we measure the time of the day as a circle, because it's a cycle!

and so everything in the human being must also be a cycle perhaps. maybe even our life itself is a cycle... we have moments where we are deeply grateful for life, and we have a cold long winters. and then sometimes we have beautiful bursts of spring and life, like life springing after being trapped for so long.
