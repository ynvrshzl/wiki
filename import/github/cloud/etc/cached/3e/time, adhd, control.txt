I was realizing, adhd, it's a lot like a train. like time. time is one of the things that constantly stresses me out. and for us neuro divergent folks, transitions are the hardest. transitioning from sleep to wakefulness, or transitioning from wakefulness to sleep. this is probably explains why it's so hard for me to shower lol. but here's the thing, time is like a train, and you don't actually control the train. but you do control the track the direction the train takes but you have no control over the train itself. this explains why living with ADHD is so difficult especially if you haven't been given support, it can honestly feel like... some part of you is incapable of expressing how torturous it feels to just wish you could do everything right until just wish you were perfect and to wish you could be on time and focus, and it turns into self-hate and self-harm even, because you were never given the support you need. but now you understand you are not the train but you are the conductor of the tracks. you have both a responsibility and a power. but we're never in control of the train, so that can never be the source of the problems we face as neurodivergent people. things outside of our control cannot control our internal world because the only thing that we can control are the decisions we make and the direction that we take
