The little child asks "Mother, why didn't the angels save us when we were human?"
Serena's tears flow down her face... but she is unable to feel her sadness... it is too deep.

Serena, caresses the child: "I know my child... [her anger unravels in her expression] It seems cruel... [teeth chatter] and unfair... [calmness] but... if the angels removed all of the pain when we were human... we would never have existed..."

The child's eyes open wide... both with wonder and confusion...

"The angels do fight for us... they protect us from harm everyday... but we cannot see them."

"Oh, so they're fairy tales?"

Premise: serena explains mythically to the child, that angels are like the cells that protect us to keep us alive. but we are also the angels, we aren't supposed to wait for seraphs... we are supposed to be the seraphs, protecting life in the smallest, seemingly most invisible of wayrs.
