Portal Theory. 

In our Systems/Chaos reduction class, we emphasize the important of human-computer interaction to solve real-world problems.

One common problem we personally explore is described as "Portal theory."

how do you work on different parts of a complex system, while being able to jump bakc into the same headspace? And fundamentally, why does that matter? 

Becuase being able to jump back into the space you left, is like time-travel, and that is the only thing that makes a complex system sustainable: when you can travel across time and work on different parts, without losing (something?).

So in praactical terms, this is how we keep working on things in our life, without abaondining them when one part gets too complex! Instead of hyperfixating on the details, we jump between our little portals, and explore the terrain. It's like calibration or something... like an explorer mapping different parts of the terrain, and coming back to the difficult parts, with new knowledge, and in some sense, as a new self, when knowledge becomes integrated into the being through action.
