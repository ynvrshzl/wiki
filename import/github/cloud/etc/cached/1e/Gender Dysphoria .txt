Dysphoria feels like there is a beautiful person watching over you. Judging the way your body looks in the mirror. Telling you how other people think you're ugly, or different than who you see on the inside.

I think the more you try to become beautiful, the more persistent and perpetual this inner narrative shadow self becomes.

This is why anxiety is a loop, and it is also personified as a beautiful person. In my case, Dysphoria is a beautiful girl, and she always tells me how I... (negative self perception)...

It really does feel like having someone beautiful, look at you in public, up and down, and judge you with their eyes. It's worse when they don't stop looking at you, to the point where you just want to scream "leave me the fuck alone!"

That is exactly what dysphoria feels like, and ultimately, it is a shattered ego.

The cure is to repair the broken parts of the self.
