t = "mediums for thought/expression"

I remember Jordan Peterson citing a quote "why think so our thoughts die instead of us." Now that sounds dramatic, but it's true from what I've observed. what if, the medium for thought is writing, becuase we human beings think primarily in language, and conversation. the other half of our thinking is imagination, which is also, translateable to language. so maybe, language is the fundamental tool we have.

but language can't express everything, it would require an enourmous amount of text to perfectly recreate and describe even a simple scene from a movie.

that's where mediums come in... abstract forms...

* like an artist learns to draw, not by forcing their  hand in perfect arcs, but by sketching.
* or how a singer writes lyrics down, and most of them are never recorded in final songs
* maybe a person who learns to think properly, spends their time writing and studying.

"practice and play"
these activities may seem wasteful, but they are infact, play, rehearsal, and ultimately, the only way we human beings exercise and learn creative abilities. in essence it is practice/play.

ofcourse, in order for any of this to be useful, by definition, we have to actually use what we practice!
