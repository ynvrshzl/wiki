Here we explore 2 key ideas: the camera as a metaphorical object, and the camera in plain-text.

Primarily, why does the Camera matter to authors? Camera is attention. It's like forced perspective. In real life, we as human beings have eyes, in creative spaces, we have synthetic vision; which is the camera. The eyes are what cast attention to things. What you see is.... [?]

The "Camera" in writing isn’t description; it’s attention. Whatever you choose to describe, becomes the interpreted spotlight for the reader.
