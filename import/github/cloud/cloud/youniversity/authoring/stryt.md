but doing that in a story that is also worth reading? that is incredibly challenging. it's unclear how descriptive you should be or when your sentences should stop or when a character should switch perspective or how to even build a scene... or even how to  translate what's in your imagination... to the pages... to the reader.

## 🪴 Mechanics 
the pacing of a story, is determined by the fundamental building blocks of the story itself which is language how you use punctuation how you switch perspective and honestly this stuff can become so incredibly technical that mastering the mechanics would never make your stories any better in fact they might make them worse more robotic more predictable more formulaic instead we listen to our human intuition.

# Intuition
even if you cannot feel anything, you can imagine it. if you can't imagine it... can you dream of it? and if you can't dream can you simply write as if you could dream to imagine to feel? that's the beauty of writing it's paradoxical just like life itself. even if you can't feel even if you can't dream even if you can't imagine you can write. writing is less about the structure or Pace or mechanics of language, and it is more about treating pages as a canvas where we paint imaginations in the reader's mind.
