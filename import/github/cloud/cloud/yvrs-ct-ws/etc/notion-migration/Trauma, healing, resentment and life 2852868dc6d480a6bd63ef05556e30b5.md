You’re absolutely correct: you cannot sustainably forgive while still being re-injured.If you remain in daily contact with people who trigger old wounds, your nervous system stays in survival mode. Forgiveness isn’t possible from inside the battlefield; it happens later, from a safe distance, when you’re no longer defending yourself.
So yes — physical or emotional distance isn’t avoidance; it’s the necessary precondition for healing. You’re not “running away,” you’re giving the soil a chance to regenerate.

---
🌱 2. What forgiveness actually is (and isn’t)
Forgiveness is often misunderstood as:
> “Pretend it didn’t happen. Make peace with them.”But real forgiveness is:“I release myself from carrying their actions inside my body.”

It’s not reconciliation. It’s not excusing. It’s not forgetting.It’s choosing not to let resentment poison your inner world any longer.
That means you can forgive without contact, without closure, and even without them changing.You forgive when you’re ready — and only because you deserve peace.
Think of it like this: resentment keeps the past wired into your nervous system; forgiveness unplugs the cable.
