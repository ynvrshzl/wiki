**# Autobiography**

We often forget, throughout the distractions of everyday, what matters most in life.

That is the core of everything I do. What matters most in life? Well... matter. Matter is what matters. The  physical world, materials, things that you can touch, things that are real. And if what's real is what matters most, then what is the most real matter? Suffering and pain. And it's only in those moments we are shocked back to life... we remember what matters most in life.

My life mission: finding a cure to the suffering of being human. I used to believe that mental health was the biggest problem in today's world, and it is definitely up there. Right along with

It's more than mental health, or illness, it's human nature and nature itself. Those are the core fundamentals of why life can be so painful. But it is so sad to me that #mental-health has become a cliche. We don't understand what we have until we lose it. That's also a cliche, but it's arguably the most real cliche, becuase when you feel it, you can't argue or think your way out of it. It's a raw, human emotion. Regret. Guilt. Grief. Sadness.

So, if in the end this is what is most real, why should I keep living when I know i'm going to die someday anyways, and there are plenty of ways to make that happen sooner on my own? This is the most important moment of your life: realizing why you should keep living, even when you could end your life to avoid being destroyed by life in the end anyway.

This is my life story, as I tried to remember the important parts and leave out what doesn't matter to me. The purpose of this is for me to both heal on my oown, and share my story to inspire someone else.

The main reason I started this website was to share what matters most in life, and that is living itself, loving our fellow human beings, and solving the problems of nature like suffering and cruelty.

People can be terrible to each other, that's why most of my stories involve deep suffering and exploring the darkest parts of human beings, and even existence itself. I've never felt loneliner in my life, when I'm around people who don't even listen to how much pain I am in. I never want you to feel that way... there is nothing worse in this world than being trapped alone and suffering, when there's people outside who could rescuve you, but they just watch you drown and tell you to swim on your own.

But people... human beings... we are complicated, creatures. Nobody knows where we came from, or how nature works, or how the universe even happened... and that's okay. That's not what really matters.

I have a philosophy that I am still creating, to help me heal and share this wtih others, i've found a way out, I survived, I know why life can be so cruel and terrible, I understand. I hvae so much to share it's overwhelming. But what matters is this: what matters most is living life, loving our fellow human beings, living for what is good, loving is the only truth.

_"Awareness is painful, but it is also the seed of transformation." _

This is something that would save her life. My past self, she nearly gave up on her life because she felt trapped. Trapped in her own body. Trapped in a dying generation. Trapped in a world burning itself alive while everyone was in their own bubble of naivety. Trapped in a consciousness that wanted to do so much and fix these issues, but trapped in a body that couldn't even get out of bed.

But what saved my life was letting go. the world is not my home, i'm just B passenger. still, i can leave my mark in this world, i can at-least try to wake up everyday and become the person that gives me the most hope. that way i stay alive and continue with hope, for myself, and for others.

That was the most painful time of my life. It wasn't nihilism that was most painful, it was the complete opposite: an explosion of immense sensitivity and from that sensitivity, meaning to live. Like when I was a child, life was beautiful. It was also painful, but i dreamt alot. I dreamt about what I would be like when I would become an adult... and do adult things. I wondered what those adults were up to... they took themselves so seriously... they scolded me and looked down on a little human creature... why are we so cruel to each other? Why do we stop over beauty? It is becuase we are ignorant, before suffering destroys us, and then we rebuild ourselves as the conscious being we were designed to be. I believe that is the purpose of human life, to evolve our consciousness and experience the human life, as vividly as possible, in all it's brutality, cruelty, but awe and glory.
