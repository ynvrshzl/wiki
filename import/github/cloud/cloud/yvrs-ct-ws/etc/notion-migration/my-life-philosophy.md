# “soulism” my philosophy for modern human beings

Created: October 14, 2025 8:39 AM
Edited: October 14, 2025 6:13 PM

this is my philosophy, part of my life’s work to understanding us human beings, and what matters most.

it’s unclear what matters most, or if that’s even the correct perspective. in that case, we enter what i call “theory space” in which the human mind starts to be swallowed by chaos, rather than the linear problem-solving helper it evolved to be. that is infact where this philosophy started intellectually, knowing what matters and what are the correct ways to use this mysterious and overwhelming nature we all possess.

### making this philosophy useful to others than myself

but moreover, beyond logic, psychology, physics, what matters most? how do we share this knowledge as wisdom, weaponry to battle against pathology and the tragedy of life, rather than mere archaic book-shelf knowledge? that is my attempt… perhaps a symbol like yin-yang or the philosophy name itself

### a critical error that nearly costed my life

venturing too deeply into my own psychology, looking for existential answers without help from anyone else. isolating myself, and physical health deteriorating. 

### guideless

there is no guide or vision for humankind… it's because in history there have been people who believed they were leading all of humanity… too much power on one place leads to…

what if there was an explanation as to what, who and why we human beings are, and how we work? not just biologically but psychologically and in our most hidden, mysterious parts, spiritually.

my philosophy is an attempt to compress and condense everything that i’ve learned and observed of human life, into a mode of being… a cosmic guide on how we human beings function, rather than a a school of thought.

i base my knowledge on the graph of human life, meaning: what things matter most? what mattters more than other parts of human life? what are the right questions to ask? 

### The most important part.

The most important part fo soulism is paradoxically, the most important part of human life: the chain of command/graph/hierarchy. 

what does a human being, a person need in order to sustainably exist without causing harm or suffering in excess? that is the core question that drives my entire life. it is the tragedy of life. suffering on it’s own is a perceptual senstation: only you feel the pain. but tragedy is suffering felt by others, and that is far more catastrophic and has led me to question if it’s even worth the fight to keep existing.

### a weapon against the abyss

i’ve stumbled upon terryfing beliefs and dangeous philosopohies, some that if they find you are your most vulnerable, could absolutely dissasemble a person’s psyche and drive them insane (it almost happened to me)

the foundation of soulism begins with the hierarchy: health is the foundation of human experience. without health there is no gravity, no order, and human beings become vessels for excess suffering: that is not good.

### No perfect belief, but all reveal 

another core part of my ideaology is that there is no perfect religion or belief system, but there definitely is a truth to discover in life, and it is not simple a mere kaleidoscpe where you get to choose what makes sense to you: we are all nodes in a network of being, and you r very existence has a causal and logical effect to all of reality, in ways that are visible and incomprehensible.

suicide is another deep pain, as i have struggled with OCD and thoughts of self-death since i developed consciousness.
