here is a summary of the story. this also helps me to have a big picture view of what's going on. these stories get extremely complex and difficult to organize and navigate... so this helps both the reader, and the author.

the gang of la is a story about resentment and vengeance... to seek justice where too many are afraid of acting.

it is the same motivation that drives the rebellion, which is the arch nemesis of the gang.

they fight and k* each other because they believe in the same thing, but they live it completely differently.

the rebellion believes in the right to rebel, not thruogh external fight, but fighting inside. the animal within. resisting, taming, rebelling. they have powerful people on their side, because they believe in something that is deeply human: justice and answering suffering.

members of the gang of la regularly destroy properties and steal from the rich to feed the poor. like if robin hood never wore a hood. they aren't afraid to show who they really are... like another similar group, which remains a complete mystery... the school of gothika.

it is said that this group has beliefs so powerful they could persuade anyone into believing what they do... the suffering of being alive... how it would all be better if it had never existed.

originally the gang of la was steering into this pit of despair... the same that drove the school to commit mass s_icide. they were more than a cult, they were a cry of humanity.

the gang and the rebellion eventually try to find equal ground so they can stop the school from truly extinguishing what remained of humankind... but the biggest curse of the gang, is that they never protected their internal selves. and when the time came, the school, one by one, persuaded each of them to destroy all of life.

now, it was the rebellion, what remained of them, their teachers, and their spirit guides, to save these troubled souls.
