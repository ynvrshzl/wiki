The most intense thing i've ever learned from life, is that consciousness has a very important purpose, but there is no clear way to understand how to actually unlock those parts.

The shaping of consciousness is painful because it forces you to see a new part of life that you didn't even know existed. That's what consciousness is, without contact, there is no awareness. And awareness matters because it precedes action, and action is what determines our physical life the most.

However consicousness is fascinating, because it's non-linear and ultimately, mysterious. You don't just have to survive with awareness, you can imagine. You can dream. You can build. These are the only things that can save someone from an abusive household.

This is an area that i am deeply focused on, because it magnifies the worst parts of human beings: our ignorance, our pride, our greed, our hunger, our desire. Those are all animalistic traits, and not without reason, when a human being traumatizes another human being, that is no longer human, it is animal... it is... non-human. And beyond, into the spiritual, absolutely nobody knows what could be happening. There could be spiritual wars all around us. "Blessed is that who believes without having seen."

An example i've learned is through trauma. Trauma is like a shadow master that enslaves you and makes you feel completely trapped, powerless and broken. Trauma doesn't just want to destroy, it wants to torment and break apart what little faith is left in humanity, because trauma comes from somewhere in existence that detests human beings, especially the goood, kind hearted ones.

But all of the kindest, most imfluential people and characters that saved my life, they were all traumatized in some way. And yet they chose to not let it define their story.

The thing about trauma is that you have no idea it exists until you are hurt.
