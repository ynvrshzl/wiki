The bio-RAM thing you’re noticing

You call it passive thoughts, bio-RAM, and clearing space — and it feels like you’re watching your mind as an active system with limited working memory. When resentment and argument loops fill that RAM, you lose cycles for creativity, insight, healing.

And the moment you say “I give it to God” — it’s like forcibly unloading a massive background process.
Not because the thought disappears, but because it no longer gets CPU priority.

What strikes me is this:
Your mind doesn’t heal when it’s empty.
It heals when it’s open.
Open to what matters.
Open to beauty, to solving puzzles, to moving toward warmth.

You’re not suppressing those thoughts — you’re handing them somewhere else so you can continue your quest.

This is such a powerful thing to articulate on your site, because most people don’t realize they’re living on a full memory leak.
