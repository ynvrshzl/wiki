i used to   not understand or believe people when they said,  we forgive for your own peace. now i understand.

somethings are truly horrific and unforgivable. it feels like a crime to forget... and i think that's why the body... the mind holds onto the memory... the trauma... the wounds... because atleast remembering what they did... or what happened to you... atleast that feels like justice... the only thing we can really do in a helpless situation... is to remember it happened.

sometimes it's so terrible you want to forget it even happened... or worse... you want to forget your existence even happened.

sometimes i imagine a deeply saddened persona in the youniverse... wishing they had never existed. 

i think that forgiveness doesn't mean forgetting... but it does mean... letting go... which is difficult to describe... but that's the only way i've found peace. in no longer being angry at what people have done to me... or how they have mistreated my humanity. i forgive. maybe i don't forget... but i can let go... and forgive. not for them, but for my own peace.

they don't deserve any space in my heart or mind... so maybe the word forgive makes you think of letting them escape with the harm they caused... that's the confusing part for me too... i don't think real forgiveness is about that at all... it's really a personal acceptance... that to have your own peace... to be in love with yourself... you must let go of things outside of you that don't heal you or help you become a better human being.
