This is an analysis of stories. This is absolutely not required to know in order to create great works. Infact, i'm not surpsised if most authors dont' know any of this; becuase you don't need to!

Stories are the most complex art form there is. I think... from what we know... It's more complicated than 3D rigging animation. It's more complex than software engineering.... It's even more complex than architecture... i think becuase stories... are deceptively format-dependent... and so you look at a book and you're like "eh, just words on pages, what's with that?" but the way my mind sees it is; - freeflow models - infinite possibility (text is essentially human imagination in written form. that's how we're even able to communicate right now!) - character, world, political, meta-relationships can be as complex as human relationships. and you have to somehow represent that with text. - constraints are not standard. stories are essentially, as far as we know... freeflowing like abstract art... with very human-specfiic constraints which are specific to each author. each author writes differently and you can feel that. there's no stylistic rule. no one tells you how you can or can't use the language. you could even go shakespeare on em' and build your own words or language lol. tolkien and some others have done exactly this. incredible, incredible brilliant human minds. stories have NO real language architecture. the lines between genres are, almost non-existent becuase material can get so complex and exploratory... there's always an element of something else. no realy purity of concepts... in the way that other complex fields specify like -> math, science, chemistry, programming...
