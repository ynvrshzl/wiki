let me share a moment that happened earlier as I was making breakfast for myself...

so to summarize i learned many things:
- the human body has many ways to protect itself against danger, pain and suffering.
- i am learning to be independent not only with following my own rythyms, work and beliefs,  but also learning that i am capable of helping myself when accidents happen.
- forgive, but never forget.
- wisdom exists everywhere, are you sensitive enough to hear it?

so essentially as i was preparing my  coffee, i wanted to boil some oatmilk in a pretty big mug, directly ontop of a stove. i know this is dangerous but i wanted to do it. And essentially, what happened was, from one moment to another, the milk started bubbling way faster than I had anticipated, and I had no choice but to pick up the mug with my bare hands and try to minimize the boiling liquid that would have clogged the oven/stovetop or most of all, could have seriously hurt me. But i managed the accident just fine. Cleaning the oven/stovetop as best I could, and being motherly and kind to myself, my inner voice, dialogue. 

i could say that i was motivated to grab the steaming mug because I wanted to avoid the mess my mother would have to clean up.. but then I said. "Wait a minute, I am an independent woman. I can clean this myself."

In that moment I realized I am a Goddess and a mom :) My inner dialogue was exactly what my biological mother sounded like as a child. She would have probably even physically abused me in moments of high stress. As I am walking this path I am learning to forgive, for my own peace, even if I... we... will never forget. So what I did in my inner world was be the opposite of the monsters that raised me. "It was an accident beautiful. I'm capable of cleaning up my mess... you're such a messy girl sometimes!! Sweet precious girl."

That calmed me down and I realized: "This mess isn't what matters, it's the lesson i'm extracting from even the most mundane of events in life. Because wisdom knows it exists everywhere... only few eyes can see it."

The crazy thing is, in the moment, I was filled with adrenaline, I couldn't even think, it was just pure instinct. And I even burned myself by grabbing that boiling mug directly from the stove. And it's crazy, because it showed me directly what adrenaline in the human body is capable of. It completely protects you from pain, at least in the moment, but then afterwards, I felt the sting all at once.

and like a true mother would... I poured honey on my burned finger, wrapped it in a soft paper now it's healing. <3 The pain was there, and I still feel it... and in the moment when I saw the burn I was like "Wait... I just burned myself?" I literally didn't feel anything. Wow. The human body is actually so beautiful i am not grateful enough for all the work my body does just to keep me here, alive. <3 Thank you body!
