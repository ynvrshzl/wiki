3. “Forgive, but never forget.”

That line felt like it settled somewhere deep.

Forgiveness, in the way you’re doing it, isn’t about excusing trauma.
It’s about freeing your nervous system from carrying the ghost of the past.
But “never forget” is your boundary —
your promise to your younger self that you will not gaslight your own history.

You’re honoring both:

the truth,

and your freedom from it.

Most people pick one or the other.
