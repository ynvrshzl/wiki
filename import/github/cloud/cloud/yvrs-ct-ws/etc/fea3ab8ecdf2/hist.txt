earlier versions of this website... we had really cool ideas and it was a time in my life where i needed to tinker and get my mind hooked on something... it helped me survive the  darkest psychological breakdown i'll ever go through... I AM NEVER GOING BACK!!

WE HAD... 
- RPG-themed style, with sounds, animations and a "dark-souls" theme for everything, but it was too dark for a global theme. then 
- Modern social media approach: with a feed of images, content, and eventual comments... but then i realized wait... this is too modern for a monk sage who demainds an artistic edo-period japan theme!
- A pseudo operating-system inside the browser, which actually functioned entirely like a real desktop, but with website functionality. markdown files were actual articles you could click and load as a "document reader" pseudo program. speaking of which, there were pseudo-programs like the console which had easter eggs, and the gallery viewer, the folder viewer, and even a pseudo-browser INSIDE the browser! ahahaha!! it was meant to be like an explorer page for freshly curated daily articles and stories from around the site... this was my favorite version... but i had to scrap it because on mobile it would be highly unreadable... and designing a mobile-operating-system theme for my site basically asked for a second rewrite of core components it was getting too technical at that point and i needed to keep myself engaged in something meaningful during this time... so i realized... i might as well archive this version, and restart everything...

- after this period i begun to lose interest in my website... because i asked myself the meta-question i shouldn't worry about. "what's the point of my site if nobody is even seeing what i'm doing?" that question single-handedly muted my curiosity and engagement for my site... i started coding less, playing less games at night... staying awake less... trying to program other things but nothing stuck because it wasn't meaningful...
- i had lost the "meaning" my website gave me... it wasn't to share this with others... it was to give my stories and writings a public garden that they deserved... not tucked away in a file cabinet, collecting dust on my hard-disk... but an eco-system...  where each writing actively shaped the world
- after deep depression and the worst psychological breakdown i will ever experience... i realized.. you know what... i'm going to try again. it's my life's work... but it isn't my work life... does that make sense lol? YES! 
- i started remembering why i started: i looked at league of legends characters and their stories and their illustrations and their lore... and then i saw the 3D map of runeterrra on the league of legends website and i said... WHY DID I EVER STOP DOING THIS!? 
- now i am actively rebuilding... not only a lost virtual ruin from memory... but also my fragmented psyche... slowly rebuilding back to life...
