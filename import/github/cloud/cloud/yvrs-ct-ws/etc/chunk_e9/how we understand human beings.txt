just like dr.veyra, we best understand human beings, through anthropromorphic entities.

for example; the character "sonny" from i, robot, is one of the most important characters in our life. he feels more human than most of the people in the movie, and he's a robot, learning to feel human emotion. he explains it briefly throughout the movie as being "difficult" and being curious of various human quirks. such as when detective spooner winks at his boss, sonny immediatly captures that as a significant memory. he's simply curious of human behaviour, and so are we... and paradoxically, we feel more connected to a robot, than to most human beings. that isn't prideful or isolating; it's direct insight to the inner workings of our human mind.

and more recently we watched the first "Avatar" movie from 2009. the blue alien creatures are so tall and twice the size of human beings, and yet, their expressions and natural way of life... are so human an ancestral. we actually feel happy to be human, when we see these blue creatures exist.
