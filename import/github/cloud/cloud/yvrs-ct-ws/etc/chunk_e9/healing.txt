The most difficult part of healing, at least from what we've experienced, is the daily action, because healing cannot happen solely through rest, and it also cannot happen through forced work. So there has to be a symbiosis, a harmony. Practically speaking, the hardest part of our day is getting out of bed in the morning, and it's not because we don't have motivation, but it's because the body is so exhausted because it has gone through so much psychologically. But we still manage to get out of bed, sometimes a little later than we'd like to, but what matters most is that we don't go back to the old way of life. There's something psychologically critical about having constant, stable, consistent rhythms every day. I think it's because our biological nature requires cycles. I mean, our internal circadian rhythm has to synchronize to sunlight, and the biology is so intelligent and adaptive that even if there is no sunlight, it will adapt to any source of light. We human beings can literally not exist without rhythm...

and the rythyms are the hardest part to maintain, becuase in those moments where you ask yourself "why am i doing this?"  you absolutely need to be equipped with the right answer, becuase otherwise, that's how trauma and mental illness trap a person in a spiral for the rest of their life.

And that's one of the deepest tragedies about human life, and about modern civilization.
