-- perfectionism --
that leads me to the third thread... perfectionism trap...

but i found the answer i was looking for all along. it's actually how programs are built sustainably. this is why UI/UX designers don't just dive into design work

in short, the answer to perfectionism is the same thing that makes these programs even possible by human beings... "manageability of complexity."

i'm studying this by myself, simply through observation! here's the main building blocks my tired brain can think of right now...

- abstraction & fragementation
- modularity
- composability
- hierarchy
- readability
- compression & constraint


let's explore some of these without getting into too much detail.
(1) abstraction & fragmentation describe how a complex thing is reduced into smaller fragments. these fragments need to be reassembled into the whole thing eventually, and that's where abstraction comes in. abstraction is simply reducing the layers of complexity, ideally, so we only have exactly what we need and nothing else. this "exactly what we need" is actually non-linear, it changes across your current context. and that's the second idea...

(2) modularity is all about how you can re-use something. but it also is like a gravity source... it's a context of meaning... depending on where you are in the complex space... the modules reveal how things are clustering near sources of patterns and truths. 

(3) composability... describes how the one part can click together in multiple ways, without breaking shape. this reduces fragmentation and creates order out of chaos => by reducing loose parts and creating linear paths again... in otherwords... turning broken things into whole.

(4) a hierarchy is necessary to establish layers of importance and meaning. it is an integral process that gives order to all chaos. hierarchy is purposefully linear, to reduce complexity, becuase we human beings can only understand things as a linear path, not multiple intersections.

(5) readability is crucial and incredibly time-consuming. but if we focus on the context of meaning, as described in earlier parts, than the order emerges by itself. folders name themselves, new items self-allocate.

(6) compression & constraint. this is paradoxical, because constraints are something you begin with, at the simple rule level... and let the chaos emerge from order... but compression reduces the layers down to only what is necessary and nothing more. compression is not itself a part of reducing complexity, but rather an emergent ally, that comes from the structure itself... specifically the hierarchy.

but the strongest idea that i have found is when you combine all of this into "metamanagement"
this is how i actually discovered it myself in obsidian, and why it saved my life.

scenario. let's say that i have 10,000 markdown files. they are filled with important ideas and topics in my life. but if all of them are important... none of them are... atleast... to my limited human senses. so the idea here is to let the system evolve and emerge on it's own, with very simple rules.

- files
- folders
- queries
- properties
- links

there is too much detail to describe here, so simply put, these five building blocks help me manage the chaos in my life, and create order, where there is none! that's the power of a QUEEN. she can move ANYWHERE but has to be PROTECTED by the other pieces!
