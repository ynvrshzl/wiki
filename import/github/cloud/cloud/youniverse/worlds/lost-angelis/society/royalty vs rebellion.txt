life in Los Angeles was a paradox between two worlds. if you lived in the outskirts with the rebellion, you would most likely suffer from poor health and poverty, if you lived in the city walls with the Royal family, you were under constant surveillance. 

and yet, a child was happier living with the rebellion, then being born in the city walls. and the Royal family constantly discussed this paradox. 

"how is it possible that our children are born under perfect condition, but they are more miserable than ever?"

none of the Royal family was allowed to admit this, but the way of life inside the walls with completely unnatural to the human being. the only kinds of people who thrived in the city walls, where augmented human beings that were more machine than man. 

but that was the culture that was a society that the Royal family envisioned from the very beginning. that someday life would have no suffering and no pain and no disease. death and immortality were two complicated to change, and so the society developed around hedonism and making the very most of human life while they had it.
