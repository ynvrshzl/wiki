# Heartbreak.
"Men experience heartbreak, much deeper than women. You ask a woman what she loses, she tells you 'just a relationship.' You ask a man, he tells you 'i lost a life.'" His friend replied: "Mayer, don't be so depressed. Not all women talk like that you know? There's plenty of women... of people out there in this big, messy world." Mayer scoffs: "That's what hurts the most. Out of all these people... you finally found someone you could trust..."

When Officer Mayer was left by his partner, it was as if his world became a prison in his mind. Bound by chains that once wrapped around each other's soul, Officer Mayer could not afford... (?) He needed to train. To continue fighting. But his body developed worsened chronic illness. To the point where he couldn't walk on his own anymore. He missed work. Nobody was there to take care of him. "Why are some people so cruel?"

Officer Mayer was not nihilistic, but he always fought with that part of him, the human side, that wished life was less pain.

Someone eventually repaired his broken body, and essentially, Officer Mayer was more machine than man.

He lost most of his human self when she left. "Grow up, you're a man now!" No. They didnt' undertsand. Their voices echoed in the walls of his mind. It was a prison. He could no longer fight, for he no longer had the will to love. Death, seemed like a few seconds of sleep away. Peace. Safety. No more pain. But that is the thorn of human love. When two human beings fall lin love, they both have to fall.

It is a tragic symbol, really, to the human life. That when you love someone so deeply, it is at that place, they could hurt you the most. He was left broken. "That woman didn't just end their relationship, she ended a life. A future. Kids. Family. Dreams. Hope. A reason to live in this damned world." Officer Mayer's new, android side replied: "We continue to fight. It is our duty. Our mission."
