## Epilogue: Young Officer Mayer
As a young man, Officer Nihil Mayer felt lost. He felt empty in his soul. Nothing seemed to answer the questions he had... _"Why is there so much pain in this world? Why do evil people get away with harm sometimes? Why can't I do anything about it? Why? Why? Why is my body too weak to fight back? Why?!"_


## Chapter 2: "The Edge"
One Moment changes his entire life - A moment in his life where, becuase of his frail body, he almost didn't save a child from drowning.

The questions he pondered. Everyday. Slowly. Slowly it ate him alive. The more he questioned, the more hopeless he felt. He looked around, and saw injustice, cruelty and the unfairness in life. Slowly, his [chronic illness (missing file)]() worsened. His mind became intoxicated with questions about the purpose of life itself.

"If life is so painful, why should I be part of this world... a world that doesn't even help the poor... or the orphans... none of us asked to be here... why are we here?"

These thoughts were eating him alive, from the inside-out.

One day, the young Officer Mayer looked at the sky. It was beautiful. Clear. Not a cloud in sight. He pondered if there really was something... somewhere up there... who cared about us. __He was on the edge.__

Officer Nihil could barely walk on his own. Nobody could take care of him. With the last strength he had left, he visited his favorite bridge. It was silent. Serene. Only the sound of water and the cool air blowing through the trees.

_(Water splashing underneath)_ Officer Mayer's ears caught the faint sound of water splashing. Exhausted, he thought to himself: _"Where is that coming from?"_ underneath him.



From that moment onward, Mayer, finally found his purpose. It wasn't hopeful, but it gave him a reason to work hard.

"My body isn't strong enough to serve..."

# Deep Lake - "Officer Mayer's Arms Must Grow."

When Officer Mayer, being a young man, notices a child drowning in a lake, his body was already moving to rescue the drowning child. He thought to himself before plunging headfirst into the lake *"But I don't know how to swim. What if I also drown?"* These thoughts would follow Officer Nihil for the rest of his life. The human side of him, the doubt, and the machine, the autonomy.

A child orphan slipped into a deep lake of water. Officer Mayer dives in the water. Intense emotion. He almost drowns with the child, but using all the force his body could give, He swam to the surface with the orphan.
