Dr. Vira's main tension throughout her life, stems from her beliefs. She sees herself as a naturalist, believing that life only exists thorugh natural selection, and the laws of physics.


It's not until she has to see the trauma and horrifying suffering of children, that she experiences an 'ego death.' She then realizes "there is more to this life than we could ever possibly understand."

"If there is so much suffering, cruelty and injustice... and there is only natural selection... why should life continue? Merciless to the weak? Why?"

This is when she goes into ego-death. Like it's horrific but it breaks the shell... and she... eventually realizes that even in nature, animals do help each other... life is not evil, inherently. Evil itself then, must be an error... a disorder.

This is the point where she decides to completely abaondon the Sciences and find a new way of life, though her career and stability keep her from moving too far away... she decides to strike a balance.

"Science, can be used to better human life, but only when it doesn't become the placeholder for a God."
