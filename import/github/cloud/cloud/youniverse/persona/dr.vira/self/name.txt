The term "vira" in Sanskrit means "hero" or "brave person." It is associated with qualities such as bravery, strength, and devotion, particularly in the context of Bhakti Yoga, which emphasizes devotion to a personal God. The word is derived from the root "vir," which means "to overpower". In various contexts, "vira" can also refer to a heroic sentiment or a person who embodies courage and valor.
