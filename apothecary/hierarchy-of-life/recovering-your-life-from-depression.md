Recovering your life from depression

"You have to put in the work to make your life better." Jeez. Thanks _Captain obvious._ That's the most helpful advice you could have possible given me. Especially when my depression is crippling my desire to just dig my own grave & dissapear. 

As sad as that may sound, it's what my depression personified feels like at their worst.

That's just the top-level information, what **REALLY** matters is your reason for putting in work to better your life. Why do you want to live a better life? Why do you believe your life is worth saving, putting in work? Especially when depression has it's claws around you, begging you to go back to bed, when you know waking up early is the right thing for you. 

That's why, having the why, is the most important question you'll ever ask yourself.

When my agoraphobia gets really bad, I ask myself this brutal question, that reminds me _why_ I'm alive: _"Would I rather die trying what I love, or die without ever having tried?"_
