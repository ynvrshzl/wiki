# The barber pole effect

Do you sometimes feel like you're possibly going in circles? Old  habits, old ways of living, old ways of being... Does it feel like no matter what you try, you keep coming back to the same place?

In nature, this effect of feeling like you're going somewhere, but in reality, you're just looping in the same space, appears in many places in nature. Like the pole of a barber shop, or a [shepard's tone](https://wikipedia/shepard_tone)

# Finding a solution

> **Key question:**
> Where in my life is giving me the illusion that I'm moving, but I'm actually not?

Before we find solutions, we need to understand the problem itself. For example, a barber pole gives the illusion of it's stripes infinitely rolling downwards, but in reality, it's just a cylinder with clever stripes. And so I ask myself: where in my life am I having barber poles instead of the progress that gives my life it's meaning?

**Where in my life is giving me the illusion that I'm moving, but I'm actually not?**

Now that I identify the problem, I can work on finding the sources & then solve these problems.

It hurts to admit this, but the tools I used to organize my life & day, actually were causing me to burnout & feel like I was making progress towards things I value, but in fact, I was just adding noise by spending time writing & researching, rather than doing the things I wanted to.

You can't just jump staright into work however, becuase the reason why I was writing so much was becuase I need a sense of control. Fundamentally, this is becuase I am still healing my nervous system & body. Again, everything in life always traces back to our psychology, and health. 

When looking for solutions, I always go to the fundamental source: health & psychology:
- Am I taking care of myself physically?
- Am I taking care of myself emotionally?
- Am I taking care of myself socially?
- Am I taking care of myself spiritually? (that's a tough one!!)

This is exactly the point where your meaning & long-term goals intersect with daily actionable steps.

# Daily actionable steps, meaning, and more

It seems simple in theory, just follow the equation: meaning + longterm goals = daily action. But in reality, figuring out the daily action is an immense storm of noise, and the higher your goals, the more noise it'll take to cut through to get there. But that is not a burden, it is in infact the greatest source of meaning: to take the maximum amount of responsibility that you can withstand!

Human beings were not designed to live downhill, we actually derive our meaning to exist when there is something valuable worth pushing uphill for, but the real sense of meaning comes from pushing itself.
