[Cyberpunk 2077](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyberpunk_2077) has got to be one of the most important collaborative art projects in all of human history. I don't even consider it a video game anymore, and it is most certainly not simply a crime simulator like Grand theft Auto 5. 

The sheer massive depth of such a game, is __on a level of civilization simulation.__

I don't think it's a coincidence, that many large open world video games with sophisticated storytelling, lore, and World design, settle on similar patterns and processes. that's essentially what I'm most fascinated by; how these worlds are created. from conception to production. I'm basically assembling on author's framework, because this kind of information is simply not publicly accessible outside of gate kept industry pipelines and processes. I think that is incredibly unnecessary... but I do acknowledge that project of this scale always require several hundred domains of human effort.

one of the most important models I'm observing, is that in all of these open world games, especially cyberpunk 2077, the engineers of the world, the world builders, the lore authors, they all fundamentally base the work on real life references. this is key, because without references, the authoring process becomes almost impossible.

I truly want to emphasize, how important references are, not just references to other work, like a video game referencing a movie, or a book referencing a classical symphony. I'm talking about reference relative to the real natural world. that seems to be where the most source of inspiration, is deeply embedded. I think even by definition, the etymology of the word inspiration, comes from Greek "inspiritus" which roughly translates "to breathe life into."

that is beautiful. the Greeks understood that inspiration was something akin to, what channels life into that which is otherwise a mechanical structure. 

e.g., sheet music is just mechanical structure, information, code, until it becomes embodied, encoded in human performance. that's like channeling inspiration. and the very act of creating music, requires inspiration. if music is approached as an entirely mechanical process, it loses a reason. it becomes analytical. and there is a noticeable difference, from the analysis of music, as compared to music. 

and there are countless other examples. but I truly believe that inspiration/reference is the core element to Great stories and world building. otherwise, a world remains an infinitely composable mechanical structure. and this is not only lifeless, it's actually impossible for human beings to work with. just like explored in the literature of the library of babel, by Jorge luis, we human beings derive meaning when there is reason for search, not the infinite access of excess. 

but I want to tie this back into World building rather than drifting off into philosophy. because, I'm noticing just how overwhelming World building truly is. the granular levels of detail are essentially endless. a story is never truly complete, and I believe the job of the author, is not to complete the world, but to capture what makes the story alive. the inspiration that fueled the original vision for the story, I believe that's really the job of the author, it's sort of a transmutation / transformation process, of inspiration/reference, into story. 

that's beautiful philosophically, but realistically, we still have to deal with a big problem; how we deal with the open-ended nature of fantasy worlds, and how we complete our stories. 

I think that's the most basic formulation of what I'm emphasizing here. and for the author's framework, it's perhaps the core tension which drives the creative process.
