Hypothesis: "Enviornments shape cognition."

I realized this while trying to replace my "cluttered graphical applications" with "clean command-line utilities." That is when i discovered a trade-off;
- Microtools, self-authorable, minimal to no bloat
- Macrotools, industry-authored, battle-tested + bloat.

This resulted in cognitive friction;

Having to constantly micro-interrupt one's workflow, "how do i split a panel? why isn't the file explorer refreshing?" May sound like trivial complaints. But overtime, this drains an enormous amount of limited human cognitive working capabilities. 

So then, I learn to appreciate the "messy graphics" of these systems... they package so much more than we will ever appreciate.
