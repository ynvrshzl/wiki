The Timeline is a fundamental model, perhaps, the most fundamental idea in stories.

Once stories have an axis of time, events are no longer free-agents scattered in space, now the fundamental constraint of time emerges order from chaos. 

Because time is passing in linear-space, it gives us a way to model our stories, without getting overwhelmed by the relationships of everything.

Then, if time is a one-dimensional axis, with only the ability to travel forwards, backwards or momentarily, the second dimension is introduced by the written story itself. 

If our ideas as authors, can be mapped onto time (e.g. events, birth, death, milestones etc.) Then, the narrative unfolds the space between these points.
